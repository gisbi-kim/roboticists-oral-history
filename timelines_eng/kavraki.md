# Lydia Kavraki — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kavraki.md`)

```
1967            — Born in Heraklion, Crete, Greece; grew up in Greece
Undergrad years — Entered the Computer Science department at the University of Crete (the department's second year of existence; second graduating class)
                  — Chose the new field at the urging of her father (an electrical engineer)
After undergrad — Began PhD studies in Computer Science at Stanford University (cohort of 16)
Stanford years  — Took Donald Knuth's course; joined the Robotics Lab (advised by Jean-Claude Latombe)
                  — Environment that included Latombe, Khatib, Yoav Shoham, Leo Guibas, and Rajeev Motwani
PhD research    — Path planning problems (configuration space, building on Lozano-Perez and Canny theory)
                  — Collaborated with Danny Halperin from Tel Aviv
                  — Visited Mark Overmars and Peter Svestka's group at Utrecht University; collaborated on similar sampling techniques
1994            — Initial idea for the Probabilistic Roadmap Planner formed
1996            — Published the major Probabilistic Roadmap Planner paper (establishing sampling-based motion planning)
After PhD       — Spent 6–8 months as a research associate at Stanford; began applying robotics techniques to drug design and protein engineering
                  — Collaborated with Paul Finn (Pfizer) and Michael Levitt
2000            — Received the ACM Grace Hopper Award (for developing PRM)
2001            — Received the IEEE RAS Early Academic Career Award
?               — Appointed Assistant Professor at Rice University (Houston, Texas, near the medical cluster)
                  — George Phillips and others played key roles in the recruitment
Rice years      — Ran the Computational Robotics and Biomedicine lab
                  — Joint appointments in Bioengineering, ECE, and Mechanical Engineering
                  — Collaborated with Wah Chiu (cryo-EM), George Bennett, Cecilia Clementi, and others
                  — Active in the Keck Center for Quantitative Biosciences
2004            — Contributed to the founding of RSS (Robotics Science and Systems), led by Sebastian Thrun
Around 2006–2007 — Began OMPL (Open Motion Planning Library) development with student Ioan Sucan at Willow Garage (Mark Moll later joined)
NASA collaboration — OMPL applied in part to the Robonaut project (collaboration with Julia Badger and Rob Ambrose)
2014            — Chair of RSS (had been offered the chair role 10 years earlier but declined due to giving birth to twins; accepted upon a renewed invitation, held at Berkeley with Pieter Abbeel)
2015            — Elected to IEEE RAS ADCOM
2020            — Received the IEEE RAS Pioneer Award
2020            — Received the ACM-AAAI Allan Newell Award
At time of interview — Noah Harding Professor of Computer Science at Rice University
```
