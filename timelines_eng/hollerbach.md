# John Hollerbach — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/hollerbach.md`)

```
Birth                — Born in Marktheidenfeld, Germany (near Frankfurt), to Hungarian-German refugee parents
Childhood            — Lived 5 years in the attic of a German priest's residence (poverty)
Emigration           — Emigrated to the U.S. as Hungarian refugees → settled in Detroit
1968                 — B.S. in Chemistry, University of Michigan
1969                 — M.S. in Mathematics, University of Michigan — no computer science degree existed at the time
1969–1971            — Worked as a chemist at IBM; took AI/CS courses at Syracuse University
Entering MIT         — Connected with Pat Winston, joined the AI Lab
1972~1975            — MIT S.M. (computer vision, Greek vase modeling) — advised by Pat Winston
Around 1974          — Brought Victor Scheinman to the AI Lab, built Vicarm (later the prototype of the PUMA robot); handwriting experiments with Vicarm
1975~1978            — MIT Ph.D. (Electrical and Computer Engineering) — David Marr's first PhD student; human handwriting model and robot implementation
1978                 — AI Lab Research Scientist (affiliated with the Psychology Department)
1982~1985            — Assistant Professor, MIT Department of Psychology
Around 1981–1982     — Started the "Year of the Robot" program — supported by the System Development Foundation and ONR
1983                 — Founded the International Journal of Robotics Research (IJRR) (Lou Paul, Mike Brady invited as editors)
1983                 — First ISRR (International Symposium of Robotics Research) — Hollerbach designed it in the Gordon Conference format
1984                 — NSF Presidential Young Investigator Award; Session Chair at ICRA Atlanta
1984                 — IEEE Journal of Robotics and Automation founded
1985                 — Hollerbach et al. published "Robot Motion: Planning and Control"; Associate Professor in MIT's Brain and Cognitive Sciences (1985~1989); built Utah/MIT Hand (collaboration with Stephen Jacobsen)
1988                 — Canadian Institute for Advanced Research Fellow
1989                 — McGill University NSERC/CIAR Industrial Chair in Robotics; ICRA Scottsdale Program Chair; hired Martin Buehler, joint lab with Ian Hunter
1994                 — Joined University of Utah as Professor of Computing School and Mechanical Engineering
1996                 — IEEE Fellow
2000                 — Became IJRR Editor-in-Chief (later expanded to 14 issues/year)
2012                 — IEEE RAS Distinguished Service Award
May 16, 2012         — Interviewed by Peter Asaro and Selma Sabanovic (Utah)
At time of interview — Built Utah robotics research group (about 10 people); ongoing TreadPort rehabilitation robot research for partial spinal cord injury patients; ISRR board member, founded CEB
```
