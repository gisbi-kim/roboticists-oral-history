# Michael Sims — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/sims.md`)

```
June 3, 1949          — Born in Memphis, Tennessee, USA
Through age 11        — Grew up in Arkansas
Around age 11         — Moved to New Jersey
Undergrad             — Rutgers University undergraduate (Physics major)
Grad school           — Switched to Mathematics/Computer Science in Rutgers grad school under Saul Amarel
PhD period            — Researched "IL", a mathematical discovery system based on the surreal numbers domain (Tom Mitchell on committee)
Around 1983~1984      — NASA Langley Faculty Fellow; developed a safety-stop system for a space station robot arm (first robotics experience)
1987                  — Right after PhD coursework, became one of the first (or second) hires of the NASA Ames AI group founded by Peter Friedland
1987~2013             — Principal Research Scientist at NASA Ames Research Center
1990                  — Earned Rutgers Ph.D. (Mathematics/CS/AI/Machine Learning)
Early NASA Ames       — Shifted research direction from space station PUMA arm work to planetary surface (field) robotics
Around 1990           — Began collaboration with Carnegie Mellon Field Robotics Center on Dante, Ambler, etc.
Early 1990s           — Adopted the Marsokhod rover from Russia's VNIITRANSMASH; collaboration with IKI (Slavo Linkin)
1990s                 — Field tests in Kamchatka with Carl Ruoff and others
Late 1990s            — TROV (Telerobotic Undersea Vehicle) tests at the Antarctica Ross Ice Cap; demonstrated at Smithsonian Air & Space Museum
1997~2015             — Participated in the JPL Pathfinder and MER missions
Around 1998           — Joined the Deep Space 1 autonomous spacecraft AI control team
Around 2000           — Steve Squyres invited him to join the MER co-investigation; selected after 2-3 Discovery proposals
2003                  — MER (Spirit, Opportunity) launched
2004                  — MER landed on Mars; operated for 7+ years
After around 2003     — Ported CMU algorithms via Mark Maimone (CMU alum)
NASA Ames period      — As leader of the Ames robotics group, advanced the vision of "one person controlling 100 robots"
NASA Ames period      — Proposed Discovery-class missions Victoria (lunar south pole permanently shadowed crater) and Long Day's Drive (Mars north pole)
Late NASA Ames        — Founded the Connect Lab; researched the "Human 2.0" (collective human agency) concept
Late NASA Ames        — Built a high-bandwidth collaboration system with UCSD CalIT2 using a hyperwall (8x6 displays)
At interview          — Preparing to launch Phone-sat (Android phone-based satellite)
June 9, 2011          — ETHW oral history interview by Selma Šabanović and Peter Asaro in Mountain View, CA
2013                  — Retired from NASA Ames; joined Moon Express (VP for Software, Chief Robotics Officer)
May 2015              — Departed Moon Express
2015                  — Joined Mars Institute as Senior Research Scientist
```
