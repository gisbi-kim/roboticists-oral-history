# Hendrik Van Brussel — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/van_brussel.md`)

```
1944. 10. 24.      — Born in Ieper, Belgium (a major WWI battlefield)
Undergrad years    — Mechanical engineering at Hoger Technisch Instituut in Ostend
1965               — Earned engineering (mechanical) degree at Hoger Technisch Instituut
1968               — Earned MS in electrical engineering at Katholieke Universiteit Leuven
PhD program        — Dynamic analysis of metal cutting processes (applying systems theory)
1971               — Earned PhD in mechanical engineering at KU Leuven
1971~1973          — Set up a metal industry development center in Bandung, Indonesia; Associate Professor at Institut Teknologi Bandung
Around 1973 (return) — KU Leuven Assistant Professor, began teaching dynamic measurement systems; chose force-controlled robotics as research topic
1976               — Received SME F. W. Taylor Research Medal
1978               — Developed AACW (Active Adaptive Compliant Wrist) (world's first active compliance)
1979               — Presented AACW at ISIR conference in Washington (sat next to Dan Whitney)
1980               — Acquired Cincinnati Milacron robot (used until 1998)
1980               — Appointed Full Professor of Mechatronics and Automation at KU Leuven; became head of PMA division
1980~1993          — Head of Division PMA (Production Engineering, Machine design and Automation)
1986               — SACODY in Esprit program, first major European project (with KUKA and AEG, spot welding robots)
1986               — Joris De Schutter earned PhD (his student and successor)
1990               — Started autonomous navigation mobile robot project based on natural environments with an Antwerp company
Around 1990        — Started friction modeling research (developed Leuven model)
Early 1990s        — Holonic Manufacturing Systems project (part of IMS program), developed PROSA architecture
1993~2001          — Chair of Mechanical Engineering Department
1995               — Developed mobile robot with free navigation without artificial landmarks (claimed world's first)
Later              — Developed Vesalius robot (laser laparoscopy), researched haptic and tactile feedback
2001~2002          — President of CIRP (International Academy for Production Engineering)
2001~2003          — Second tenure as head of Division PMA
2003~2010          — Department Chairman
2007~2009          — President of euspen (European Society for Precision Engineering and Nanotechnology)
2008               — Received Joseph Engelberger Award
2011. 7. 8.        — Interview with Peter Asaro conducted in Leuven
At time of interview — Pursuing medical robot spinoff, continuing holonic multi-agent research
```
