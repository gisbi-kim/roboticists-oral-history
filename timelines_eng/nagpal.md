# Radhika Nagpal — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/nagpal.md`)

```
Birth             — Born in the United States while her father was a PhD student at Georgia Tech
Childhood (until age 8) — Moved with family to Amritsar, India for schooling
High school years — Amid political unrest and curfews in Amritsar, decided to apply to U.S. universities
1987              — Received the National Talent Search Scholarship Award (India)
1989              — Entered MIT as an undergraduate (thanks to a tuition assistance program)
Freshman year     — 6.001 computer science course set her career direction
1992–1993         — Began a student internship at Bell Labs (collaborated with Rae McLellan)
1993–1994         — Extended Bell Labs internship; deferred graduate school for one year
1994              — SB and SM in electrical engineering and computer science from MIT
1995–2001         — AT&T Bell Labs Graduate Fellowship for Women
PhD               — Entered the PhD program at MIT; advised by Gerry Sussman and Hal Abelson
PhD               — Joined the Amorphous Computing project (Sussman, Knight, Abelson)
During PhD        — Gave birth to her daughter while a graduate student
2001              — PhD in electrical engineering and computer science from MIT ("Programmable Self-Assembly using Biologically-Inspired Local Interactions")
After 2001        — Return to Bell Labs fell through due to the dot-com crash; took a lecturer position at MIT
Later             — At Mark Kirschner's invitation, spent a year in a biology lab (systems biology)
Later             — Appointed assistant professor in Harvard's computer science department
Early in faculty career — First robotics experiments with Lego Mindstorms (self-assembly, inspired by termites)
2005              — Received the Microsoft New Faculty Fellowship
2007              — Received the NSF Career Award
Around 2007       — Started the TERMES climbing robot project
2010              — Received the Anita Borg Early Career Award (BECA)
Later             — Postdoctoral researcher Mike Rubenstein joined; started the Kilobot project
2012              — Received the Radcliffe Institute Fellowship
2014              — Selected for Science's Top 10 Breakthroughs
2014-12           — Selected by Nature as one of the "Top 10 scientists and engineers who mattered"
2015-01-24        — IEEE History Center oral history interview (College Station, Texas)
2015              — Received the Harvard McDonald Award for Excellence in Mentoring
2017-04           — Presented at the Vancouver TED conference
```
