# Richard Volz — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/volz.md`)

```
1937-07-10        — Born in Woodstock, Illinois (a town of 5,000 outside Chicago)
High school years  — Graduated from Woodstock High School (class of 77); operated amateur radio
Undergrad          — Entered Northwestern University, initially majoring in mathematics
                    After funding ended, transferred via the engineering co-op program and chose electrical engineering
                    Won the Eshbach Award (1960) and the IRE Outstanding Student Award (1960)
Graduate           — Pursued M.S./Ph.D. at Northwestern with a 3-year NSF Fellowship
                    Won the Cabell Fellowship (1963~64); advised by Gordon Murphy
                    Researched parameter sensitivity and optimization techniques in control systems
1964              — Joined the University of Michigan EE faculty; member of the systems engineering lab
Around 1969~1970   — Developed a computer-based control design system; extended IBM code and distributed it to Stanford,
                    Rensselaer, UK, Spain, and Australian universities (pre-MATLAB era)
1975              — Eta Kappa Nu Outstanding Teacher Award (Univ. of Michigan)
1981              — New dean Jim Duderstadt at Michigan launched the Center for Research in
                    Integrated Manufacturing; joint Air Force OSR grant secured
                    Volz served as director of its Robotics Research Lab
Around 1982~1983   — Developed Puma robot vision/insertion systems on the Intel 432 (Ada machine), in Ada
Around 1982~1983   — Attended the first IEEE robotics activity meeting (chaired by George Saridis,
                    with George Bekey, Tony Bejczy, Lou Paul, etc.); the Council was launched
1983              — Attended the first ICRA conference (Atlanta)
1984              — President Reagan announced the space station; appointed to NASA's space robotics committee
                    (chaired by Bob Cannon)
1986              — Appointed to the Aerospace Safety Advisory Panel right after the Challenger accident
                    Reviewed replacement of the Shuttle's General Purpose Computers
1988              — Became Chair of Computer Science at Texas A&M (1988~1997)
                    With Lynn Conway and Mike Walker, patented the "time clutch / position clutch" telerobotics concept
1988              — IEEE Robotics and Automation became a Society (from a Council)
                    (Tony Bejczy-led); Volz participated in editorial activities
1990              — Served as General Chair of ICRA 1990 in Cincinnati
Around 1990~1991   — Appointed to the Air Force Scientific Advisory Board
                    Concurrently served on the NASA Space Station Advisory Board, AIDA Board, and 4 panels in total
1991-05            — First flight of the Shuttle's new General Purpose Computers
1993              — Received the NASA Special Service Award
1994              — Received the U.S. Air Force "Decoration for Exceptional Civilian Service"
1994~1999         — Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Transactions on Robotics and Automation
1990s             — Robotics projects on nuclear materials handling, including the Amarillo National Research Center for Plutonium
                    NASA AirCam telerobotics simulation research via United Space Alliance
                    Research on haptic gloves and astronaut training simulations
1997              — Stepped down as Texas A&M department chair
1999~2003         — IEEE RAS Vice President for Publications
2003              — Elected IEEE RAS President-elect
2004              — After deciding to retire from A&M, formally retired in August (informally continued until his students graduated)
2004~2006         — President of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society
2006~2007         — End of presidential term (assisting after the death of predecessor Kazuo Tanie)
                    Established the Conference Editorial Board and Electronic Products & Services Board,
                    introduced strategic planning, overseas conference rotation, co-founded Transactions on Haptics, etc.
2012-05-16        — Interviewed by Selma Šabanović and Peter Asaro in St Paul, MN
```
