# Jim Bobrow — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/bobrow.md`)

```
Birth             — Born in Newton, Massachusetts, USA
Around age 1      — Moved with family to California; raised in suburban Los Angeles
Childhood         — Developed an interest in mechanical engineering by modifying the Briggs and Stratton engine on his minibike; worked in his father's garage
1977              — Earned BS in Mechanical Engineering from California State University, Long Beach
After 1977        — Worked as a satellite engineer at Hughes Aircraft and TRW
1979              — Earned MS in Mechanical Engineering from UCLA
PhD               — At UCLA, co-advised by Steve Dubowsky and Steve Gibson; researched point-to-point minimum-time motion algorithms for manipulators
1982              — Earned PhD in Mechanical Engineering from UCLA
After PhD         — Worked about 1.5 years in the CAD/CAM division at McDonnell Douglas Automation; published a paper on machine-tool path generation
1984              — Joined UC Irvine, Department of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering (the department's first robotics faculty)
Early UCI         — Adopted a pneumatic robot direction due to limited research funds; built several blue pneumatic robots
UCI era           — Extended from time-optimal control to maximum-payload optimal control; demonstrated PUMA robots lifting 3x the manufacturer-spec weight
About 1996–2001 (10–15 years ago) — After colleague Dave Reinkensmeyer joined, began rehabilitation robotics research
Rehab research    — Gait rehabilitation robots (clinical trials at UCLA), upper-limb rehabilitation robots ("slacking controller")
PhD students      — Bryan Martin, Garrett Sohl, Scott Ploen (JPL), Eric Wolbrecht (faculty at Idaho), and about 15 others
Collaborations    — Steve Dubowsky (MIT), Frank Park (Seoul National), Oussama Khatib (Stanford), Athanasios Sideris (UCI), Mike McCarthy (UCI), physician Steve Cramer, and others
2011              — At time of interview: Professor of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at UC Irvine; continuing research on rehabilitation robotics, dynamics, and optimization
```
