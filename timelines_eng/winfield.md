# Alan Winfield — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/winfield.md`)

```
1956              — Born in Burton on Trent (English Midlands), UK
Childhood          — Spent hours dismantling/assembling radios and gramophones; fascinated by microelectronics
Secondary          — Burton on Trent Grammar School (one of the last grammar schools in the UK)
Undergrad          — Entered the University of Hull, studying electronic engineering (microelectronics-oriented)
                    Specialized in communications engineering; final project on error-correcting codes
PhD years          — Awarded a PhD scholarship for top undergraduate honors; PhD at Hull on
                    digital communications, information theory, and error-correcting codes
                    Advised by Rod Goodman; has an anecdote of personally introducing himself to Claude Shannon
PhD period         — Invented and patented a computer architecture
1984              — Left academia to co-found MetaForth Computer Systems
                    (a native Forth instruction processor — the world's fastest single-board processor at the time)
                    Failed in commercial PCs; pivoted to safety-critical data communications (now APD Communications)
~Around 1989       — Transitioned from Managing Director to Technical Director
1992              — Returned to academia as a non-executive director of the company; joined the
                    University of West England Bristol (UWE) as Associate Dean (Research) and
                    Hewlett-Packard Professor of Electronic Engineering
1993              — Co-founded the Intelligent Autonomous Systems Lab (now the Bristol Robotics
                    Laboratory) with Chris Melhuish and Owen Holland
                    Initially started swarm experiments with self-built mobile robots in the corridors
Around 1995~1996   — Owen Holland tracked down 6 of Grey Walter's tortoise robots, borrowed one from
                    Nicholas Walter and restored it; Winfield himself led the project to build
                    replicas for the Millennium Dome exhibition
Around 1998        — First Linux-based "Linuxbots" design (with Owen Holland); published a wireless-network
                    swarm paper (early Wi-Fi era, hand-hacking Linux drivers)
1999~2000         — Collaborated with Caltech (Rod Goodman, Alcherio Martinoli) on the DARPA "dog's nose"
                    project (humanitarian land mine detection)
                    Also collaborated with BAE Systems (Bristol lab)
Early 2000s        — Began mathematical modeling of swarm behavior; published papers proposing the concept of "swarm engineering"
Around 2008 (4.5-year project) — Started "Emergence of Artificial Culture in Robot Societies"
                    E-puck-based imitation learning / memetic evolution experiments
~Around 2012       — Participated in the EU FET project Symbrion (Symbiotic Evolutionary Robot Organisms)
At time of interview — Director of the UWE Science Communication Unit, EPSRC Senior Media Fellow,
                    Honorary Visiting Professor at the University of York, etc.
2013-03-25        — Interviewed by Peter Asaro in Sheffield, UK
```
