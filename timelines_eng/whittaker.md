# Red Whittaker — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/whittaker.md`)

```
Childhood          — Grew up in Hollidaysburg, Pennsylvania (central PA, near Altoona)
                    Loved building things from tools and junkyard parts; daydreamed about space and robots
Around age 8       — Built models of robotic space creatures and spacecraft
1973              — Earned a B.S. in Civil Engineering from Princeton University
After              — Entered CMU graduate school; advised by Paul Christiano
                    Researched dynamic analysis of plates on elastic media (integrals of complex Hankel functions)
Graduate years     — Taught engineering classes himself (formal instructor, not TA), at the peak of undergraduate enrollment
1975              — M.S. in Civil Engineering from CMU
1979              — Ph.D. in Civil Engineering from CMU
                    Hired as faculty in Carnegie Mellon's College of Engineering
                    Same year, Westinghouse's gift founded the CMU Robotics Institute
                    Received the Teare Award for Teaching Excellence
Fall 1983          — Started the Three Mile Island cleanup robot project
1984-04           — Delivered the TMI cleanup robot, completed in about 6 months
                    Ran a team of ~25 (mostly undergraduates), with 13 core and 3~4 highly committed members
                    Team member John Bares later joined RedZone Robotics, then founded Carnegie Robotics
1984              — Completed the first autonomous robotic excavator, and the first outdoor navigation machine, Terragator
                    (first applications of pavement camera navigation, mobile robot lidar scanning, 3-tier architecture, etc.)
1984-11           — First entry into the TMI containment building
1986              — Built the Nav Lab (a blue Chevy van) outdoor navigation vehicle
                    Same year, formally joined the Robotics Institute
1987              — Founded RedZone Robotics
1987~1988         — First demonstration of GPS-based outdoor navigation (held the patent)
1988~1989         — Developed an autonomous rock truck for mining
1990              — Fully integrated GPS navigation into the rock truck
                    ASV/Ambler period — Mars prototype walking robot
Later              — Dante (a climbing walking robot for crater exploration), the first spherical lidar scanner,
                    Trinocular Vision (2 sets, 6 cameras total), and other applications
                    Discovered a natural decapod (10-legged) creature during Antarctic exploration
                    Nomad — Antarctic meteorite search and Atacama Desert exploration (with Eric Rollins, Ben Shamah)
                    Drew on his own farming experience to push unmanned tractor and farm machinery automation
                    (later commercialized through John Deere, Trimble, EZ Steer, etc.)
2003-03-14        — Learned of the DARPA Grand Challenge announcement
                    Secured first funding 9 months in; mounted technology on a used farm vehicle chassis
2004-03-13        — Competed in DARPA Grand Challenge 1; eliminated at ~7 miles after stopping on a hump
                    (had recovered from a Hummer rollover during practice the prior week)
                    Decided to enter the second challenge (with two self-funded vehicles, in a hare-and-tortoise strategy)
Late 2005          — DARPA Grand Challenge 2 — the lead vehicle's fuel line bent and the engine stalled,
                    but the backup vehicle overtook it just before the finish line; both vehicles completed the course
2006-05-02        — DARPA Urban Challenge announced; decided to enter after about a month of deliberation
About 18 months later — Won the DARPA Urban Challenge (with Chris Urmson and other key team members)
Around 2010        — Competing for the Google Lunar X Prize — Falcon 9 payload, attempting the lunar-night-survival bonus
                    Allies: General Motors, Lockheed Martin, Caterpillar, Aerojet,
                    Harmonic Drive, International Rectifier, etc.
2010-11-23        — Interviewed by Peter Asaro and Selma Šabanović in Pittsburgh, PA
                    Then-titles: Fredkin Research Professor, Director of the Field Robotics Center,
                    Chief Scientist of the Robotics Engineering Consortium
```
