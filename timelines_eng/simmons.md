# Reid Simmons — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/simmons.md`)

```
Birth                — Born in Akron, Ohio, USA
Around age 2         — Family moved to Buffalo, NY, where he was raised
Undergrad            — Computer Science at the University of Buffalo
After undergrad      — Worked one year at a computer design/graphics company in Ann Arbor
Grad school          — Entered MIT, majoring in artificial intelligence (advisor Randy Davis)
Grad school years    — Encountered robotics indirectly through students in Rod Brooks' group at the same time
PhD period           — Research on causal reasoning, rule-based systems, and the geological interpretation domain
PhD degrees          — Earned M.S./Ph.D. in AI at MIT
1988                 — After PhD, joined Tom Mitchell, Red Whittaker, and Takeo Kanade's NASA Mars Rover project as postdoc; moved to CMU
Around 1988~1992     — Mars rover prototype project; gait planning and navigation planning research
Early 1990s          — Focused on robot software architecture (task-level control) for the first 7-8 years
Mid-1990s            — Markov model-based indoor navigation with Sven Koenig; 300-mile autonomous drive with the Xavier robot
1995                 — Sanjiv Singh became his first PhD graduate (now CMU faculty)
Around 1995          — Student Mark Maimone (post-doc) → moved to JPL, ported CMU algorithms to Mars Spirit/Opportunity
Around 1998          — Joined the autonomous spacecraft AI control system team for the Deep Space 1 mission (witnessed Cape Kennedy launch)
2002                 — Entered the AAAI Mobile Robot Challenge with the Grace robot (multi-institution collaboration)
Around 2002          — Began the Roboceptionist project (in collaboration with Anne Mundell and Michael Chemers of CMU School of Drama)
2004                 — Received the Newell Research Award
1999                 — NASA Software of the Year Award (member of the Remote Agent team)
At interview         — Graduated about 13 PhD students (Sven Koenig at USC, Chris Urmson on leave at Google, etc.)
At interview         — Multi-robot large-structure assembly research with Sanjiv Singh
At interview         — Fault detection / human-assistance research with Brennan Sellner and Laura Hiatt
At interview         — Introduction of Rachel Kirby's omnidirectional humanoid "COMPANION" for social navigation
At interview         — NASA project collaborations including Atacama Desert Trek
November 23, 2010    — ETHW oral history interview by Peter Asaro and Selma Šabanović in Pittsburgh, PA
```
