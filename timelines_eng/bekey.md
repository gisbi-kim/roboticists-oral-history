# George Bekey — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/bekey.md`)

```
Birth             — Born in Bratislava, Slovakia
Just before WWII  — Family moved to Bolivia to wait for U.S. visas
5 years in Bolivia — Waited for visas to be issued
Age 17            — Arrived in Los Angeles with family
Undergrad         — Began Electrical Engineering at UCLA (3 years)
1949–1950         — Senior year at UC Berkeley
1950              — Earned BS in Electrical Engineering from UC Berkeley
1952              — Earned MS in Electrical Engineering from UCLA
After MS          — Operated computers at UCLA; in the afternoons, pursued an MS in Theology at USC seminary (about 2.5–3 years)
Theology halted   — Stopped just before the 4th year
1962              — Earned PhD in Electrical Engineering from UCLA (advisor John Lyman, on human operator models); joined USC
Late 1960s        — With Bob McGhee and PhD student Andrew Frank, built the quadruped walking robot "Phony Pony" (the first in North America)
Late 1960s        — During his TRW years, helped build a hybrid (analog+digital) simulator for intercontinental missiles
1968              — Co-authored book on analog/digital hybrid computation
Early 1970s       — Co-founded the Department of Biomedical Engineering at USC
1980s             — Recruited Barry Soroka (from Stanford); introduced PUMA manipulators with NSF funding; founded the USC Robotics Lab
1984              — Founded the IEEE Journal of Robotics and Automation (later upgraded to Transactions); formed the IEEE robotics group with George Saridis, Antal Bejczy, and others
Mid-1980s         — Collaborated with Tomović (Yugoslavia) on a 5-finger synergistic robotic hand
Mid-1980s         — First entry in the Georgia Tech robot helicopter competition (led by PhD students Andy Fagg and Tony Lewis)
About 20 years ago — Founded the Autonomous Robots journal (Kluwer; Rod Brooks authored Vol. 1, Issue 1)
1990s             — Numerous projects on hexapod/quadruped learning robots, mobile robots, helicopters, and more
1996–1998         — President of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society
Around 2002       — Officially retired from USC (40 years of service)
After 2002        — Continued as faculty at Cal Poly San Luis Obispo, also teaching world religions
2006              — Led an NSF-supported survey delegation on robotics in Asia and Europe
2010              — At time of interview: Resident in Arroyo Grande; produced 38 PhDs; focused on robot ethics research
```
