# Ray Jarvis — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/jarvis.md`)

```
January 9, 1941    — Born in Rangoon, Burma (to British-Asian mixed parents)
1947               — Moved to Freemantle, Australia (west coast); attended Christian Brothers school
Childhood/teens    — Built photo enlargers, model boats, and radios; learned technical drawing at night school
1962               — B.S. in Electrical Engineering, University of Western Australia
PhD program        — Hybrid computing research with IBM 1620, then DEC 10 (first time-sharing computer outside the U.S.); advised by Dr. Brian Leary
1968               — PhD in Electrical Engineering, University of Western Australia — global optimization (random search)
1968~1970          — Visited Purdue University (originally tried to meet King-sun Fu, actually collaborated with Ed Patrick); introduction to pattern recognition; published Jarvis-Patrick (JP) clustering algorithm
1970               — Joined Australian National University (ANU) as Senior Lecturer — Computer Science group within Statistics Department (later spun off as separate department)
1970s              — Revisited Purdue (1-year sabbatical) — joint image segmentation research with King-sun Fu
Around 1980~1982   — Full-scale transition to computer vision and robotics
Around 1982        — After visiting SRI, built his own laser rangefinder (time-of-flight) — "first in the Southern Hemisphere"; won ARC grant
Early 1980s        — Direct phone call with Joe Engelberger to buy Puma 250 robot for $20,000 → started hand-eye coordination
1985               — Joined Monash University Department of Electrical and Computer Systems Engineering as Department Head (Melbourne)
1987               — Founded Intelligent Robotics Research Center at Monash; later Director
1987~1988          — Head of Department, Monash
Around 1991~1993   — 3-year, $1 million GIRD project with BHP (mining company) — 3D stripe-light rangefinder, etc.; adopted at steel mill for measuring rolled sheet surfaces
1992               — IEEE Fellow
Around 1992~1996   — Australian Research Council panel member
Around 1996~1998   — Won special ARC grant — freely purchased $250,000 worth of equipment (manipulators, vehicles, sensors)
Early-to-mid 2000s — Visited Aarne Halme in Finland, brought in Marsokhod small Mars rover from St. Petersburg, Russia; bought a Finnish agricultural tractor vehicle
2003~2007          — Ran Center for Perceptive and Intelligent Machines (half-scale after losing candidacy to Hugh Durrant-Whyte's Center for Autonomous Systems)
Subsequently       — 5-year forest fire extinguishing autonomous driving project with Country Fire Authority (forest fire scout and extinguishing vehicles) — experiments on a 20-acre site outside Melbourne
Ongoing research   — Semi-autonomous wheelchair (joystick → eye-gaze → Kinect-based); covert path planning for security, escape, and disaster scenarios; cyber-model based localization using Velodyne and other sensors
August 30, 2011    — Interviewed by Selma Šabanović and Matthew R. Francisco (Flagstaff, AZ)
At time of interview — Semi-retired, hoping for emeritus status; ongoing multi-robot and HRI research
Posthumous         — Australian Robotics & Automation Association Lifetime Achievement Award (posthumous)
```
