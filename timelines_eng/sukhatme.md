# Gaurav Sukhatme — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/sukhatme.md`)

```
Birth~age 21       — Born and raised in India
Undergrad years    — Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Bombay
Around 1989 (undergrad) — Read about Grey Walter's tortoise robots, SRI Shakey, etc., and became fascinated by robotics
1991               — Earned BTech in Computer Science and Engineering at IIT Bombay
1991               — Entered USC graduate school, moved to the US at age 21
Late 1991~early 1992 — Took George Bekey's intro robotics class, also took Michael Arbib's class
Early grad school  — Joined Bekey's lab; researched robot hand grasping/planners, assisted autonomous helicopter project
1993               — Earned MS in computer science at USC
1993~1994          — Began JPL/NASA-funded mobile robot benchmarking dissertation project
1997               — Earned PhD in computer science at USC
1997               — Joined USC as a Research Associate
1997. 12.          — Promoted to USC Research Assistant Professor
2000               — Promoted to USC Assistant Professor
2000               — Founded and ran the USC Robotic Embedded Systems Lab
2002               — Received NSF CAREER Award
2002               — Co-Director of USC Robotics Research Lab; began as Co-PI of CENS (UCLA) (August)
Early~mid 2000s    — Through CENS involvement, transitioned to marine/underwater robotics research (collaboration with David Caron)
2005               — Promoted to USC Associate Professor (joint appointment in electrical engineering)
2006               — Received Okawa Foundation Research Award
2009               — Promoted to USC Full Professor (joint appointment in electrical engineering)
2009. 9.           — Director of Research, USC Department of Computer Science
2010. 11. 11.      — Interview with Peter Asaro conducted in Los Angeles
At time of interview — Supervised 14 PhDs and 7~8 postdocs (about 20 in total)
```
