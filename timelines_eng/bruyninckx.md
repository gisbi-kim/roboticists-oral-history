# Herman Bruyninckx — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/bruyninckx.md`)

```
December 22, 1962     — Born in Turnhout, Belgium (small town near the Dutch border)
Childhood–high school — Followed his older brother into the science track; similar trajectory until about age 23
1984                  — MS in Mathematics from Katholieke Universiteit Leuven (thesis on a special solution to Einstein's general relativity)
1987                  — MS in Computer Science from the same university (interfacing with the IBM Skyway robot in Modula-2)
1988                  — Postgraduate MS in Mechatronics (CAD–machinery interfacing)
August 1988 – July 1989 — Military service
1988–89               — Began PhD in Mechanical Engineering at Leuven under Joris De Schutter (a third-generation "grandson" of Hendrik Van Brussel)
Early PhD             — Joined the first European project FIRST (Fundamentals of Intelligent Robot Systems); interacted with Mike Brady
PhD years             — Joined the second European project SECOND
1995                  — PhD from Leuven Mechanical Engineering
After 1995            — Research with FWO (Flemish science foundation), Stanford Robotics, and KTH (Sweden) Centre for Autonomous Systems
1998                  — Appointed Assistant Professor at Leuven
November 1, 1998      — Decided to make open source central to his academic research
2000                  — Joined as a member of EURON (European Robotics Research Network)
2001                  — Started the OROCOS project (sparked by a request from three Belgian machine tool companies; OROCOS European project with KTH/LAAS)
October 2003          — Promoted to Associate Professor
2008                  — Promoted to Professor; EURON funding ended and shifted to community-driven model
2007                  — Took over as EURON Coordinator (after Henrik Christensen moved to the US)
Afterwards            — Collaborated with Willow Garage; worked on OROCOS-ROS bridges through the PR2 beta program
RoSta external expert — Participated in robotics standardization activities
Afterwards            — Joined the Rosetta project (preparing safety standards)
RETF push             — Drove the launch of the Robotics Engineering Task Force (modeled on the IETF)
July 8, 2011          — IEEE oral history interview conducted in Leuven
October 2013          — Promoted to Full Professor
February 2014         — Took on a part-time Full Professor appointment at Eindhoven University of Technology
```
