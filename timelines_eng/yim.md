# Mark Yim — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/yim.md`)

```
Childhood          — Born in Cheverly, Maryland
High school        — Wrote in a class essay that "it would be cool to build a scary robot"
1987              — B.S. in Engineering Mechanics from Johns Hopkins University (ME was not yet accredited there)
Graduate           — Entered Stanford University; advisor Jean-Claude Latombe (CS)
                    Both M.S. and Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering
During PhD         — Took Ed Carryer's "Smart Product Design Lab" (mechatronics) — a turning point in his life
1989              — M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford
Around 1990        — Built his first robot, a small assessed car (sensor-based obstacle avoidance)
PhD years          — Started from a cartwheel-robot idea, leading to the modular self-reconfigurable robot concept
                    (his wife suggested "let it reconfigure itself too")
1994              — PhD in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford
Right after PhD    — Joined his friend's startup Virtual Technologies, working on haptic feedback for VR gloves
                    (vibration feedback patents from this period later contributed to the Immersion vs. Microsoft/Sony
                    lawsuits, which settled for $108 million)
Later              — Moved to Xerox PARC; researched MEMS-based modular robots
                    DARPA office director suggested a PM role, which he declined; instead won a grant of about
                    $4.5 million from that program to push modular research, including PolyBot
                    Founded a 5–6 person robotics group at PARC (left due to layoff/management burden, etc.)
Around 2004        — Joined the University of Pennsylvania Mechanical Engineering as Associate Professor
                    (recruited by Vijay Kumar); joined the GRASP Lab
                    Later collaborated on modular robots with Daniela Rus, Hod Lipson, Eric Klavins, etc.
                    Served on the Willow Garage advisory board for ~5–6 years, visiting each summer for research
                    Joined the DARPA Nano Air Vehicle consortium via Lockheed (with Vijay Kumar on maple-seed
                    flyer control) — finished early, then had budget clawed back
Recent             — Collaborated with architect Simon Kim for ~5 years: robot ballet, Shakespeare,
                    a collaborative opera with Opera Philadelphia
Around 2014        — Started a Penn startup via the UPstart program based on low-cost robot arm IP
                    Developed mobile manipulators for nursing homes via an NSF grant
                    (continued snake-robot work, inspired by a snake found in the family car)
At time of interview — Gabel Family Associate Professor, GRASP Lab
2014-09-15        — Interviewed by Peter Asaro in Chicago, IL
```
