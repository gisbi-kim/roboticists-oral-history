# Carl Ruoff — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/ruoff.md`)

```
Birth               — Born in Los Angeles, California, USA; grew up in Compton
1960                — Graduated from Compton Senior High School
Early 1960s         — After working, attended Graceland College (a religious school) in Ohio; dropped out after one semester
1961–1967           — Worked as a mechanical designer at Arrowhead Metal Products, Philco-Ford, TRW, Purex, etc.
1967                — BS in Mathematics from California State University, Long Beach
1967–1970           — Scientific programmer at McDonnell-Douglas Aircraft
1968–1969           — Lecturer in the UCLA Physics Department
1970–1971           — Lecturer at UCLA again
1971                — MS in Physics from UCLA (originally aimed at particle physics; switched due to job market)
Summer 1971         — Summer job at Information International (machine-language programming experience)
April 1972          — Joined Bendix (Dayton, Ohio); right after marrying, drove a Volkswagen out and joined the PACS smart robot project
1972–1974           — Manager of the PACS control system at Bendix (preparing the 1974 Machine Tool Show demonstration)
September 1977      — Joined GE R&D Center (Schenectady), research on robotics and computer vision
1977                — On a business trip, visited SRI International and contacted JPL personnel
October 1978        — Joined Jet Propulsion Lab (JPL), Robotics & Teleoperator Group; wrapped up the 1984 Mars Rover project
After 1978          — Promoted to Robotics Group supervisor after his boss left for Boeing
1982                — Hired Brian Wilcox, with whom he had long worked at Bendix
1982                — Met Fred Culick at Caltech's "Physics of Computation" course taught by Feinman, Carver Mead, and John Hopfield
1984                — Co-wrote a JPL/Caltech joint robotics proposal with Fred Culick, secured $300K/year DDF
1985                — Entered Caltech PhD program (advisor Fred Culick; CS minor under Carver Mead)
1985                — Started the Telerobotics Project at JPL (~30 people), proposed local autonomy
1989                — Outdoor autonomous driving demonstration in an arroyo near JPL (with Brian Wilcox and others)
1990s               — Participated in the Sojourner Mars rover project (arrived on Mars in 1997, project manager Donna Shirley)
1993                — PhD in Mechanical Engineering from Caltech (dissertation: neural-network-based hand-eye coordination and learning to catch)
1995–               — Began as lecturer in UCLA Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering
1996                — JPL Outstanding Mentor Award
1996–2000           — Visiting Associate Professor in Caltech Mechanical Engineering
Late 1990s          — Served as Robotics Section manager, supported Sojourner and many other Mars projects
2001–               — Division Technologist for the JPL Observational Systems Division & Science Division
2008                — NASA Exceptional Service Medal
June 2, 2011        — ETHW oral history interview with Selma Šabanović and Peter Asaro (Pasadena)
```
