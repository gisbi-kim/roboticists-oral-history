# Luc Steels — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/steels.md`)

```
1952. 1. 22.       — Born in a village near Antwerp, Belgium
Undergrad/grad years — Studied linguistics at the University of Antwerp (BA and MA)
1974              — Earned MA in linguistics
1977              — Earned PhD in linguistics
1977              — Moved to MIT, began computer science research at the AI Lab under Marvin Minsky
1979              — Earned MS in computer science at MIT; PhD dissertation on distributed problem solving influenced by Society of Mind
Right after MIT   — Joined Schlumberger, applying knowledge engineering to geology and oil exploration
1980              — Schlumberger-Doll researcher
1982              — Promoted to leader of Schlumberger's geological expert systems program
1983              — Returned to Belgium, appointed full professor of computer science at Vrije Universiteit Brussel (VUB), founded the AI Lab
1984~1985         — Collaborated with Oxford ethologist David McFarland, formed the "agent" concept
1986~early 1990s  — Behavior-based robotics, LEGO vehicle ecosystem experiments (influenced by Elmer/Elsie)
1988              — Hosted Chris Langton; co-organized European workshop with Rodney Brooks
1989              — Co-founded VUB Computer Science Department and became department chair
1994              — Began long-term collaboration with Sony Tokyo group (pre-AIBO), spent one year in Tokyo
1994              — Shifted back to language research, launched evolutionary language systems research program
1995              — Ended VUB department chair role
1996              — Co-founded and became operating director of Sony Computer Science Lab Paris
Early 2000s       — Spatial language experiments with Sony QRIO humanoid (with students such as Michael Spranger)
Around 2007 onward — Used Sony QRIO humanoid (about 7 years as of the interview)
Around 2011       — CREA Research Professor at CSIC/UPF Institute of Evolutionary Biology
2011. 7. 8.       — Interview with Peter Asaro conducted in Brussels
2011. 7. 18.      — Humanoid robot opera scheduled for the IJCAI opening in Barcelona
Career totals     — Supervised over 30 PhDs, 200+ papers, 15 edited books
```
