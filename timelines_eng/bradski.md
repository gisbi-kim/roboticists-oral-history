# Gary Bradski — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/bradski.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Arizona, then grew up in Palo Alto (Silicon Valley)
High school           — Graduated from Palo Alto High School
Undergrad             — EE major at UC Berkeley (originally physics, later switched); worked on wind turbine research under Prof. Otto Smith
Graduate school       — PhD in biological brain modeling at Boston University under Stephen Grossberg (had earlier experience at Neuroscience, an EEG company)
Right after PhD       — Briefly worked as a Wall Street quant, then joined Intel Research (image processing libraries group)
1999                  — Started OpenCV at Intel Research (leveraging Russian contractors)
2001                  — OpenCV officially announced at CVPR
Dot-com bust era      — Collaborated with Intel Manufacturing on statistical machine learning; released the Bayes Net Learning Library
Sabbatical period     — Sebastian Thrun visited Intel Research, leading to a sabbatical at Stanford
DARPA Grand Challenge 2 — Joined the Stanford team (Stanley), organized the computer vision team, and won
Late Intel years      — Used DARPA Cognitive Architectures subcontract funding to buy a robot arm, sparking Andrew Ng's STAIR program
Afterwards            — Left Intel; spent about a year at the Video Surf startup
Around 2008           — Joined Willow Garage at Andrew Ng's invitation; continued OpenCV support
Willow Garage years   — Developed PR2 perception/object recognition framework; integrated ROS with OpenCV
April 2011            — Held the first Solutions in Perception Challenge with NIST and the White House OSTP (Thomas Kalil)
Afterwards            — Co-founded Industrial Perception; taught Perception for Robotics at Stanford
June 9, 2011          — IEEE oral history interview conducted
```
