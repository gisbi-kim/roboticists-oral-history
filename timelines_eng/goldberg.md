# Ken Goldberg — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/goldberg.md`)

```
Birth                — Born in Ikenne (a small village), Nigeria; parents both teachers (father a metallurgist, mother a psychologist)
Age 6 months         — Family moved to Columbus, OH, USA, where his father did a PhD at Ohio State
Later                — Moved to Bethlehem, Pennsylvania (a steel town), where he grew up
Undergrad            — Entered the University of Pennsylvania, initially metallurgy → switched major to electrical engineering
Undergrad junior     — Studied abroad in Scotland for a year; captivated by robotics after taking an AI course
Late undergrad       — After returning to Penn, did undergraduate research in Ruzena Bajcsy's ESP (Experimental Sensory Processing) Lab
1984                 — BS in electrical engineering from the University of Pennsylvania
PhD years            — Entered CMU; studied under Marc Raibert (tactile sensing) and Matt Mason (manipulation theory)
CMU years            — PhD thesis: part-orienting algorithms for parallel-jaw grippers (a sensorless solution for all polygonal parts)
CMU years            — Filed a low-friction gripper mechanism patent (granted after graduation)
CMU years            — Took courses on stochastic manipulation and decision theory
CMU years            — Collaborated with art students on a robot painting installation; held a solo show in the student gallery before graduating
1990                 — PhD in computer science from CMU
1990                 — Joined USC as faculty (in George Bekey's group); continued manipulation research on part feeding/fixturing
1993                 — USC students introduced him to the World Wide Web; began the idea of a web robot
1994-08              — Public release of the Mercury Project (the world's first telerobot, a virtual radioactive excavation scenario); a week later the Australian Trevelyan team released stacking blocks
1995                 — Moved to the University of California, Berkeley
Around 1995~96       — Released the Telegarden (operated 24/7 for 9 years; color camera, plant raising, social chat)
Around 1995~96       — Co-founded the IEEE RAS Technical Committee on Networked Robots (with Roland Siegwart, Wolfram Burgard, etc.)
Early Berkeley       — Met philosopher Hubert Dreyfus; began a 4-year telepistemology project
Late 1990s           — Tele-Actor project with Eric Paulos and John Canny (NSF, 3 years)
2000                 — Exhibited an Ouija board piece at the Whitney Biennial
2002                 — Commissioned by IEEE RAS President Dick Volz as planning chair for a new automation journal
2003~2008            — CONE (Collaborative Observatories for Natural Environments) project (Texas bird observation), Arkansas ivory-billed woodpecker search
2004                 — Launched IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering (the result of 3 years of planning)
Around 2005          — Expanded into medical robotics; collaborated with Johns Hopkins (Allison Okamura, Russ Taylor, Greg Chirikjian, Noah Cowan) and the NIH
Later                — Patented steerable needles
Around 2008          — Collaborated with new Berkeley faculty member Peter Abbeel on learning-based surgical robotics, applied to the da Vinci
At time of interview — Director of the Center for New Media; working on the Opinion Space social media visualization, the Robotics 2.0 white paper, and an installation at the Contemporary Jewish Museum
2010-11-17           — IEEE oral history interview in Berkeley, CA
```
