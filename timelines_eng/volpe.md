# Richard Volpe — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/volpe.md`)

```
1984              — Earned a B.S. in Physics from Loyola College Baltimore
Junior year        — Took the "Robots in the Workplace" Jan-term course; became fascinated with robotics
                    and decided to pursue graduate study at CMU
1984              — Entered Carnegie Mellon University's physics graduate program
1986              — Earned an M.S. in Physics at CMU
                    Worked in Takeo Kanade's lab afterwards; due to funding, briefly moved to
                    Steve Shafer's group (computer vision)
During PhD         — Switched to manipulation research under Pradeep Khosla (using NSF funding left over after
                    Marc Raibert's move to MIT); won an Air Force fellowship
PhD years          — Integrated force control / impedance control work on the DirectDriveArm-2 (DDArm-2)
1990              — Earned a Ph.D. in Applied Physics from CMU
1991              — Joined JPL; worked under Samad Hayati on a manipulation project for space station exterior inspection
Early JPL          — Manipulation-focused work, including Mars Rover Sample Return
Mid-1990s         — Joined the Rocky-7 rover effort as manipulation lead, later overall lead engineer and task manager
1997              — Tested the first version of Rocky-7 around the time of the Sojourner Mars landing
Later              — Led the CLARAty software system; moved into program management
2001~2004         — Manager for Mars Regional Mobility and Subsurface Access in JPL's
                    Space Exploration Technology Program Office
2004~             — Section Manager of the Mobility and Robotic Systems Section under the
                    Autonomous Systems Division (8 groups, ~100 people)
2007~             — Member of the JPL Science and Technology Management Committee (STMC)
June 2008          — Joined the Phoenix Mars Lander mission's robot arm operations team; spent 3 months
                    in Tucson operating on Mars time, contributing to scientific findings such as the discovery of ice
2011-06-06        — Interviewed by Peter Asaro and Selma Šabanović in Pasadena, CA
```
