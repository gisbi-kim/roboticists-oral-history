# Tomas Lozano-Perez — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/lozano_perez.md`)

```
1952. 8. 21           — Born in Guantánamo, Cuba
~Age 10               — Left Cuba; brief stay in Miami, then moved to Puerto Rico
High school           — Graduated from high school in Puerto Rico
University            — Entered MIT (changed major to Electrical Engineering and Computer Science)
1973                  — BS from MIT; undergraduate thesis in computer vision under Patrick Winston
1973~1974             — Worked at MIT AI Lab (1 year); helped Hirochika Inoue with the Little Robot during his visit, sparking interest in robotics
Start of grad school  — On Winston's recommendation, researched mechanical assembly languages; evolved into FINDSPACE/MAKESPACE problem of SHRDLU
1976                  — MS from MIT (introduced grasp set concept for grasping); IJCAI paper presentation
1976~1977             — Worked one year at IBM T.J. Watson Research Center; participated in AUTOPASS project with Peter Will and Mike Wesley; demonstrated visibility-graph-based motion planning
1979                  — After negotiation with IBM, published in Communications of the ACM under the condition of "no robot implementation" (co-authored with Wesley)
PhD years             — Advised by Berthold Horn; coined "configuration space" and developed generalized algorithms
1980                  — PhD from MIT (configuration space approach)
1981                  — Appointed faculty at MIT
Early 1980s           — Joint research on fine motion planning (LMT) with Russ Taylor and Matt Mason; published pre-image backchaining
Early 1980s           — Served twice as IEEE ICRA program chair
1980s                 — Developed HANDEY system (integrated grasping and regrasping); object recognition research with Eric Grimson via Interpretation Tree
1980s, first PhD wave — Advised John Canny, Bruce Donald, Mike Erdmann, Van-Duc Nguyen, Nancy Pollard, and others
1990                  — Temporarily left robotics; turned to molecular modeling and machine learning (computer-aided drug discovery)
1990~1994             — Senior Staff Fellow at Arris Pharmaceuticals; established multiple instance learning problem with Tom Dietterich
2000s                 — After MIT departmental administrative duties (Associate Department Head, etc.), returned to robotics during sabbatical
~2005                 — Sabbatical in Barcelona, Spain
After return          — Joint research with Leslie Kaelbling on POMDP-based grasping and belief space planning; discussions of continuous POMDPs with Russ Tedrake and Rob Platt
2010                  — Received Willow Garage PR2 (one of 11)
2011                  — IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Pioneer Award
2011                  — ETHW oral history interview (Flagstaff, AZ)
```
