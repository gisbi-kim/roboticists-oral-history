# Terry Fong — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/fong.md`)

```
Birth                — Born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, USA
Primary/secondary    — Grew up in the suburbs of Chicago
Undergrad            — Entered MIT in aerospace engineering; dream of designing aircraft shifted toward robotics
Undergrad sophomore  — Part-time job at the MIT Space Systems Lab (Dave Akin); started neutral buoyancy robotics
1988                 — BS from MIT
Master's             — Developed the SPAM (Stewart Platform Augmented Manipulator) underwater robot arm at the MIT Space Systems Lab
1990                 — MS in aerospace from MIT
1990                 — First stint at NASA Ames (a break before the PhD)
1990~1991            — Under group leader Mike Sims; worked on iWARP/parallel computing and VR interfaces
Around 1991          — Group leader changed to Butler Hine; VR user interface research in earnest (Polhemus/Ascension tracking)
Early 1990s          — Collaborated with planetary scientists like Carol Stoker; teleoperation of an ROV in McMurdo Sound, Antarctica
1994                 — Collaborated with CMU on the Dante II Mount Spurr volcano exploration robot
1994                 — Ended his first stint at NASA Ames; entered the CMU Robotics PhD program
Early CMU            — Researched adding proprioception to Navlab under Chuck Thorpe and Red Whittaker
1.5 years into PhD   — Wife joined a Swiss research group, so left Pittsburgh and moved to Switzerland; conducted PhD research at EPFL
1996-03~2000-12      — Co-founder and VP at Fourth Planet, Inc. (a California startup, VR visualization)
EPFL years           — Co-advised by Charles Baur in the "Virtual Reality and Active Interface" group; collaborative-control research with Pioneer robots
2001                 — PhD from Carnegie Mellon (HRI / collaborative control)
2002~2004            — Deputy leader / postdoc in the EPFL VR & Active Interfaces group
2004-06              — Returned to NASA Ames; collaborated with Illah Nourbakhsh
Right after 2004     — Launched the "Peer-to-Peer Human Robot Interaction" project (collaborating with Reid Simmons, the Robonaut group, and Alan Schultz/NRL)
Late 2004            — Took over as group director when Illah Nourbakhsh returned to CMU; grew the group from about 15 to 32 people
Late 2000s           — Pivoted toward Robots for Human Exploration; planetary mapping work (HiRISE/MRO, LROC, Apollo Metric Camera)
Late 2000s           — Google Earth Moon/Mars collaboration (a neighboring campus); used GigaPan
Around 2010          — Public crowdsourcing of GigaPan-based simulated human exploration mission scouting
2011-06-10           — IEEE oral history interview at Moffett Field, CA
2017                 — Appointed NASA Senior Scientist for Autonomous Systems (ending his role as Intelligent Robotics Group director)
```
