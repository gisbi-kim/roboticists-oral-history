# Brian Wilcox — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/wilcox.md`)

```
Childhood          — Born in Ridgecrest, California; grew up at China Lake (a Naval weapons research complex)
                    Father was an NWC engineer (PhD in physics); learned physics from family discussions early on
                    Built rockets with neighborhood kids (multiple dangerous incidents)
After 2nd grade    — Moved to Washington, D.C. (his father was assigned to the Pentagon)
A year later       — Moved to Detroit, Michigan (father joined GM)
5th grade ~ college — Lived in Santa Barbara, CA (after GM's new defense division relocated to nearby Goleta)
1968              — First job in high school at Minicars, Inc., working on hybrid/shared-vehicle concepts
1973              — Earned B.S. in Physics and B.S. in Mathematics from UC Santa Barbara
1973~              Entered the UC Berkeley physics PhD program; within 6 months realized he was an engineer, not a physicist
1975              — Altair PC appeared — co-founded Polymorphic Systems (3 founders)
                    Grew (to ~50 people) by developing first-generation memory-mapped video terminal interfaces, etc.
1977~1978         — Lost $500K in the wake of computer-store bankruptcies; sold the company to an investor group
Late 1979          — External investment came into Polymorphic Systems; Wilcox's stake reduced to 5%
Summer 1982        — Joined JPL via Carl Ruoff (joining the Mars Rover Sample Return project)
1985              — Became supervisor of the JPL Robotic Vehicles Group (~2005)
Around 1989        — Mars Rover Sample Return concept came in at $13B; cancelled by NASA HQ
                    Followed by the shift to Mars Environmental Survey → Pathfinder/Sojourner development
1992              — M.S. in Electrical Engineering from USC (non-thesis, via JPL TV link)
                    Received the NASA Exceptional Engineering Achievement Medal
1996              — Mars Pathfinder launched
1997              — Sojourner Mars landing/operations (Wilcox's group handled SW, electronics, control;
                    diagnosed/fixed a comms issue in 36 hours; succeeded once each at hazard avoidance and find-rock)
1999              — Received the JPL Award for Excellence
Later              — Various planetary exploration robot work, including nanorovers
Around 2005        — Stepped down as supervisor of the Robotic Vehicles Group
2005~ (interview)  — PI for ATHLETE (All-Terrain Hex-Limbed Extra-Terrestrial Explorer);
                    pursuing future lunar/asteroid applications (bought a Hollywood prop asteroid model
                    for $6,000 to build a simulation environment)
2011-06-02        — Interviewed by Selma Šabanović and Peter Asaro in Pasadena, CA
```
