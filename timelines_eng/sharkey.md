# Noel Sharkey — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/sharkey.md`)

```
1948                   — Born at Belfast Royal Victoria Hospital, Northern Ireland
Around age 4           — Father fell ill; family moved to mother's hometown of Coleraine
Childhood              — Childhood and adolescent education in Coleraine
Pre-college            — Worked as electrician's apprentice at the University of Coleraine, helping build the University of Ulster
1970                   — At age 21, moved to England and began work as a psychiatric nurse (1000-bed hospital)
Early 1970s            — Quit nursing midway, took a 2-year break, then returned to high school
University entrance    — Studied English, mathematics, music, psychology, and English literature at a UK university
PhD period             — Earned PhD in experimental psychology at Exeter University under Don Mitchell
PhD period             — Word recognition (top-down model) research; influenced by Schank/Abelson scripts
Around 1981            — Postdoctoral researcher at Yale University; natural language understanding research in Roger Schank's AI Lab
After 1981             — Switched to machine learning research; began studying calculus/linear algebra
Stanford period        — Joined Gordon Bower's lab (before age 35); cognitive psychology/memory research
Around age 35          — Returned to UK as New Blood Lectureship in the Linguistics department at Essex University
Essex period           — Tenured within 2 years; later promoted to Reader and Director of the Centre for Cognitive Science
Late Essex             — Moved to the Computer Science department; introduced robots
Around 1988            — Developed interest in grounded language after a short visit to Lakoff's group (Berkeley)
After 1988             — First robot "Frank" (biscuit tin form) neural network learning experiment — successfully drove autonomously down hallways
Early 1990s            — Acquired a Nomad robot ($25k), Khepera robots, and others; built up a lab of about 12 PhD students
Early 1990s            — Ran a bio-inspired robotics conference in London; first BBC TV appearance (unintentionally appeared on a live news broadcast)
Early 1990s            — Demonstrated camera GA back-prop experiment on BBC2's "Tomorrow's World"
Mid-1990s              — Appointed full professor in the Computer Science department at Sheffield University (with safety-critical systems grant)
Late 1990s             — Joined BBC "Robot Wars" as judge; ran for 7 series + international editions, totaling 16 series
Same period            — BBC "Techno Games" commentator; co-host of "Bright Sparks"
Around 2000            — Developed predator-prey GA robot exhibit at the Magna Science Adventure Centre in Rotherham (UK) with a 5-postdoc team (18 months)
Early 2000s            — 14ft autonomous blimp project, built by Per Lindstrand, operated at Magna
Mid-2000s              — Awarded EPSRC Senior Media Fellowship (3 years + 2-year extension)
Around 2005~2006       — Began serious investigation of military UAVs/autonomous weapons and study of humanitarian law
2007                   — The Guardian op-ed "Robot Wars are a Reality" — argued robots cannot distinguish combatants from non-combatants
Around 2008            — Met with Jurgen Altmann, Rob Sparrow, Peter Asaro, and others at Sheffield
Around 2009            — Co-founded the International Committee for Robot Arms Control (ICRAC)
Late professorial      — Editor-in-chief of Connection Science journal (22-year tenure)
Late professorial      — Director of Centre for the Policy of Emerging Technologies (European chapter), NHS Health 2020 advisor, Nuffield Foundation member
At interview           — Age 64, full professor of robotics/AI at Sheffield University
March 24, 2013         — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro in Sheffield
```
