# Shinichi Yuta — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/yuta.md`)

```
1948              — Born in Nagasaki Prefecture, Japan; grew up in Tokyo
1966              — Entered Keio University, majoring in electrical engineering (advanced from Keio High School without entrance exams)
                    Also interested in social sciences and economics; ran reading groups with friends
Graduate          — Researched electrical circuits, systems theory, optimization, and control under
                    Associate Professor Takahashi at Keio (theory-focused, unrelated to mechanics)
1975              — PhD in Electrical Engineering from Keio University
1975~1978         — Research assistant at the Tokyo University of Agriculture and Technology (Koganei)
                    Researched control applications in medical imaging (ultrasound diagnostics, CT); introduced EMI's CT to Japan
                    Collaborated with Osaka Medical University; one of the world's first ultrasound CT researchers, by his own account
1978              — Moved as a lecturer to the Institute of Electronics and Information Sciences (IEIS) at the
                    University of Tsukuba (Tsukuba, not Kobe)
                    Continued medical imaging research; later decided to pivot to industrial applications
Early Tsukuba      — At Professor Kameyama's suggestion, joined students in building small mobile robots for the
                    micro mouse maze contest, entering robotics through this (recognizing its value as an educational tool)
Late 1980s         — Began full-scale autonomous mobile robot and autonomous navigation research
                    Set the long-term goal of an 8 km autonomous return to the lab from Doho Park in Tsukuba City
Later              — Recognized the need for optical range sensors; co-developed compact, lightweight, low-cost
                    scanning range sensors with Hokuyo (now standardized worldwide in robotics)
                    Provided vehicle control SW for Mitsubishi Heavy Industry's "wakamaru" robot
                    (provided source code and explanations without formal budget)
                    Shared navigation know-how with Arisaka and other security companies for security robots
                    Many of his students went on to major companies such as Honda, Toyota, Hitachi, and Mitsubishi
During Tsukuba     — Dean of the College of Engineering Systems (2 years)
                    Chair of the Institute of Engineering Mechanics and Systems (2 years)
                    Vice President of the University of Tsukuba (research, international, and industry liaison) (2 years)
                    Director of the Industry Liaison Office (4 years)
                    Collaborated with Professor Harada Akira (now President of Sapporo Municipal University) on a
                    remote mobile robot for a Kansei Engineering museum
Around 2006 (5 years before the interview) — Started Tsukuba Challenge — a public 1 km autonomous-driving experiment on pedestrian streets
                    (a collaborative learning event, not a competition; with cooperation from police and Tsukuba City)
                    50–70 teams from across Japan participate every year
At 2011            — Professor in the Graduate School of Systems and Information Engineering at the University of Tsukuba; ~40 students in the lab
                    33rd year at Tsukuba
2011-07-06        — Interviewed by Selma Šabanović and Matthew Francisco in San Francisco, CA
```
