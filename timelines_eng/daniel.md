# Ron Daniel — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/daniel.md`)

```
Birth                — Born in Maidstone, Kent, England
School years         — Attended Maidstone Technical High School for Boys (with engineering education)
Undergrad            — BSc in electrical engineering from Brunel University, London
PhD years            — PhD in control theory (the H-infinity era) at Cambridge King's College under Professor McFarlane
1982-02-01           — Joined Oxford (Sir Edmund Hall) as a research fellow; began nuclear reactor remote-manipulator research with the CEGB at Marchwood
1982~1985            — Developed remote maintenance systems for CEGB Magnox reactors (kinematics, singularities, etc.)
Early 1980s          — Named the "Robotics Research Group" with PhD student Vaughan Mitchell, around the time Mike Brady and Dave Forsyth joined
Around 1985          — Mike Brady was appointed to the Oxford chair in Information Engineering (Daniel involved in recruiting him)
Late 1980s           — Collaborated with the UK Atomic Energy Authority (Harwell); with PhD student Patrick Fischer developed a BSP (Bilateral Stewart Platform) 6-DOF direct-drive haptic device combined with a Puma 560
Late 1980s           — After visiting JPL, demonstrated remote LEGO assembly at Oxford (a 9-year-old child using force feedback on the Puma 560)
Late 1980s           — Built a direct-drive compliant arm; worked on exo-skeleton-based calibration; parallel inverse kinematics on transputers
Late 1980s           — Identified the gearbox stick-slip phase-lag mechanism; experimented with differential drive
Late 1980s onward    — Collaborated with JET (Joint European Torus) on force-reflection teleoperation
Around 1990          — Collaborated with Mary Marlborough Lodge on accelerometer wristbands for gesture recognition in disabled children (early form)
Late 1990s~2000s     — During the robot lab rebuild, conducted independent computer vision research (stereo + photometric stereo + Kalman filter) from a 7th-floor office
2000s                — Hardware-in-the-loop robot research in a wind tunnel with Mark Batcheck
Late 2000s           — Spun off Eykona Technologies (later Fuel3D Technologies), commercializing a 3D wound (ulcer) measurement camera
At time of interview — Fuel3D technical advisor + Oxford engineering lecturer + Brasenose College Fellow; shifting toward jet engine mechanism applications
2013-03-09           — IEEE oral history interview at Oxford
```
