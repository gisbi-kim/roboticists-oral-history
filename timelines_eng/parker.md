# Lynne Parker — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/parker.md`)

```
Birth               — Born in Knoxville, Tennessee, USA
Childhood           — Older sister (one year senior) chose engineering; she chose computer science to differentiate herself (at her father's suggestion)
Undergrad years     — BS in Computer Science from Tennessee Tech University (Cookeville)
MS years            — Entered MS program in Computer Science at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville (evening program)
During MS           — Worked full-time at Oak Ridge National Lab (ORNL); after taking Chuck Weismann's AI course, joined ORNL's CESAR robotics lab
Early ORNL          — Joined the Human-Robot Symbiosis project with the HERMES series of mobile robots (in charge of task allocation)
MS thesis           — Algorithm research on robot navigation in environments with multiple moving targets (simulation)
1989                — Took leave from ORNL, entered MIT PhD program (advisor Rodney Brooks)
PhD years           — Multi-robot cooperation research, developed the ALLIANCE distributed architecture (the world's first PhD dissertation in multi-robot)
1994                — PhD in Computer Science from MIT, returned to ORNL
1994–2002           — Distinguished R&D Staff Member at ORNL, continued multi-robot research
2002                — Joined the EECS department at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville (affiliated with the CISML center)
Post-PhD years      — Mentored students and postdocs including Yi Guo, Raj Madhavan, Stefano Carpin, Daisy Tang, and Hao Zhang
UTK era             — Founded the Distributed Intelligence Lab; peer-to-peer HRI research with the Meka robot
2012–2014           — After serving as editor of IEEE Transactions on Robotics, Editor-in-Chief of the ICRA Conference Editorial Board (2012, 13, 14)
2014                — Editor-in-Chief of CEB at ICRA 2014 (Hong Kong) (program chair: Bill Hamel)
2015                — General Chair of IEEE ICRA 2015 (all-female organizing committee, including herself)
Early 2017          — Detailed to NSF, became Division Director of the Information and Intelligent Systems (IIS) Division (within CISE, overseeing NRI, Big Data, etc.)
December 15, 2017   — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro (Indiana University)
Afterwards          — Served as Associate Dean and Interim Dean of the UTK Tickle College of Engineering
Afterwards          — Took up roles as White House OSTP Deputy CTO and Assistant Director of AI
```
