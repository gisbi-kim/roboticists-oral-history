# Manuela Veloso — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/veloso.md`)

```
Birth              — Born in Lisbon, Portugal
Primary/secondary education — French school (primary) followed by public high school in Lisbon
1980               — Earned Licenciatura in electrical engineering at Instituto Superior Técnico (Lisbon)
MS years           — Researched information automation/databases
1984               — Earned MS in electrical and computer engineering at Instituto Superior Técnico
1984               — Moved to Boston University (USA), began studying computer science
1986               — Earned MS in computer science at Boston University
1986               — Entered Carnegie Mellon University CS PhD program
PhD program        — Advised by Jaime Carbonell; PhD dissertation on analogical reasoning-based planning, with Herb Simon on the committee
PhD years          — Observed Hans Moravec/Chuck Thorpe's outdoor autonomous navigation robot on her commute path
1992               — Earned PhD in CS at CMU; after interviewing at 16 schools, stayed at CMU after a phone call from Allen Newell
1992               — Appointed Assistant Professor at CMU School of Computer Science; advised first student Karen Haigh on Reid Simmons's Xavier robot
1994               — At AAAI, watched Alan Mackworth/Michael Sahota's 1-on-1 robot soccer demo; student Peter Stone decided to do a PhD on robot soccer
1995               — Received NSF CAREER Award
1995~1996          — Built first small soccer robots (assembled with hot glue gun)
1996               — Co-founded RoboCup (with Hiroaki Kitano, Minoru Asada, Dominique Duhaut, Enrico Pagello, Silvia Coradeschi, etc.)
1997               — First RoboCup competition at IJCAI; began Sony AIBO collaboration (through Masahiro Fujita)
1997               — Promoted to Associate Professor
1998               — Experienced "kidnapped robot" problem during a Sony AIBO game → researched new localization algorithms
1999~2000          — Visiting Professor at MIT (Rodney Brooks's lab, co-taught 6.034 with Patrick Winston)
2002               — Promoted to Full Professor at CMU; from this year Sony AIBOs gained wireless communication capability
2002               — Sony AIBO learning/activity recognition research (with Sonia Chernova, Doug Vail, etc.)
2005~2006          — New hardware introduced for CM Dragons small robots (Mike Licitra, Stefan Zickler, James Bruce)
2006               — Named Herbert A. Simon Professor
2006~2007          — Sabbatical at Harvard Radcliffe Institute (Sargent-Faull Fellow), exchanges with social scientists
After 2007         — RoboCup trustees began annual 5 humans vs. 5 winning robots match
2009               — Received ACM/SIGART Autonomous Agents Research Award
Around 2011        — Began developing CoBot service robot with Joydeep Biswas; introduced "symbiotic autonomy" concept
Around 2012        — Incident of CoBot autonomously disappearing → turning point in recognition of autonomous creations
2013~2014          — Sabbatical at NYU CUSP, researched urban data
2014               — Promoted to University Professor
2014               — AAAI President-Elect; Past President of International RoboCup Federation
2014. 9. 16.       — Interview with Peter Asaro conducted in Chicago
At time of interview — 4 CoBots had cumulatively driven about 1,000 km autonomously in CMU buildings
```
