# Kazuhiro Kosuge — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kosuge.md`)

```
1955                  — Born in Tsu City, Mie Prefecture, Japan
University            — Entered Tokyo Institute of Technology, studied control engineering under Prof. Katsuhisa Furuta
After MS              — Joined Nippon Denso (now DENSO), worked there for about 2 years and 3 months
~1980                 — Returned to Tokyo Tech's control engineering department; appointed Research Associate without a doctorate (possible under the Japanese system)
While in position     — Researched nonlinear control theory; developed model-following controller for manipulators (using Nova/Data General minicomputers)
Mid-1980s             — Researched dual-arm cooperative control using direct-drive manipulators
~1988                 — Developed single master multi-slave control system using Nachi-Fujikoshi industrial robots (custom CPU in collaboration with Sony)
1988                  — Received Ph.D. (control engineering) from his alma mater
1989~1990             — Visited MIT Mechanical Engineering for one year; researched master-slave control in Harry Asada's group
After return          — Appointed Associate Professor in Toshio Fukuda's lab at Nagoya University (4 years 6 months)
During this period    — Developed manipulator-based human power augmentation systems; established stability-guaranteed impedance control
Age 39                — Moved to a mechanical engineering-related department at Tohoku University as full Professor
~1997                 — While researching cooperative control of multiple mobile robots, started Dance Partner Robot project (triggered by a hospitalized student's research topic change)
2005                  — Demonstrated dance partner robot PBDR at Aichi Expo NEDO project (collaboration with designer Tatsuya Okonogi and Nomura Unison; 2-week demonstration)
Later                 — Based on Aichi Expo experience, developed PaDY (Parts/tools-Delivery-to-You) robot for automotive factories
Ongoing               — Continued physical human-robot interaction research with funding from JSPS Kakenhi, NEDO, and industry
Society activities    — Founded and chaired SICE System Integration Division; RSJ board member and Editor-in-Chief of Advanced Robotics (5 years, expanded from 4 to 8 issues per year); JSME mechatronics division chair
IEEE RAS              — Served as Junior Past President; led membership fee reduction and introduction of e-membership (dues from 22 to 9 USD)
2012                  — Interview (St. Paul, MN, ICRA)
```
