# Alicia Casals — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/casals.md`)

```
Birth/upbringing      — Born in Barcelona, Spain
Undergrad             — BS in Industrial Engineering at Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC)
Right after graduation — Began teaching at the same university; research in computing and vision
Early 1980s           — Advisor Josep Amat visited Japan as part of a Catalan delegation and brought back robotics information, sparking her work in robotics
Early activities      — Ran robotics training courses for industry (the first in the region); hands-on with PUMA and ABB robots
PhD                   — PhD at UPC on object recognition based on curvature contours
1986                  — After Spain's EU accession, began collaboration with LAAS (Toulouse, George Giralt)
1987                  — First EU project (Esprit, with LAAS, MATRA, and Oxford) on mobile robots, vision, and tracking (competed with the Connection Machine)
Early 1990s           — Second EU project with Philips (automation of screen manufacturing); vision-based industrial applications
Early 1990s           — Built a Braille keyboard for visually impaired students; developed soft assistive robots based on foam-rubber modules (with hospital partners)
1992–93               — Began collaborations with surgeons and marine biologists (underwater), expanding robotics application areas
Around 1995           — Contract with Digital to develop 3D vision (tracking and object recognition)
1996                  — First clinical surgery using a laparoscopic robot to hold the camera (a first in Spain and among the world's firsts)
Company founding      — Founded ROVISEC (robot+vision) for industry collaboration; later sold the company through a peseta banknote inspection business
1999                  — Attended the Mission de France-led Japan-Europe robotics meeting; discussed launching EURON (driven by Henrik Christensen)
2005                  — IEEE ICRA General Chair
Afterwards            — Joined IEEE RAS AdCom and served as VP; founded the Spanish Robotics Chapter
2012                  — Founded a surgical robotics spin-off; pursued commercialization of teleoperation system through Mayo Clinic collaboration
Afterwards            — Collaborations on neurorehabilitation (exoskeletons, electrical stimulation, brain-machine interfaces) with CSIC, Hospital de Tetrapléjicos de Toledo, and others
January 23, 2015      — IEEE oral history interview conducted in College Station, Texas (Professor at UPC, Institute of Bioengineering)
```
