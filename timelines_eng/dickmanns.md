# Ernst Dickmanns — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/dickmanns.md`)

```
1936-01-04          — Born in Newcastle, near Cologne, Germany
~1947               — Primary education in the village of Lutztoph, where his father taught
1947                — Entered the Gymnasium in Porz (9 years)
Age 16~17           — First learned differential equations and was captivated
1956                — After Gymnasium, started practical work to enter aerospace (Dornier Aircraft Engineering, near Munich)
1956                — Additionally did metallurgy training in Kulstoph; began aerospace engineering studies at RWTH Aachen
1961                — Earned the Aachen Diploma (engineering degree); started aerospace research at DFVLR (Germany's NASA equivalent) in Mülheim
1962~1971           — Aerospace research in earnest
1963                — Moved to Oberpfaffenhofen
1964~65             — Control engineering and aerospace applications research at Princeton University on a NASA fellowship
1969                — PhD from Aachen Tech (re-entry trajectory optimization)
1971~72             — Postdoc at NASA Marshall Space Flight Center (Huntsville, AL) on shuttle orbiter re-entry research
1972                — Visited Bundeswehr Federal Armed Forces University; received a faculty offer
1974~75             — Led the launch and orbital insertion of Symphony, the first European communications satellite, with the DFVLR group
1975-01-01          — Became acting head of the DLR Oberpfaffenhofen institute (overseeing about 700 people)
1975-10             — Joined Universität der Bundeswehr München as professor
1977                — First doctoral dissertation began (the start of the vision systems development line); pole-balancing research
Around 1980         — Vision experiments with an air-cushion vehicle for satellite docking
1981                — First international conference demonstration of pole-balancing on 4 Intel 8085s
1986                — Began collaboration with Daimler-Benz (Daimler-Benz centennial of the automobile)
1987                — First Autobahn autonomous drive of the VaMoRs vehicle (96 km/h, 8086/2MHz, transputers)
1987                — Presented Kalman filter–based dynamic vision at the Santa Cruz NATO Advanced Study Institute (skeptical reception → reversed by next-day demonstration)
1987~1988           — Began collaboration with the US Army on negative obstacle detection
1987~1994           — PROMETHEUS European project (7 years, in collaboration with Daimler)
1991                — Runway recognition flight experiments at Brunswick
Around 1991~92      — Developed a 30-liter full-video-rate stereo interpretation system
1992                — Began autonomous driving on German Autobahns and other public roads with VaMoRs (with special permission)
1993-03             — Free-floating object grasping remote control experiment inside the Columbia D2 Spacelab box on the space shuttle
1994-10             — Public autonomous demo of two Daimler S500 vehicles near Charles-de-Gaulle Airport, Paris (130 km/h, autonomous lane changes)
1995                — Pivoted vision development from civilian to military applications; strengthened collaboration with US NIST and the Army
1995~96             — European Cleopatra project; 1600 km autonomous drive from Munich to Odense (Denmark) (top speed 180 km/h)
1998                — Visiting professor at Caltech
1990s~early 2000s   — Off-road autonomous driving; EMS-Vision (MarVEye multi-focal saccadic vision) development
2001                — Retired from Universität der Bundeswehr München
2010-06-21          — IEEE oral history interview in Hofolding, Germany
```
