# Bob Bolles — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/bolles.md`)

```
1945                  — Born in Baltimore, Maryland
Around 1947           — Moved to Gainesville, Florida at age two and a half; grew up there (father was a music professor at the University of Florida)
1966                  — During Gainesville high school years, interned with Prof. Ralph Selfridge at the University of Florida; first programming experience using Fortran II
Undergrad years       — Math major at Yale (chose math since there was no CS department); part-time programming work related to Educational Testing
Early grad school     — MS in Electrical Engineering at the University of Pennsylvania; during PhD studies, decided to enlist during the Vietnam War era
Navy service          — Spent about four years teaching computer science at the Naval Postgraduate School (Monterey); interacted with Gary Kildall
PhD studies           — Moved to Stanford under Jerry Feldman; collaborated with Lou Paul on perception for automated assembly of the Ford Model T water pump
1976                  — Joined SRI International after Stanford (degree formally awarded in 1977)
After 1976            — Worked on industrial vision projects at SRI (Industrial Affiliates Program); developed the Local Feature Focus method
1981                  — Presented at the first ISRR (International Symposium of Robotics Research, Bretton Woods)
Early 1980s           — Developed the RANSAC algorithm with Marty Fischler; published in ACM magazine
Mid-1980s             — Stereo vision project on the Autonomous Land Vehicle (Martin Marietta); collaborated with Keith Nishihara and JPL
About 20 years ago    — Developed epipolar-plane image analysis with Harlan Baker (early structure-from-motion work)
Late 1990s            — Participated in SRI's Centibots project (100 robots exploring a building); also connected to Kurt Konolige's Flakey robot
ISRR 4                — Hosted the 4th ISRR at UC Santa Cruz, co-hosted with Bernie Roth
Three years           — Editorial board member of the International Journal of Computer Vision (invited by Takeo Kanade)
Mid-2000s             — DARPA LAGR program; developed visual odometry with Mooly Eden (Modey Agerwald)
2~3 years ago (2007–8) — Ported airborne video surveillance algorithms for the Predator UAV under a Raytheon subcontract
Around 2010 (two months prior) — Started DARPA ARM-S/ARM-H project, collaborating with Columbia (Peter Allen) and CMU (Matt Mason, Sid Srinivasa)
November 18, 2010     — IEEE oral history interview conducted in Menlo Park (then program director at SRI's AI Center)
```
