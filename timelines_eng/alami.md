# Rachid Alami — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/alami.md`)

```
1954           — Born in Morocco
1974           — Studied in Morocco until age 20, then moved to Toulouse, France
1975           — Entered ENSEEIHT engineering school in Toulouse; inspired by Prof. Henry Farreny's A.I. course
1978           — Earned BS (equivalent to MS) in Computer Engineering from ENSEEIHT; returned to Morocco
1978–1980      — Worked as a computer engineer at Morocco's Ministry of Equipment and Dams; developed flood simulation models
1980           — Returned to Toulouse for research; joined Georges Giralt's group at LAAS
Early 1980s    — Participated in the French national robotics program ARA (Automation and Advanced Robotics); built his own Lisp interpreter
Early 1980s    — Developed NNS (No Name System), a high-level control system for a Flexible Assembly Cell
1983           — Earned PhD in Robotics from Paul Sabatier University
1984           — Appointed full-time CNRS researcher
1984           — Presented NNS at his first ICRA (Atlanta)
1985–1986      — Visited major U.S. labs including MIT, CMU, Stanford, U. Penn., SRI, Berkeley
1986–1992      — Participated in the French Mars rover project RIISP (in collaboration with CNES)
Around 1986    — Together with colleague Raja Chatila, shifted to autonomy research using the Hilare mobile robot
1992           — Started the multi-robot cooperation project MARTHA (lead PI on the LAAS side); introduced the plan-merging concept
1999           — Co-organized the 1st Workshop on Human-Friendly Robotics at LAAS Toulouse (CNRS-NSF, with John Canny)
After 1999     — Led the French national project HR+ (Human-Robot Plus)
Mid-2000s      — Coordinator of the European FET project COGNIRON (Toulouse)
Late 2000s     — Completed the European project PHRIENDS (physical human-robot interaction)
2011           — At time of interview: Director of Research in Robotics & A.I. at LAAS-CNRS
```
