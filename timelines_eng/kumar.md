# Vijay Kumar — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kumar.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Patna, Bihar, India; grew up in Central India and New Delhi
Undergrad             — BS in Mechanical Engineering at IIT Kanpur
Age 21                — Decided to study in the US; entered PhD program at Ohio State University
MS                    — MS in kinematics under Ken Hunt (Australian) at Ohio State
PhD years             — Under Kenneth Waldron's supervision, participated in the 6-legged Adaptive Suspension Vehicle (ASV) project (~$5M, ~1.6-ton vehicle, gait and load distribution research)
1987                  — PhD in Mechanical Engineering from Ohio State
1987                  — Appointed faculty at University of Pennsylvania (influenced by Richard "Lou" Paul, Burton Paul, and Ruzena Bajcsy)
Late 1980s~early '90s — Collaborated on Bajcsy's DARPA project; researched two-robot cooperative object transport and multi-finger manipulation
1991                  — NSF Presidential Young Investigator Award (turning point in decision to remain in academia)
1990s                 — Walking chair (legged chair) project for the disabled (commercialization explored with Johnson & Johnson and others, but not realized)
Late '90s~early 2000s — Researched mobile manipulators and multi-mobile-robot cooperative exploration and mapping
Mid-2000s onward      — Expanded to multi-aerial-robot (UAV) cooperation research; collaboration with Stephen Pratt (Arizona State) on ant behavior research
2003                  — ASME Fellow
2008                  — PhD advisee won ICRA Best Paper Award
Summer 2011           — Started autonomous flying robot project for Fukushima collapsed buildings with Tohoku University (NSF-funded)
2011                  — ETHW oral history interview (Philadelphia, PA), during his time as GRASP Lab Director
2012                  — World Technology Network Award; served as White House OSTP Assistant Director (Robotics & CPS) 2012~2014
2013                  — Popular Science Breakthrough Award; National Academy of Engineering member
2014                  — Engelberger Robotics Award
2017                  — IEEE RAS George Saridis Leadership Award; ASME Robert E. Abbott Award
Later                 — Founded autonomous flight solutions company Exyn Technologies; appointed Dean of Penn Engineering
```
