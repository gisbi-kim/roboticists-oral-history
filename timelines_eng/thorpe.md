# Chuck Thorpe — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/thorpe.md`)

```
Birth              — Born in Michigan, USA
Childhood          — Moved through Michigan/Illinois/Texas/Delaware/Belgium following his physician father
Childhood~youth    — Lived 7 years in Congo (Zaire) (his father ran a hospital)
College years      — BA in natural sciences at North Park College (Chicago); switched from pre-med to computers
Year after college — High school teacher in Congo (8 years total in Congo)
1979. 8.           — Entered Carnegie Mellon University CS PhD program
1979               — Joined Raj Reddy as advisor with the launch of the Robotics Institute (one of the first RI grad students along with Lee Weiss and Jim Crowley)
Early PhD          — Collaborated with Hans Moravec, improved Stanford mobile robot vision system (15 min → 30 sec movement cycle)
1984. 8.           — Submitted Autonomous Land Vehicle proposal
1984. 9.           — Defended PhD; immediately notified by Raj Reddy of ALV meeting, started postdoc
1986               — NavLab 1 (Chevy van) became fully operational; son Leland born the same year
1990               — Dean Pomerleau drove ALVINN neural network on Pennsylvania Turnpike (NavLab 2, Humvee)
Mid-1990s          — Developed RALPH system with NavLab 3 (Dean's Honda Accord)
1995               — "No Hands Across America" drive from D.C. to San Diego with NavLab 5 (Pontiac minivan) (98.5% autonomous)
1997               — San Diego I-15 Automated Highway System demo (NavLab 6~10), Secretary of Transportation rode along; AHS ended
After 1997         — DOT-funded research on single-vehicle lane departure collision avoidance systems
Later              — Dean Pomerleau spun off AssistWare → sold to Cognex → then Takata
2004~2010          — Founding dean of Carnegie Mellon Qatar campus
Around 2009        — ALV project 25th anniversary event (Captain → General Rick Lynch attended)
2010. 11. 22.      — Interview with Peter Asaro/Selma Šabanović conducted in Pittsburgh
At time of interview — Senior Vice President and Provost at Clarkson University, supervised 17 PhDs; son Leland working at Pomerleau's company after MS in robotics from CMU
```
