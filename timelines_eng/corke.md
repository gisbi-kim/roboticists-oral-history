# Peter Corke — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/corke.md`)

```
August 24, 1959       — Born in Melbourne, Australia
Childhood             — Attended school in Melbourne, then moved to rural Castlemaine where he attended high school
Childhood             — Took apart radios and TVs; sure of an electronics engineering career from a young age
1980                  — BS in Electrical Engineering from the University of Melbourne
Early grad school     — Research assistant on his advisor's funding, then taught (digital systems and control theory)
1984                  — Joined CSIRO (Australia's national applied R&D agency)
1986                  — MS in Electrical Engineering from the University of Melbourne (control theory)
1988                  — Nine-month fellowship at the University of Pennsylvania GRASP Lab (Lou Paul, Ruzena Bajcsy, Vijay Kumar)
After returning home  — Began part-time PhD with CSIRO sponsorship (advised by Malcolm Good)
Late 1980s            — Worked on high-speed video processing hardware ("blob feature" boards), applied to food sorting and traffic monitoring
PhD thesis            — Vision-based control (visual servoing); modeling integration of camera, image processing, and robot using a Unimate PUMA
Around 1994           — Co-organized the ICRA Visual Servoing workshop with Greg Hager (GRASP colleague), including Seth Hutchinson
1995                  — Moved to Brisbane and started CSIRO mining automation activity (starting with two people)
1996                  — Hutchinson, Hager, and Corke published their visual servoing IEEE Transactions tutorial paper (his most cited paper)
1995–2005             — Automation of 3,000–4,000-ton dragline excavators and underground tunnel vehicles (LHDs); licensed to Caterpillar (MINEGEM)
1999                  — Organized the ISER meeting in Sydney; met Daniela Rus
1999                  — Sabbatical at the University of Illinois with Seth Hutchinson (also collaborated with Mark Spong)
Around 2004           — Daniela Rus offered six wireless sensor network nodes, sparking environmental monitoring collaboration
Sensor network era    — Built their own nodes; applied to rainforests, underwater, sugarcane fields, and cattle (livestock) tracking; virtual fencing experiments
Collaboration with USC — Helicopter-based sensor network deployment experiments with Daniela Rus and Gaurav Sukhatme
Around 2005           — Expanded into flying robots and underwater robots for environmental monitoring (targeting Great Barrier Reef surveys)
2008                  — Left CSIRO (lab then numbered 40 people); the research group reached 25 years tenure as he turned 50
2009                  — Sabbatical-style stays in Paris and Oxford; began writing the Visual Control book
January 2010          — Joined Queensland University of Technology (QUT) as Professor of Robotics and Control
2009                  — Joined IEEE RAS AdCom; took over as Editor-in-Chief of the IEEE RAS Magazine (succeeding Stefano Stramigioli)
2010–2013             — Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Robotics & Automation Magazine; introduced electronic distribution
Around 2011 book release — Published Robotics, Vision and Control (about 600 pages)
November 4, 2013      — IEEE oral history interview in Tokyo (his ADCOM term ending that year); preparing an online robotics course in MOOC form
```
