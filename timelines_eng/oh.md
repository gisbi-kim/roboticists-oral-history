# Jun Ho Oh — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/oh.md`)

```
October 3, 1954     — Born in Seoul, Republic of Korea
Childhood           — Read encyclopedias 10–20 times out of curiosity, built radios, walkie-talkies, and audio gear himself
1977                — BS in Mechanical Engineering from Yonsei University
1979                — MS in Mechanical Engineering from Yonsei University
After 1979          — Worked about 2.5 years at the Korea Energy Research Institute
Study abroad        — Entered PhD program at UC Berkeley (advisor Masayoshi Tomizuka)
1985                — PhD from UC Berkeley (dissertation on adaptive control of milling machines)
Around 1985         — Returned to Korea, joined KAIST as a faculty member
Late 1980s–early 1990s — As a hobby, built his own RC helicopter simulator and published a paper on camera-based hover autocontrol (early paper in helicopter hovering)
2002                — Began building his first humanoid robot with $50,000 of his own money
2002                — Demonstrated first humanoid to KAIST colleagues, secured internal funding, built second robot
2003                — Secured about $500,000 in government funding, began full-scale humanoid research
2004                — After KHR-1 and others, officially announced HUBO; started identifying as an IEEE/roboticist
After 2004          — Continued HUBO series development with Frontier Initiative program support (~$500K/year)
Afterwards          — Delivered HUBO units to 7 Drexel-and-other US sites, 2 in Singapore, plus Chinese and Korean institutions (12–16 units total at $400K each)
Afterwards          — Spun off Rainbow Co. through KAIST licensing (KAIST holds 20% equity)
May 15, 2012        — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro and Selma Šabanović (St. Paul, MN, USA)
2015                — Won the DARPA Robotics Challenge (Team KAIST)
```
