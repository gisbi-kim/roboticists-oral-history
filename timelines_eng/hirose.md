# Shigeo Hirose — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/hirose.md`)

```
1947                — Born in Tokyo (postwar)
Childhood           — Interest in making things; built a non-electronic alarm device to control the gas of the family's Japanese-style bath
High school years   — Started building radios; made small robot toys
Undergrad years     — Mechanical engineering at Yokohama National University; read Masahiro Mori's book "Robot" (NHK Publishing) and decided to pursue robotics research
1971                — Graduated from Yokohama National University with B.S. (first-class honors); failed University of Tokyo entrance exam, accepted at Tokyo Tech → joined Prof. Umetani's lab
June, 1st year of MS — Bought a real snake from a snake shop and began biomechanics experiments on snake locomotion (continued buying snakes every spring for 4 years)
2nd year of MS      — Built first snake robot — very successful, decided to continue robotics research
1972                — Completed first snake robot (per his own recollection; later exhibited at the Computer History Museum)
1976                — Earned PhD at Tokyo Tech (snake biomechanics and snake robot topic); started as Research Associate / Assistant Professor
1976                — First quadruped (spider robot) attempt — failed
1978                — Built quadruped with tactile sensors, succeeded in stair walking
1979                — Associate Professor, Tokyo Tech
1981 or 1982        — Visited Ohio State University at Bob McGee's invitation, met Marc Raibert (hopping machine) and others
Mid-to-late 1980s   — Presented energy-efficient legs at the ASME LA conference; pantograph mechanism → adopted in OSU's Adaptive Suspension Vehicle
1992                — Full Professor, Tokyo Tech
1997                — Published a paper on a quadruped demining robot
Late 1990s          — Designed Titan VIII research quadruped platform at Prof. Inoue's request (1.5 million yen, about 80 units sold)
Around 2000         — Developed Titan XI, a 7-ton quadruped for steep slopes, commissioned by a construction company
After 2001          — Visited Afghanistan via Japanese government reconstruction support → instead of a walking machine, built the Gryphon demining robot based on a buggy vehicle
2002                — JCO nuclear accident response robot — co-developed with Mitsubishi Heavy Industry
2003                — Japanese government discontinued nuclear reactor robot support → published a critical paper in a robotics journal
2004                — Co-founded HiBot Corporation (with students)
2002                — Honorary Professor, Shengyang Institute of Technology (China)
2003                — IEEE Fellow
2006                — Japanese government Medal with Purple Ribbon
2009                — Joseph Engelberger Robotics Award (RIA)
2011                — Distinguished Professor appointment; interviewed by Selma Šabanović in Flagstaff (August 28, 2011)
2013                — Professor Emeritus, Tokyo Tech; subsequently HiBot CEO & CTO
2014                — IEEE Robotics and Automation Award
Ongoing work        — Helios series (operating Helios IX, designing Helios X) — mobile robots for nuclear decommissioning; Kansai Electric Power's high-voltage line inspection robot (5 years of development) — won Robot Grand Prix
Since 1997          — Founded and ran the JSME Robot Grand Prix
```
