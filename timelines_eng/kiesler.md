# Sara Kiesler — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kiesler.md`)

```
Birth           — Born in Washington, D.C., USA; grew up there
                 — Father: SEC government official; Mother: biologist
Undergrad       — Psychology at Simmons College in Boston (specialized in social psychology)
1963            — M.A. in Psychology, Stanford University
                 — When advisor Leon Festinger moved, she married and relocated to Ohio State
1965            — PhD in Psychology, Ohio State University (degree from OSU but dissertation written at Yale on the topic of "gratitude")
                 — Second advisor: Timothy Brock
After PhD       — Appointed Professor in the Social Sciences department at Carnegie Mellon University
1980s           — Amid government crackdowns on "social science research," shifted to computer-mediated communication research
                 — Group decision-making experiments via email/chat — discovered the "flaming" phenomenon
                 — Pioneered the field of computer-mediated group interaction
1998            — Moved to California; joined Interval Research Company (peak of the dot-com era)
                 — Saw her first homemade robot here
Around 1999     — Sensing Interval's troubles, looked for the next position
After return    — Re-hired by the CMU School of Computer Science
After CMU return — Joined Sebastian Thrun's Nursebot Project (Pearl robot)
                 — Participated in writing the 5-year NSF ITR program proposal
Around 2001     — Conducted exercise-encouragement experiments with the elderly with undergraduate Jennifer Goetz
                 — Experimented with toy robots using the Wizard-of-Oz technique
First Ro-Man presentation — Won Best Paper Award for research on robot personality and task fit
                 — Yoga vs Jelly Bean task experiment (joint with Goetz)
Subsequently    — Won a multimillion-dollar project on cognitive and social design with Jodi Forlizzi
                 — Modular redesign project for Pearl's head
                 — Advised students such as Aaron Powers (lab manager), Cristen Torrey, Carl DiSalvo, Bilge Mutlu
                 — Collaborated with Pamela Hinds (Stanford)
                 — Co-edited the HCI Journal special issue on human-robot interaction
HRI Conference  — Helped found the first HRI Conference with Alan Schultz, Greg Trafton, Cynthia Breazeal, and others
                 — Held a graduate student workshop in Carmel with Pamela Hinds
2002            — Elected to the CHI Academy
2009            — Received the Lifetime Achievement Award
?               — Elected ACM Fellow
At time of interview — Hillman Professor of CS and HCI at CMU; launching the HRI Journal; SnackBot and HERB projects underway
2013            — Received the Allan Newell Award for Research Excellence
2015            — Received the International Communication Association Williams Prize
```
