# Howie Choset — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/choset.md`)

```
Birth/upbringing      — Born in New York; grew up on Long Island
Middle school (7th–8th grade) — Built electronically controlled vehicles using shoe-box chassis and motorized wheels
High school years     — Attended John F. Kennedy High School; advanced to the national finals of the Duracell Battery competition (sonar-avoidance robot)
1990                  — Double bachelor's at the University of Pennsylvania: Computer Science and Engineering (Moore School) and Wharton (entrepreneurial management)
Undergrad years       — Worked on robot vision in Ruzena Bajcsy's GRASP Lab (learned programming and writing from Helen Anderson)
1991                  — MS in Mechanical Engineering at Caltech (switched majors: CS to ME)
Grad school years     — Advised by Joel Burdick; developed sensor-based planners on Greg Chirikjian's snake robot
1993                  — Joint work on snake robot local planning with Japanese researcher Nobuaki Takanashi (NSF Japan summer program)
March 3, 1996         — PhD in Mechanical Engineering from Caltech ("Hierarchical Generalized Voronoi Graph")
Right after graduation — Computational geometry work at a second Japanese lab; coverage algorithms at Nomadic Technologies
1996                  — Joined Carnegie Mellon University as faculty
1998–2000             — Postdoc Keiji Nagatani (Tohoku); research on topological localization
Afterwards            — Developed medical snake robots: collaborations with Alon Wolf (postdoc, now at Technion) and cardiac surgeon Marco Zenati (Pittsburgh→Harvard)
About 2004            — Co-founded Medrobotics with backing from the Pittsburgh Life Science Greenhouse (Jim Jordan)
Afterwards            — Brought in a CEO from Boston Scientific; raised $12M in investment
Afterwards            — Successful first human cardiac diagnostic surgery (patient discharged the next day)
Around 2014           — Medrobotics obtained European CE marking; started use in throat surgery
Research areas        — Used modular snake robots to explore Egyptian caves and pyramids and Turkish burial mounds
Collaboration         — NRI funding with Russ Taylor (JHU) and Nabil Simaan (Vanderbilt) on concurrent situational awareness
Collaboration         — Snake locomotion research with Dan Goldman (Georgia Tech), Science publication forthcoming (two weeks after the interview)
September 15, 2014    — IEEE oral history interview conducted in Chicago (CMU Robotics professor; director of the undergraduate robotics minor)
Afterwards            — Co-founded a second company for modular robot actuators
```
