# Toshio Fukuda — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/fukuda.md`)

```
1948-12-12          — Born in Toyama, Japan
Primary/secondary    — Completed primary, middle, and high school education in Japan
Undergrad           — Mechanical engineering at Waseda University; studied under Professors Tsuchiya and Kato (biomechanics and biped walking robots)
Undergrad years     — Spent a year on circulatory systems for organ preservation and fluidic systems
1971                — BE from Waseda University
1973                — MS from the University of Tokyo
1973~1977           — Research at Yale University (New Haven, CT) on advanced adaptive control and related topics
1977                — DSc from the University of Tokyo
1977~1979           — Research Scientist at the Japanese government's National Mechanical Engineering Laboratory (AIST), Tsukuba
1979~1980           — Visiting Research Fellow at the University of Stuttgart (West Germany)
1981~1989           — Associate professor at Science University of Tokyo; began full-scale robotics research
1983~1984           — Began research on the first inspection & maintenance robots (for hostile environments such as oil refinery tanks)
Mid-1980s           — Organized the Factory Inspection & Maintenance Robots technical committee within the Robotics Society of Japan
1986~1987           — Began micromanipulator development (inspired by observing microorganisms in a pond)
1980s               — Modular robotic line collaborations with industrial firms such as Denso
1988                — Founding General Chair of IEEE IROS (International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems) in Tokyo
1989~2013           — Professor of Mechanical Engineering at Nagoya University
1990~2011           — Founding Chair of MHS (Int'l Symp. on Micro-Nano Mechatronics and Human Science)
1990s               — Theorized the Cellular Robotic System; researched its combination with neural networks
1994                — Published the Cellular Robotic System book
1995                — Elected IEEE Fellow
1997                — Received the IEEE Eugene Mittelmann Achievement Award
1998~1999           — President of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society
2000                — IEEE Third Millennium Medal
2001~2002           — IEEE Division X Director
2002~2005           — Founding President of the IEEE Nanotechnology Council
2004                — IEEE RAS Pioneer Award
2005                — Founding Chair of the IEEE ARSO (Advanced Robotics Technology and Social Impacts) Workshop
2000s               — Medical robotics (intravascular surgery) research; developed an MRI/CT-based human vascular simulator
Late 2000s          — Founded a venture spin-off to commercialize the human simulator (operating 6~7 years at the time of interview)
2008                — Founding Chair of the IEEE SII (System Integration International) Workshop
2009                — IEEE RAS George Saridis Leadership Award
2010                — IEEE Technical Field Award on Robotics and Automation
2011                — IEEE/RSJ IROS Harashima Award
2011-09-25          — IEEE oral history interview in San Francisco
2013                — After retiring from Nagoya University, joined Meijo University and Beijing Institute of Technology as professor
2013~2014           — IEEE Region 10 Director
2015                — Received the Medal of Honor with Purple Ribbon from the Japanese government
2017                — Elected foreign member of the Chinese Academy of Sciences
2017~2018           — IEEE Division X Director (second term)
2018-10-05          — Announced as 2019 IEEE President-Elect
2020-01-01          — Scheduled to take office as IEEE President
```
