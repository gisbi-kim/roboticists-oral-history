# Joel Burdick — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/burdick.md`)

```
May 22, 1959          — Born in Rahway, New Jersey
Childhood–high school — Family moved frequently; attended seven schools along the East Coast
Undergrad             — Duke University; started in chemistry but switched to mechanical engineering, double-majored in chemistry and ME
1982                  — Entered Stanford graduate school (originally aiming for fluid mechanics)
Early grad school     — Worked on force control with Oussama Khatib (then a postdoc), communicating through Pascal code
Mid grad school       — Switched first advisor from Tom Binford to Bernie Roth; worked on redundant manipulators
Grad school days      — Built part of the first JPL Stanford Hand electronic interface before Ron Fearing took over
Early 1988            — PhD from Stanford
1988                  — Joined Caltech as Assistant Professor in Mechanical Engineering (sparked by John Craig's recommendation)
Early in faculty career — Built a 30-DOF hyper-redundant snake-like robot with first PhD student Gregory Chirikjian
Late 1980s            — Began collaborating with JPL (Carl Ruoff, Samad Hayati, Homayoun Seraji, and others)
Late 1980s–1990       — Robotics research funded by NSF and ONR
Early 1990s           — Lean times for robotics funding; diversified to NIH, NASA, and DOE
Mid-1990s             — Sabbatical at Inria (Sophia Antipolis) with Jean-Daniel Boissonnat; co-authored with Subir Ghosh
1990s                 — Hopping robot collaboration with Paolo Fiorini (JPL); sabbatical in Verona
Japan collaboration   — Nobuaki Takahashi and Shuuji Kajita (MEL/AIST) visited for 1–1.5 years on Japanese government fellowships
About 12 years ago (~1999) — Called in by Caltech's Richard Andersen to start work on monkey brain–robot arm neural prosthetics
About 8 years ago (~2003) — Pivoted to spinal cord injury research; joint work with UCLA and University of Louisville
At time of interview (2011) — Published first clinical results from human spinal cord injury study
At time of interview  — One of six teams in the DARPA ARM program; partnered with JPL on the manipulation challenge
At time of interview  — Denso-commissioned ultra-wideband radar for pedestrian detection led to a Korean military DMZ sensor project
June 6, 2011          — IEEE oral history interview conducted at Caltech in Pasadena (then R.L. & D.M. Hayman Professor)
```
