# Shigeki Sugano — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/sugano.md`)

```
1958. 9. 29.       — Born in Tokyo, Japan; grew up in the Tokyo/Chiba area
Middle school years — Interested in cutting-edge tech such as robots, rockets, and computers; fascinated by professors Ichiro Kato and Masahiro Mori
1977 (1st year undergrad) — After entering Waseda University, visited Kato Laboratory with a friend
Undergrad years    — Joined Kato Laboratory, researched prosthetic limbs (below-knee prosthesis)
1981               — Earned BS in mechanical engineering at Waseda
Start of MS program — Joined the new WABOT-2 (piano-playing robot) project, introduced 16-bit microcomputer
1983               — Earned MS in mechanical engineering at Waseda
1985               — At Tsukuba Exposition, WABOT-2 performed daily electronic organ demonstrations for 6 months
1987~1991          — Research associate at Waseda University
1989               — Earned PhD in mechanical engineering at Waseda (topic: piano-playing robot)
1991               — Appointed regular faculty in Waseda's mechanical engineering department
Just before PhD~Stanford — Started new "kokoro (mind)" robot project with Professor Kato
1993~1994          — Visiting researcher at Stanford University Mechanical Engineering, discussions with Bernie Roth and Oussama Khatib
During Stanford stay — Mentor Professor Ichiro Kato passed away
After about 1994   — Began full-scale work on TWENDY-ONE and other human-symbiotic robots and the WAMOEBA project
2000               — Member of Waseda Humanoid Robotics Institute
2000~2007          — Developed TWENDY-ONE system over 7 years through confidential industry collaboration
2001~2012          — Director of Waseda WABOT-HOUSE Lab
2007. 11.          — TWENDY-ONE system press release; visited Willow Garage 2 days later
2008               — Received IEEE RAS Distinguished Award
2011               — Appointed Vice Dean of Waseda Creative Science and Engineering
2012               — Appointed Director of Chubu-Area Industries Techno-Innovation Institute
2012               — Received RSJ Distinguished Service Award
2013               — Coordinator of MEXT Leading Graduate Program (Waseda Embodiment Informatics)
2014. 9. 15.       — Interview with Peter Asaro conducted in Chicago (age 55 at the time)
At time of interview — Engaged in robot collaboration project related to the Fukushima nuclear accident
```
