# Masaru Uchiyama — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/uchiyama.md`)

```
1949. 9. 16.       — Born in a small village in Fukuoka Prefecture, Japan
Childhood~youth    — Grew up in Fukuoka; commuted to middle and high school in Kurume city (30~40 min by bus and train)
Middle/high school years — Interested in building model airplanes and the like, decided to pursue engineering
Around 1967        — Entered University of Tokyo at age 18, majoring in mechanical engineering
1972               — BS in mechanical engineering at University of Tokyo
1972~1973          — Joined automatic control lab (advisor Sumiji Fujii) for MS program, became interested in computer-controlled mechanical arms
MS years           — Designed a 3-DOF nail-hammering robot, used Hitachi minicomputer
1974               — MS in mechanical engineering at University of Tokyo
1974~1977          — PhD program, dynamic control and analysis of manipulator kinematic singularities
1977               — Earned PhD at University of Tokyo
1977               — Research Associate in Department of Precision Engineering at Tohoku University (Sendai), joined Hakomori lab
Early research     — Briefly stepped away from robot research, studied unsteady flow measurement using Kalman Filter
1980               — Promoted to Associate Professor, able to advise graduate students
Around 1980        — Resumed robot research timed with Japan's robotics renaissance; researched flexible robot systems and dynamic sensing
Early 1980s        — Visiting researcher at UC Santa Barbara Research Institute
Late 1980s         — Joined NASDA space robot research committee at the recommendation of Tokyo Tech's Professor Umetani
1992               — Appointed Professor in Space Machines Laboratory, Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics
1990s              — Researched ETS-7 satellite teleoperation (using haptic master arm)
Around 2005        — Demonstrated HRP2 humanoid playing drums at Aichi Expo (#1 in children's vote)
After around 2005  — Associate Professor Konno and others jointly researched KAWADA HRP2 humanoid
Later              — Expanded to UAV (unmanned aerial vehicle) research
2011               — Elected IEEE Fellow
2013. 11. 3.       — Interview with Peter Asaro conducted in Tokyo
2015               — Elected IEEE Life Fellow
Career totals      — Supervised about 100 MS students and over 30 PhDs (more than half went into academia)
```
