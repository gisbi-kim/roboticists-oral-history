# Allison Okamura — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/okamura.md`)

```
Birth               — Born in Fontana, California, USA; raised in Riverside
Undergrad years     — Entered UC Berkeley Mechanical Engineering; did no robotics as an undergrad (research assistant in CAD education)
1994                — BS in Mechanical Engineering from UC Berkeley
Entering grad school — Entered Stanford Mechanical Engineering, joined Mark Cutkosky's lab
Early PhD           — First robotics project: dexterous manipulation at the fingertips of a two-fingered robot hand
1996                — MS in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford
During PhD          — Course project with the Phantom haptic device; became fascinated by Sensable's devices
During PhD          — Worked part-time at Immersion in Silicon Valley designing and analyzing haptic devices
During PhD          — Vibration-feedback collaboration with Robert Howe (Harvard) and Jack Dennerlein (first journal paper)
2000                — PhD in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford
2000                — Joined Johns Hopkins University as assistant professor in Mechanical Engineering
Early Hopkins       — Joined Russ Taylor's Computer Integrated Surgical Systems ERC, began haptics research on the da Vinci Surgical System
Hopkins era         — Cooperative manipulation (steady-hand) robot research with Greg Hager (CS), formalized the Virtual Fixtures concept
Hopkins era         — Major steerable-needle collaboration with Bob Webster, Greg Chirikjian, Noah Cowan, Ken Goldberg, and others (multi-year NIH support)
2004                — NSF CAREER Award
2005                — IEEE RAS Early Career Award
Sabbatical          — Sabbatical in Amy Bastian's neuroscience lab (stayed at Hopkins); entered rehabilitation robotics and neuroscience
2009                — IEEE Technical Committee on Haptics Early Career Award
Late Hopkins        — Promoted to associate then full professor; served as Vice Chair of Mechanical Engineering (11 years total at Hopkins)
2011                — Husband denied tenure at Hopkins; moved family to California, joined Stanford Mechanical Engineering as professor
Stanford era        — Large NRI projects with Pieter Abbeel, Ken Goldberg (Berkeley) and others; continued research on prosthetics, BMI, and needle steering
September 16, 2014  — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro (Chicago)
2015                — Began as Editor-in-Chief of IEEE ICRA (3-year term)
```
