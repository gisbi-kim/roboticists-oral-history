# Bob McGhee — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/mcghee.md`)

```
1929              — Born in Detroit, Michigan, USA
Childhood         — Grew up around Detroit and the Toledo, Ohio area
1952-02           — BS from the University of Michigan (Ann Arbor); married at the same time
1952              — Commissioned as a U.S. Army Ordnance Corps officer (guided missile maintenance officer)
1952–1955         — Army service; developed interest in ballistic missiles and inertial navigation
1955              — Joined Hughes Aircraft Company (technical staff, 1955–1963)
1957              — MS in electrical engineering from the University of Southern California
1963              — PhD in electrical engineering from USC
1963              — Appointed assistant professor in USC's electrical engineering department (1963–1967)
1964              — Professor Rajko Tomović of the University of Belgrade visited USC; collaboration began on electronic lower-limb prostheses
1966              — Built the "Phony Pony" with George Bekey and graduate student Andrew Frank (the world's first electronically coordinated walking vehicle)
1967–1968         — Promoted to associate professor at USC
1968              — Moved to Ohio State University as a full professor of electrical engineering
Early 1970s       — Began developing the Bionic Bug (NSF-funded, 6-legged, 300-pound walker)
1974              — Visited the Okhotsimski lab at Moscow State University in the Soviet Union
1976              — Second Soviet visit; adopted a common configuration for walkers
Around 1980       — Real-time control of the Bionic Bug with a PDP-11/70 and 1MB memory
Early 1980s       — Began the DARPA Adaptive Suspension Vehicle (ASV) project (collaboration with Kenneth Waldron)
Mid-to-late 1980s — Completed the ASV (7,000-pound 6-legged walker); tested at Ohio State and in the Virginia Piedmont region
1986              — Moved to the Naval Postgraduate School (NPS) as a full professor of computer science
Around 1986–1990  — Ended walking robot research; shifted to unmanned underwater vehicle research
Around 1988       — Sehung Kwak's Prolog-based free gait paper (published in Advanced Robotics)
Late 1990s        — Operated a trilingual (C++/LISP/Prolog) unmanned underwater vehicle system in Monterey Bay
Around 1992       — Joint Aqua robot research with Japan's Porton Harbor Research Institute
2005–2006         — Retired from NPS
After retirement  — As emeritus professor, volunteered on inertial navigation technology for human foot tracking
2010-11-19        — IEEE History Center oral history interview (Monterey, CA)
```
