# Ayanna Howard — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/howard.md`)

```
Childhood/teens         — Grew up in Pasadena, California
6th grade               — Inspired by Bionic Woman and other sci-fi, became interested in robots; initially aspired to be a doctor → switched to engineering due to dislike of biology dissection
Undergrad               — B.S. in Computer Engineering, Brown University
Sophomore year          — Object contour tracking and recognition with a robotic manipulator (advisor: Wolovich)
Summer 1994             — First NASA JPL internship (right after freshman year)
Graduate school         — Entered PhD program at University of Southern California (USC); advised by Homayoun Seraji; Fortran→C conversion and robotic manipulation research
USC PhD program         — First advisor was Ken Goldberg, then George Bekey advised the PhD after he left
Dissertation            — Robot manipulator for sorting hospital medical waste (visually and deformation-detect HIV needles, fabric, etc., then grasp with two-handed gripper)
1999                    — Transitioned to full-time JPL position (40 hours)
JPL years               — Mars exploration projects, terrain assessment and navigation algorithms (vision + learning + fuzzy logic), algorithms for safe Mars landing, FPGA-based traversability sensor development
JPL collaborators       — Isa Nesnas, Eddie Tunstel, Richard Volpe, Larry Matthews, Curtis Padgett, Jacob Barhen, etc.
Around 2003             — NASA research budget frozen after shuttle accident → decided to move to academia
July 2005               — Joined Georgia Tech (School of Electrical and Computer Engineering)
Early Georgia Tech      — Built indoor Mars yard; deployed autonomous rovers on Alaska/Colorado glaciers with NASA Earth Science Office support (climate change research)
Subsequent shift        — Reoriented research toward healthcare and children with disabilities; first project "Playing with Toys" (collaboration with Charlie Kemp)
Subsequent positions    — Second director of Georgia Tech's robotics PhD program; interviewed Henrik Christensen as a hire; Associate Director of Robotics Institute (Research)
NSF I-Corps participation — Discovered commercialization potential of tablet interface device for children with cerebral palsy
2013                    — Founded Zyrobotics — therapeutic and educational products for children with disabilities
At time of interview    — Zyrobotics at 7-person scale; trained students Sekou Remy (Clemson), Douglas Brooks (SwRI), Lonnie Parker (NUWC), Hae Won Park (postdoc, Zyrobotics co-founder), and others
January 22, 2015        — Interviewed by Peter Asaro (College Station, Texas)
```
