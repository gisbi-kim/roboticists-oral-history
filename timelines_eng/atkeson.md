# Chris Atkeson — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/atkeson.md`)

```
1959              — Born in Washington, D.C., USA (2 years after Sputnik)
Childhood         — Grew up in Washington; spent some time in the Philippines in the late 1960s before returning to Washington in the 1970s
Late 1970s        — Entered Harvard University; majored in Biochemistry with an MS in Applied Mathematics (Harvard then included CS within applied math)
PhD               — PhD program at MIT Brain and Cognitive Sciences (then Psychology); advisor Emilio Bizzi
PhD year 1        — Human motor research
PhD year 2        — Monkey experiments and human motor research
Late PhD          — Switched to robotics; collaborated with John Hollerbach (MIT AI Lab) and Neville Hogan (MechE); weight/inertia estimation experiments with PUMA robots
PhD thesis        — Dissertation on "How do you know the weight of an object you've picked up?"
After graduation  — Assistant professor in MIT Brain and Cognitive Sciences and the AI Lab
MIT era           — Researched content-addressable memory (memory-based learning); used the MIT Direct Drive Arm
MIT era           — Advised postdocs Andrew Moore and Stefan Schaal; PhD students David Reinkensmeyer, Ying Zhao, Sherif Botros, and others
MIT era           — Ball-hitting/juggling robots (ideological debates with Dan Koditschek's group), whip-cracking robot, etc.
1994              — Moved to Georgia Tech (following his then-girlfriend and future wife Jessica Hodgins's job)
Georgia Tech era  — Continued the memory-based vs. neural network debate; serious collaboration with Mitsuo Kawato (ATR, Japan); worked with Sarcos robots (carrying over the MIT Utah Hand connection that Hollerbach had introduced)
GT era            — Aware Home project (with Gregory Abowd, Aaron Bobick, Beth Mynatt, et al.); experimented with lecture capture systems ("robe of knowledge")
2000              — Moved to Carnegie Mellon University (marking the millennium)
CMU era           — Humanoid research with Sarcos and the DB (Dynamic Brain) robot; advised PhD students such as Darrin Bentivegna; 3-ball juggling, devil sticking, etc.
CMU era           — Care Media (Informedia spinoff) project; co-PI on the NSF Engineering Research Center "Quality of Life Technology"
CMU era           — Mentored junior faculty including Yoky Matsuoka, James Kuffner, and Hart Macgyver
2010              — At time of interview: Professor at the CMU Robotics Institute and the Human-Computer Interaction Institute
```
