# Hirochika Inoue — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/inoue.md`)

```
July 5, 1942               — Born in Kagoshima City (southern Japan)
Childhood                  — Interest in building model airplanes and radios amid postwar poverty; designed in his head while reading magazines
1961                       — Entered the Faculty of Engineering, University of Tokyo; chose mechanical engineering major (hesitated about electrical engineering)
1965                       — Earned bachelor's degree; entered graduate school — advised by Prof. Fuji, immediately accepted his offer to "build a mechanical hand that turns a crank"
1967                       — Earned master's degree
1st year of PhD            — Tried adaptive control (subsonic aircraft) → gave up due to U.S. standards dependency, returned to mechanical hand
Late PhD years             — University of Tokyo closed for 1 year due to student protests, plans to use medical school computer fell through; resumed after normalization in spring 1968
1968 (spring)              — Leased a computer for 6 months from a computer company (April~September) → finished PhD dissertation
1970                       — Earned Ph.D.; joined ETL (Electrotechnical Laboratory, MITI) at friend Shirai-san's recommendation
1st year at ETL            — Hand-eye system project — within 6 months produced 3 major papers on OS, hand-eye, and visual feedback; successful demonstration in September
ETL years                  — Active as one of 6 computer-driven robot groups along with Stanford, MIT, SRI, Edinburgh, and Hitachi
1973                       — 1-year visit to MIT AI Laboratory — met Marvin Minsky, Patrick Winston (at the time of lab director transition), students John Hollerbach and Tomás Lozano-Pérez; demonstrated 1/1000-inch precision assembly
After 1973                 — Returned to ETL, formed the Stewart platform parallel manipulator idea (influenced by Minsky)
1978                       — Returned to University of Tokyo as Associate Professor in Mechanical Engineering (negotiations took more than 2 years)
Late 1970s                 — Participated in establishing MITI's mechatronics concept — formulated academic and technical definitions
1984                       — Promoted to Full Professor
University years           — JSK (Jouhou System Kougaku) Lab — 5 themes of manipulation, mobility, vision, intelligence, and system integration, about 30 PhD students; all core elements developed in-house
Late 1990s                 — Renamed department to Mechano-Informatics (Ruzena Bajcsy proposed the name during her stay at his home)
1994                       — JIRA Award
Around 1996                — Started humanoid robot integration project
1999                       — Joseph Engelberger Robotics Award
Early 2000s                — Leader of METI's HRP humanoid national project — HRP2, HRP4, etc., commercialized
2004                       — Mandatory retirement from University of Tokyo (age 60)
April 2004 ~ September 2009 — Inspector General of JSPS (Japan Society for the Promotion of Science)
2005                       — Project leader of Aichi Expo robot event (budget over $50 million, about 50 universities participating); IEEE RAS Pioneer Award
2008                       — Emperor's Medal with Purple Ribbon
2011                       — IEEE Technical Field Award
August 30, 2011            — Interviewed by Selma Šabanović and Matthew R. Francisco (Flagstaff, AZ); attended ISRR
At time of interview       — Senior Research Advisor at AIST Digital Human Engineering Research Center; Professor Emeritus, University of Tokyo
2016                       — Japan Academy Prize (for contributions to robotics)
```
