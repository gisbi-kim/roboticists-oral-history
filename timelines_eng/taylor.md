# Russ Taylor — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/taylor.md`)

```
Birth              — Born in Hampton, Virginia, USA
Age 6              — Moved to near Valley Forge, Pennsylvania
Summer after high school — Took a programming course at the University of Pennsylvania
College years      — Summer internships at Bell Comm/Burroughs Corporation and others
1966~1970          — Undergraduate at Johns Hopkins University (Interdepartmental Engineering, electrical engineering + OR)
1970               — Earned BES at Johns Hopkins
1970~1976          — PhD program at Stanford University (AI Lab, advisor Jerry Feldman)
During PhD         — Participated in AL language development, exchanges with IBM's Peter Will and David Grossman
1976               — Earned PhD in computer science at Stanford ("Synthesis of Manipulator Control Programs from Task Level Specifications")
Right after PhD    — One summer of military service
Around 1976        — Joined IBM Research (Yorktown Heights) robotics group (Peter Will as manager)
1976~1978          — Robot project for inspecting mainframe backplane wiring in Poughkeepsie
After 1976         — Developed AML robot programming language
1978~1979          — Temporary assignment to IBM Boca Raton (Florida), product architect for the 7565 industrial robot
Around 1979        — Returned to IBM Research (Yorktown), promoted to manager
1982               — Began development of AML/X object-oriented robot language
Later              — Promoted to second-line manager overseeing 4 groups (Robot Systems & Technology, etc.)
Mid-management years — Decided to join surgical robot project (collaboration with UC Davis's Bill Bargar and Howard Paul)
Late 1980s         — Developed ROBODoc prototype with Peter Kazanzides in one year, handed off to UC Davis for animal use
Later              — Spun out Integrated Surgical Systems
Later              — Established Computer-Assisted Surgery group within IBM Research and developed LARS robot (jointly with Johns Hopkins)
1995               — Moved to Johns Hopkins University, appointed John C. Malone Professor
1994               — Elected IEEE Fellow
1996~1997          — Wrote NSF Engineering Research Center proposal
1997               — Secured NSF ERC funding (Hopkins, CMU, MIT, Brigham and Women's Hospital)
After 1995         — Recruited Greg Hager, Allison Okamura, Peter Kazanzides, and others
Later              — IBM licensed Taylor's patent portfolio to Intuitive Surgical
2014. 9. 15.       — Interview with Peter Asaro conducted in Chicago
At time of interview — Leading medical robotics/image-guided surgery research at Hopkins, 325+ papers
```
