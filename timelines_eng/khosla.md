# Pradeep Khosla — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/khosla.md`)

```
Birth           — Born in India
School years    — Completed schooling through high school in India
1980            — B.S. in Electrical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur (undergrad thesis: a TDC316 disassembler — Computer Society)
1980–1982       — Worked on real-time control of power systems in India (stayed out of patriotic motivation)
1982            — Began MS/PhD studies at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), advised by Chuck Neuman with co-advisor Fritz Prinz
Master's years  — Developed seam-tracking algorithms for a welding robot
1984            — M.S. in Electrical Engineering, CMU
From late 1983  — Began PhD research under Takeo Kanade
PhD research    — CMU Direct-Drive Arm II (SCARA configuration, direct-drive)
                 — Built a Marinko 64-bit CPU multiprocessor architecture from scratch; demonstrated 500Hz real-time control
                 — Collaborated with Don Schmitz, Mark De Louis, and others
1986            — PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering, CMU; appointed Assistant Professor at CMU
Early after PhD — Follow-on force control research using the direct-drive arm (first PhD student Richard Volpe — JPL)
                 — Demonstrated equivalence of impedance control and force control
                 — Demonstrated the impact control concept
Around 1990     — Started the CMU Reconfigurable Modular Manipulator System
                 — Worked with students such as Chris Paredis (Georgia Tech) and Laura Kelmar
                 — David Stewart's Chimera real-time modular OS and Matthew Gertz's Onika icon-based programming language developed
1993            — Decided to join DARPA at the urging of DARPA Director Gary Denman
1994.01–1996    — Program Manager, DARPA Software and Intelligent Systems Technology Office
                 — Managed programs in real-time planning/control, manufacturing, automation, and design engineering
1996            — Returned to CMU
1996–           — Millibots project with DARPA's Dick Urban (5cm-scale distributed sensor collective)
                 — Featured in Scientific American
Late 1990s      — Shifted focus to embedded software security (collaborations with Adrian Perrig and Rohit Negi)
1997–1999       — Founding Director, Institute for Complex Engineered Systems (ICES)
1999–2004       — Head, CMU ECE Department
2000–2004       — Director, Information Networking Institute
2001–2008       — Founding Director, CMU CyLab (largest cybersecurity research center in the U.S. within five years)
2004–2012       — Dean of the College of Engineering, CMU
2008            — Promoted to University Professor
2012            — Received the Light of India Award
2012.08         — Inaugurated as the 8th Chancellor of UC San Diego
At time of interview — Dean of CMU's College of Engineering (interview conducted in 2010)
```
