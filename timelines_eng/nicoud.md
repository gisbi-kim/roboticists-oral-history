# Jean-Daniel Nicoud — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/nicoud.md`)

```
1938-08-31        — Born in Lausanne, Switzerland
Childhood         — Fascinated by Grey Walter's tortoise robots (mid-1950s Scientific American article)
Undergrad         — Studied physics at EPFL (Ecole Polytechnique)
1963              — Earned physics degree from EPFL
After graduation  — Took a teaching position at a college but, unable to get a chance to teach physics, switched to electronics
1965              — Received a bag of 1,000 transistors discarded by Bosch and built his first robot (inspired by Grey Walter, relay logic)
After 1965        — Returned to EPFL; began calculator research in earnest
1969              — PhD from EPFL ("Decimal-to-binary conversion and relays")
1971              — Founded the company DIDEL (didactiques électroniques)
1973              — Appointed full professor at EPFL
1974              — Organized the first International Conference on Microprocessors
1974              — Took over as head of the Microcomputer Laboratory (until 2000)
1974              — Designed a personal computer for Digital Equipment (DEC)
1975–1976         — Built six personal computers for DEC
1980–1982         — Vice chair of the IEEE P896 committee
Around 1980       — Developed the Smaky personal computer family; opened the lab to children every Wednesday
1980s             — Designed the mechanics of technician André Guignard's Depraz Mouse (foundation of the Logitech mouse)
1980s             — Developed CALM (Common Assembly Language for Microprocessors)
Around 1991       — Built the Cyclop robot (linear camera + HC-11), used in student contests
1991              — Attended his first robotics conference, in Trento
1991              — Carried out the Cafer 5cm robot student project
Early 1990s       — Ran a robotics lab of about 30 PhD students
1992–1993         — Developed the Khepera robot (Francesco Mondada redesigned it from the Cafer)
Around 1995–1996  — Recruited Dario Floreano into the lab; researched neural networks and genetic algorithms
1995–1996         — Built 1-cubic-inch / 1-cubic-cm robots; entered Japan's Fukuda micro robot contest
1997              — Began winding down all projects
2000              — Retired from EPFL; focused on running the private company DIDEL SA
2000s             — Built 5g flying robots with Jean-Christophe Zufferey
Around 2008       — Completed the 5g flying robot (10g total airframe)
2011-07-18        — IEEE History Center oral history interview (Lausanne, Switzerland)
Later             — Developed children's educational robot kits and C-language embedded modules at DIDEL
```
