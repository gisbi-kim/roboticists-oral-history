# Michael Arbib — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/arbib.md`)

```
1940           — Born in England
1947           — Moved to New Zealand at age 7
1949           — Moved to Sydney, Australia at age 9; attended high school and university there
Undergrad years — Read Norbert Wiener's "Cybernetics" and turned away from pure math; encountered Grey Walter's tortoise robots
1960           — Earned BS in Pure Mathematics from the University of Sydney
1960           — Collaborated with neurophysiologist Bill Levick; influenced by "What the Frog's Eye Tells the Frog's Brain"
1960           — Entered MIT PhD program (advisor: Wiener, later switched to a statistical mechanics expert)
1961           — Paper analyzing Pitts's 1943 work published in Journal of the ACM
During PhD     — Lectured at the University of New South Wales in Australia; wrote his first book "Brains, Machines, and Mathematics"
1963           — Earned PhD in Mathematics from MIT (probability and stochastic processes)
1963           — Crossed the U.S. then traveled in Europe and the Soviet Union; met Rudolf Kálmán
Around 1964    — Postdoc under Kálmán at Stanford for 5 years; discussed Shakey/STRIPS with Nils Nilsson at SRI
Stanford era   — With Richard Didday, proposed a winner-take-all network model; developed schema theory
1970           — Became founding chair of the Department of Computer and Information Science at the University of Massachusetts Amherst
1977           — Founded the Center for Systems Neuroscience; Shun-ichi Amari joined as the first visiting researcher
1979           — At a brain conference, encountered Marc Jeannerod's hand-movement research and expanded into hand control
Around 1980    — Founded the Laboratory for Perceptual Robotics; introduced the Salisbury Hand; researched hand control with Thea Iberall and Damian Lyons; Lyons developed the RS (Robot Schemas) language
UMass era      — Hired Andy Barto and Rick Sutton, laying groundwork for reinforcement learning theory; Ron Arkin researched robot control with frog models
1982           — With Amari, organized "Competition and Cooperation in Neural Nets" conference in Kyoto; befriended Mitsuo Kawato
1986           — Moved to USC, recruited by George Bekey; collaborated on USC/Belgrade Hand research
USC era        — Advised PhD students Thony Lewis, Andy Fagg, and others; recruited Stefan Schaal, Maja Matarić, and Gaurav Sukhatme as assistant professors
Late 1990s     — International collaborations on neural mechanisms of hand movement with Japan, France, Italy; after the discovery of mirror neurons, ran human fMRI studies with Scott Grafton
2011           — At time of interview: USC Fletcher Jones Professor of Computer Science with multiple joint appointments; focused on language evolution research
```
