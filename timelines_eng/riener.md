# Robert Riener — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/riener.md`)

```
Birth               — Born in Munich, Germany; grew up in the same area (father was an automotive technician)
Childhood           — Torn between technical and medical careers
1988–1993           — Studied Mechanical Engineering at TU München and the University of Maryland
1993                — Dipl.-Ing. degree from TU München
1993                — Joined the Institute of Automatic Control Engineering, began research on neuroprosthetics and functional electrical stimulation (FES) (mentors Jochen Quintern; orthopedist Rainer Burgkart)
PhD years           — Neuroprosthetic simulation and control; control of standing, sitting, walking, and stair climbing
1997                — Doctorate (Dr.) from TU München
1998–1999           — Postdoc at Politecnico di Milano's Centro di Bioingegneria; pioneering measurements of walking and stair-climbing data on various inclines
After 1999          — Returned to TU München, pursued Habilitation (biomechatronics)
Late TU München     — Developed a haptic-interface-based knee diagnosis simulator with Burgkart; commercialized a childbirth simulator
2003                — Joined ETH Zurich as professor (Switzerland), joined Volker Dietz and Gery Colombo's Lokomat group
Early ETH           — Began developing rehabilitation robots such as the ARMin exoskeletal arm for stroke patients; active lower-limb prosthesis project
2010                — Appointed full professor in the ETH Department of Health Sciences and Technology (Sensory-Motor Systems) and full professor of Rehabilitation Engineering
Afterwards          — Active at the Spinal Cord Injury Center, Balgrist University Hospital
2011                — euRobotics Technology Transfer Award
July 15, 2011       — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro (Zurich)
2012                — euRobotics Technology Transfer Award (second time)
Afterwards          — Continued collaboration with Hocoma on new Lokomat models and VR scenarios
```
