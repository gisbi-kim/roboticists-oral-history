# Norm Caplan — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/caplan.md`)

```
July 26, 1925         — Born in New York City; grew up mainly in Queens and Brooklyn
After high school     — Enlisted in the US Navy as an electronics technician specializing in radar
After discharge       — Enrolled at NYU College of Engineering
1951                  — BEE (Bachelor of Electrical Engineering) from NYU
1954                  — MEE (Master of Electrical Engineering) from NYU; six years in the NYU research division while serving as Adjunct Associate Professor
Subsequent industry   — Worked on electronic countermeasures at Burrows and RCA; Polaris submarine communications project
Afterwards            — Joined Tracor; HF communications and direction-finding research; Chief Engineer/VP of the Systems and Sciences Division
1973                  — NYU College of Engineering closed; joined NSF as a rotator and converted to permanent staff as Program Director for Control & Automation
NSF years             — Bundled KS Fu, George Saridis, Kashyap, and John Liu at Purdue to establish the Advanced Automation Laboratory
1983                  — Drove the founding of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Council (led by George Saridis with KS Fu, Lou Paul, and others on the committee)
Around 1983           — First ICRA conference (presumed St. Louis, with KS Fu as General Chair)
September 1983        — Attended the G7-affiliated IARP (International Advanced Robotics Programme) Tokyo organizing meeting as the US delegate
Mid-1980s             — Active on the NATO Science Committee robotics panel; workshops in Brussels, Toulon, Genoa, and elsewhere
1991                  — Took office as President of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society
1996                  — General Chair of ICRA Minneapolis
2000                  — Retired from NSF (served three times as a Division Director)
2000–2002             — Two-year collaboration with the Johns Hopkins ERC (Hopkins-MIT-CMU surgical robotics center)
2005                  — Received the IEEE RAS Distinguished Service Award
2006                  — Second time as ICRA General Chair (Orlando)
May 16, 2012          — IEEE oral history interview conducted in St. Paul, MN
```
