# Bernie Roth — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/roth.md`)

```
Birth               — Born in the Bronx, New York City, USA; grew up in the same area
High school         — Entered Stuyvesant High School, then transferred to and graduated from Christopher Columbus High School
Undergrad           — BS in Mechanical Engineering from City College of New York
Right after grad    — Began as instructor at City College and as drafting instructor in Hunter College's art department; got married
Starting grad school — Entered Columbia University's evening MS/PhD program (took advisor's classes at night, used IBM 650 and other machines)
End of PhD          — Resigned from City College teaching, finished PhD on a Columbia fellowship (dissertation area: kinematics, developed the continuation method)
1958                — MS in Mechanical Engineering from Columbia
1962                — PhD in Mechanical Engineering from Columbia, moved with family to Stanford, California (annual salary $8,000)
1962                — Assistant professor in Stanford Mechanical Engineering, taught kinematics
Around 1964         — Joined John McCarthy's SAIL project after a phone call from Frederick Terman, began developing robotic arms
Mid–late 1960s      — Modified a Rancho prosthetic arm to implement computer-controlled robotic arms (nicknamed Shaky / Butter Fingers)
Late 1960s          — Developed the digital pneumatic arm ORM (Norwegian for "snake") and follow-on hydraulic arms
1968–1969           — First sabbatical: visited TU Delft, Netherlands (started writing "Theoretical Kinematics"); students Vic Scheinman and Ken Waldron ran things at Stanford in his absence
Spring 1969         — Screened the SAIL film at the inaugural IFTOMM conference, met Ivan Ivanovich Artobolevskii and others
After 1969          — Soviet Academy exchange visits, friendship with Aron Kobrinsky, sought East–West exchanges
After 1972          — Vic Scheinman founded Vicarm, then was acquired by Joe Engelberger's Unimation, leading to the development of Puma
1973                — At the IFTOMM conference in Yugoslavia, founded the Romansy series, served as US committee member
Mid-1970s           — Lou Paul wrote the first robotics textbook; later sponsored John Craig's book
1980s               — Secured Sun-1 computers and funding for John Craig's book through the Systems Development Foundation; diversified funding via NSF
1980s               — Advised Ken Salisbury (multi-fingered hand PhD), Jeff Kerr (grasping), John Craig (control), Madhu Raghavan (inverse kinematics), and others
Afterwards          — Visited the Paris robotics institute one month per year (advised Mavroidis)
Afterwards          — Exchanges with Shigeo Hirose (Tokyo), Georges Giralt, and Raja Chatila (LAAS); visiting professorships in India (Bangalore, Kanpur, New Delhi)
2000                — ASME Outstanding Design Educator Award
Afterwards          — Co-founded the Hasso Plattner Institute of Design (d.school); served as interim director when Dave Kelley was ill, then continued as co-director
2007                — Honorary doctorate from the University of Paris
November 18, 2010   — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro and Selma Šabanović (Stanford)
```
