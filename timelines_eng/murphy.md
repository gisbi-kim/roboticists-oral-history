# Robin Murphy — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/murphy.md`)

```
Around 1957       — Born in Mobile, Alabama, USA (a few days after the launch of Sputnik, during the space race era)
Childhood         — Moved to Douglas, Georgia for schooling
Childhood         — Dreamed of working on a space station; influenced by her mechanical engineer father
College           — BS in mechanical engineering from Georgia Tech
After undergrad   — Worked as a process control mechanical engineer at Dow Chemical
Later             — Worked at Turbitrol in Atlanta (now part of Johnson Controls)
Late 1980s        — Returned to Georgia Tech for an MS in computer science, leveraging her process-control computing experience
1988              — MS in computer science from Georgia Tech
Early PhD         — Entered the PhD program with a Computer Integrative Manufacturing Systems Fellowship
PhD               — Joined Ron Arkin as his first PhD student (reactive robotics paradigm)
PhD               — As a Rockwell International Fellow, brought in cognitive psychologist Herb Pick as an outside committee member; researched sensor fusion
1992              — First robotics PhD from the Georgia Tech College of Computing
1992–1998         — Associate professor at the Colorado School of Mines
1995              — Oklahoma City bombing and Kobe earthquake; began the disaster robotics field with Satoshi Tadokoro
1997–1998         — Member of the Defense Science Study Group
1998              — Moved to the University of South Florida; collaborated with Florida Task Force 3
2001              — First emergency-response use of ground robots at the 9/11 World Trade Center incident
After 2001        — Co-founded the RAS Safety, Security and Rescue Robotics Technical Committee with Satoshi Tadokoro and Paolo Fiorini
2003              — Promoted to full professor at the University of South Florida
2004              — Identified limitations of ground robots during Hurricane Charley
2005              — Flew UAVs in Pearlington, Mississippi during Hurricane Katrina
2005              — Received the U.S. Air Force Exemplary Civilian Service Award
2008              — Moved to Texas A&M University; uses Disaster City
2010              — Appeared in the documentary "Living With Robots" (Sundance Film Festival)
2011              — Tohoku earthquake / Fukushima Daiichi nuclear accident; advised the Honeywell T-Hawk UAV team in Tokyo
2011 follow-up    — Supported port cleanup and missing-person searches with unmanned underwater vehicles
2014              — Received the ACM Eugene L. Lawler Award
2014              — Published the book "Disaster Robotics"
2015-01-22        — IEEE History Center oral history interview (College Station, TX)
2016              — Responded to the Syrian boat refugee crisis
2017              — Responded to Hurricane Harvey (cumulative total of 27 disaster deployments)
```
