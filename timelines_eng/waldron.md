# Ken Waldron — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/waldron.md`)

```
Childhood          — Born in Sydney, Australia
                    Engineering interest shaped by his maternal grandfather, who worked as a draftsman for the water authority
Undergrad years    — Received an Australian steel industry traineeship (vacation internship + salary)
1964              — Earned a Bachelor of Engineering (Mechanical) from the University of Sydney
1965              — Earned a Master of Engineering Science from the University of Sydney
1965-06           — Entered Stanford graduate school; joined Bernie Roth's lab
                    Continued his linkage mobility research from Australia as his PhD topic
Around 1968~1969   — Acted as assistant professor and taught classes during Bernie Roth's sabbatical
                    Advised on Vic Scheinman's Stanford Arm design; shared an office and discussed inverse rate
                    kinematics with Don Piper
1969              — Earned a PhD from Stanford Mechanical Engineering
After PhD          — Returned to Australia for a faculty position at the University of New South Wales
During sabbatical  — Visited the University of Maryland; was interviewed during a visit to a former roommate at the University of Houston
Around 1974        — Decided to move to the U.S. (partly for his deaf child's education); joined Univ. of Houston as a professor
                    (focused on mechanism theory at this time, with little robotics)
1979              — Moved to Ohio State University
                    Joined Bob McGee's legged walking robot work (the OSU hexapod)
1981              — Started the DARPA-funded Adaptive Suspension Vehicle (ASV) project
                    (oversaw mechanical design; with student Simon Song and others, recast static stability walking theory)
Late 1980s         — Visited Stanford on sabbatical with Vic Scheinman, Bernie Roth, etc.
                    Joined Oussama Khatib's Artisan Project (a hybrid serial-parallel robot)
                    Published a paper on serial-parallel kinematics with Madhu Raghavan
1990              — End of the ASV project
1993~2000         — Chair of Mechanical Engineering at Ohio State University
1994              — Received the Machine Design Award
1997              — Received the Joseph F. Engelberger Award
1999              — Awarded a Doctor of Engineering by the University of Sydney
2000              — Moved to Stanford as Professor (Research)
                    Mobile robot work funded by DARPA/SARIC; continued the dynamically stable quadruped work
                    brought from Ohio State; pursued dynamically stable bipedal walking research
Later              — Served 8 years as President of IFToMM (International Federation for the Promotion of Mechanism and Machine Science)
                    Collaborated with Gamini Dissanayake at UTS Australia on a steel-bridge inspection climbing robot
2011              — Retired from Stanford
2011-06-08        — Interviewed by Selma Šabanović and Peter Asaro at Stanford, CA
```
