# Jean-Paul Laumond — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/laumond.md`)

```
1953                  — Born in a rural village north of Toulouse, France
School years          — Schooling in Brive; entered Toulouse University; transferred to mathematics after classe préparatoire
1976                  — MS in Mathematics from Université Paul Sabatier (Toulouse); obtained teaching credentials
1976~1981             — High school mathematics teacher (5 years, in northern France and elsewhere; first appointment at age 24)
~1977                 — At a friend's recommendation, first meeting with Georges Giralt at LAAS; advised at the second meeting to pursue a computer science degree
~1983                 — Earned computer science degree while teaching (studied Hopcroft–Tarjan graph decomposition algorithms)
1983                  — Presented first sole-author paper at IJCAI '83 in Karlsruhe (mobile robot environment learning); first exposure to HILARE project
1984                  — PhD in Robotics from Université Paul Sabatier
1985                  — Appointed CNRS permanent researcher (age 32); joined LAAS
1985                  — Giralt posed the nonholonomic motion planning problem, which became Laumond's lifelong research theme
1986                  — At Amsterdam IAS Symposium, first proof of feasibility of car-like robot parking
1989                  — Obtained Habilitation
Summer 1990           — Visited Stanford for 3–4 months at Jean-Claude Latombe's invitation; proved controllability of multi-trailer systems
1991~1995             — Member of France's Comité National de la Recherche Scientifique
1992~1995             — Led EU project PROMotion (including pure mathematicians; nonholonomic motion planning)
1992~2002             — Participated in EU project MOLOG and others (virtual prototyping applications)
2001~2002             — Founded and served as CEO of Kineo CAM (commercializing motion planning technology, automotive virtual prototyping)
2003~2007             — Scientific advisor to Kineo CAM, then separated
2003                  — Started humanoid motion planning research; presented bipedal walking character at Symposium on Computer Animation
2004                  — Met AIST's Eiichi Yoshida and Kazuhito Yokoi at IROS Sendai → start of HRP-2 collaboration
2005~2008             — Co-Director of France-Japan Joint Robotics Laboratory (JRL); demonstrated humanoid motion planning including barbell manipulation with HRP-2
2006                  — Appointed Research Director of LAAS Gepetto group (dedicated to humanoid motion)
Later                 — Mathematical model research of human walking with Alain Berthoz; published in Journal of Neuroscience
2011                  — ETHW oral history interview (San Francisco)
2011~2012             — Selected as Liliane Bettencourt Chair of Technological Innovation at Collège de France
Ongoing               — Lectures on robotics at Ecole Normale Supérieure (Paris)
```
