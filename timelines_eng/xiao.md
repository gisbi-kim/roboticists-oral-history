# Jing Xiao — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/xiao.md`)

```
Childhood ~ undergrad — Born in Beijing, China
Undergrad          — Entered Beijing Normal University, majoring in physics
                    Switched to electrical engineering in the second year; later moved toward digital circuits, computer engineering, and computer science
                    Final undergraduate project simulated physics experiments using Apple 1 and 2 computers
Graduate study     — Came to the U.S. for the University of Michigan (interdisciplinary Computer, Information &
                    Control Engineering program)
PhD years          — Worked with advisor Dick Volz on the peg-and-hole assembly problem
                    PhD dissertation on "Uncertainty in fine motion planning"
                    Fellow students worked on assembly sequence planning and force-sensing-based state recognition
PhD completion     — Earned both M.S. and Ph.D. in the above program at the University of Michigan
                    Graduated as the robotics boom was ending — a tough job market
1990              — Joined the Computer Science department at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte
1997              — After tenure, took a sabbatical at Ford Research Lab; researched sequencing optimization
                    of vehicle assembly lines
1998              — Visited Stanford University for half a year (hosted by Jean-Claude Latombe,
                    interacting with Oussama Khatib); researched contact-constrained motion planning
1998              — Became Program Director at the National Science Foundation for robotics and human augmentation —
                    served about 2.5 years; pushed the 'Personal Robotics' initiative
1999              — Elected to the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Administrative Committee (AdCom) (3-year term)
Around 2001        — Returned to UNC Charlotte; researched real-time adaptive motion planning in unknown environments
                    (especially mobile manipulators and high-DOF robots)
Later              — Researched haptic rendering (multi-contact situations), deformable object simulation, etc.
                    Collaborated with KU Leuven (Joris De Schutter, Herman Bruyninckx) on autonomous compliant
                    motion planning and execution
                    Provided autonomy algorithms for the Clemson University Walker group's elephant trunk
                    (continuum manipulator) robot — autonomous grasping in cluttered environments and obstacle avoidance
                    Collaborated with GM on surface assembly (surface alignment)
                    Collaborated with Beihang University on haptics
2013              — Death of her PhD advisor Dick Volz
At time of interview — IEEE RAS Vice President for Member Activities
2015-01-22        — Interviewed by Selma Šabanović in College Station, Texas
```
