# Robert Ambrose — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/ambrose.md`)

```
1964            — Born in Boston, USA
Late 1960s      — Family moved to Houston, Texas
Summer 1969     — Watched the Moon landing in Houston and decided to join NASA (age 5)
Childhood       — Attended elementary school in Houston and Chicago; began building with motors around age 5
Middle school   — Self-built a closed-loop control system for solar panels using photo resistors
Early 1980s     — Entered Washington University in St. Louis, majoring in Mechanical Engineering
Undergrad/MS    — BS and MS in Mechanical Engineering; worked on linkage synthesis and beta-tested CAD systems for the F18 and Harrier
Summer 1987     — Began PhD at UT Austin under Delbert Tesar, researching modular manipulator design methodology
During PhD      — Attended with his wife; worked on internet-based remote robot teleoperation in a NASA consortium (UT Austin, UT Arlington, Texas A&M, Rice)
1989–1990       — Teleoperated a Robotics Research 1607 KB manipulator from Austin
Summer 1991     — Earned PhD from UT Austin
1991            — Joined NASA Johnson Space Center (contractor) in Charlie Price's Technology Branch
After 1991      — Developed manipulator joints for the EVA temperature range (-50 to +100°C); ran thermal-vacuum chamber experiments
Early NASA era  — Captured technology from the cancelled FTS (Flight Telerobotic Servicer)
1996            — Started the Robonaut project
1998            — The name "Robonaut" was finalized
August 1999     — Became Robonaut project leader
September 1999  — Robonaut successfully grasped a tool for the first time (via teleoperation)
2000            — Added a second arm (left arm) and a waist joint
2001            — Repackaged the torso; introduced skin/tactile sensors
2002            — Built Robonaut 1B1; integrated with Segway under DARPA sponsorship (Mark Swinson, then Doug Gage)
Early 2000s     — Collaborated with university researchers including Rod Grupen, Alan Peters, Maja Matarić, and Cynthia Breazeal
2005–2006       — NASA shifted toward lunar exploration; directed to develop a next-generation lunar rover
2006            — Built a custom rover for the final Robonaut 1 series model
2007            — Built the Chariot 1A rover (later joined by 1B)
Around 2010     — Remote demo of the Centaur II + Robonaut 2 combination in Arizona, commanded from Houston
2011            — At time of interview: PI of the Game Changing Development Program at NASA JSC and 6th-year leader of the Human Robotics Systems Project
```
