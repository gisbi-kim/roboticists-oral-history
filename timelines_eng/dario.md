# Paolo Dario — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/dario.md`)

```
Birth                — Born in Piombino, Tuscany, Italy
1977                 — Mechanical engineering degree from the University of Pisa (thesis in biomedical engineering: pulsatile artificial heart)
1977                 — Married Laura
Right after 1977     — One year of military service
After military       — Started research at the University of Pisa; conducted sensor technology research at the CNR (Italian National Research Council) Institute of Clinical Physiology
1980                 — First time on an airplane, visiting the United States (age 29; collaborating with Pierre Galletti and Peter Richardson at Brown University)
1982                 — First met Tony Bejczy during a US visit (JPL); fully entered robotics
1983                 — One-month stay in China (Academia Sinica, Zhejiang University) followed by his first visit to Japan
1983~1986/87         — Stayed at Brown University (Providence) with family; assistant professor (research) position; piezoelectric polymer research with Johnson & Johnson
1983                 — Tenured assistant professor appointment at the University of Pisa
1984                 — First ICRA (the conference just before St. Louis 1985); began contact with Khatib, Salisbury, Bajcsy, Lozano-Perez, Inoue, Fukuda, etc.; first met Ruzena Bajcsy
1985                 — ICRA (St. Louis) participation
1986                 — Tenured associate professor appointment in biomedical engineering at Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna
1986                 — Organized the first NATO robotics workshop at Jean Vertut's urging
1986~87              — Published a robotic palpation paper with Massimo Bergamasco in IEEE Trans. Biomedical Engineering (early medical robotics)
1989                 — Organized a second NATO workshop and edited the resulting book
1989                 — Founded the ARTS Lab (Advanced Robotics Technology and Systems)
1991                 — Organized the ICAR conference in Pisa (501 attendees)
1991                 — Founded CRIM (MiTech), a second lab focused on micro robotics
1991                 — Founded SM (Scienzia Machinale), Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna's first spin-off; launched a medical robot arm
1990s                — Frequent visits to Japan and the US (interactions with Steve Jacobsen in Salt Lake, CMU, Tokyo, Waseda, Nagoya, etc.)
2000                 — Promoted to full professor at Scuola Superiore Sant'Anna
2000s                — Collaborated with Giulio Sandini; played a key role in forming the EURON / EUROP networks
At time of interview — Coordinator of the BioRobotics Institute (about 160 people, 80 PhD students); driving the Robot Companion FET flagship
2011-07-28           — IEEE oral history interview in Pisa
```
