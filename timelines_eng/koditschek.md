# Daniel Koditschek — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/koditschek.md`)

```
Birth           — Born in Montclair, New Jersey, USA; settled in Montclair after living in several New Jersey towns
High school     — Graduated from Montclair Public High School (interested in English literature and history; weak in math)
College         — Vietnam War era — exempted from the draft because his lottery number was 364 (very high)
Early undergrad — Began as an English literature major at Yale University
                 — Drifted through linguistics, anthropology, and others; eventually took a leave of absence
During leave    — Read popular science books such as Norbert Wiener's "God & Golem" and felt the limits of his math
                 — Decided to return to school and tackle math and engineering
After returning — Met Yale engineering professor K. S. Narendra (a control theorist) — a major influence
1981            — B.S. in Engineering and Applied Science, Yale University
Right after graduation — Decided not to enter academia; worked as a literacy tutor for rural and migrant workers in upstate New York
                 — Inspired by Cesar Chavez, attempted to organize farm workers in the Northeast; gradually began regular visits to Narendra's lab
1979            — Eventually returned to Yale graduate school; researched adaptive control under Narendra
1983            — M.S./Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering, Yale University
1983            — Appointed to the Yale faculty
1984–1985       — Discovered Marc Raibert's hopping robot research; deeply influenced
1986            — Student Martin Buhler joined the lab
1987–1988       — Buhler's first juggling robot (planar puck) began operating
1988–1989       — With student Elon Rimon, globalized Khatib's potential field idea (algebraic-topology obstacle discovery)
                 — Louis Whitcomb built the spatial version; Al Rizzi implemented spatial juggling
1991            — Left Yale
1992            — Moved to the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor
1990s           — Collaborated with biologists such as Bob Full (UC Berkeley) (triggered when student Bill Schwin found pogo stick literature)
                 — Contributed to the formation of the field of neuromechanics
2001            — Developed the RHex robot (collaboration with Martin Buehler; PhD work by Uluç Saranlı)
2004            — Visited the University of Pennsylvania
2005.01         — Accepted the chair position of Penn ESE (Electrical and Systems Engineering) at the urging of Dean Eduardo Glandt
                 — Appointed Alfred Fitler Moore Professor
Later Penn years — Collaborated with Robert Ghrist on computational topology
                 — Goran Lynch's PhD thesis on dynamical climbing RHex (RiSE) (collaboration with Bob Full, Mark Cutkosky, Johnathan Clark)
                 — Student Johnathan Clark went on to become a faculty member in Florida
2016            — Received the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Pioneer Award
2017            — Received the Penn Heilmeier Award for Excellence in Research
At time of interview — Chair of Penn ESE; IEEE Life Fellow
```
