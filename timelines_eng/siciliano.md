# Bruno Siciliano — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/siciliano.md`)

```
October 27, 1959        — Born in Naples, Italy
Childhood ~ undergrad   — Graduated from a science-track high school (Lyceum) in Naples; entered Electronic Engineering at University of Naples Federico II
1982                    — Earned Laurea (M.S. equivalent) in Electronic Engineering in 4.5 years
Right after 1982        — Entered the first cohort of the newly established Research Doctorate (Ph.D.); turned to robotics research at Lorenzo Sciavicco's urging
Early PhD               — Met Bernie Roth and Oussama Khatib at the RoManSy conference (lifelong friend of Khatib)
1983                    — Attended ISIR (first international industrial robot conference) in Chicago
September 1985~June 1986 — Visiting scholar in Wayne Book's group, Mechanical Engineering at Georgia Tech; RALF (flexible arm) project
1986                    — Returned to Naples after U.S. travel; awaited final dissertation completion
1986                    — First ICRA attendance (San Francisco)
1987                    — Earned Dr.Eng. in Electronic Engineering from Naples
1987~1989               — CRIAI Scientific Collaborator; unemployed post-doc, dependent on parents' home
1989                    — Appointed Assistant Professor at University of Naples Federico II (declined Stanford offer)
1990~1991               — Opened the first robotics course at Naples; began drafting lecture notes
1991                    — Promoted to Associate Professor
1991                    — Editorial board member of IEEE Transactions on Robotics & Automation
Around 1992~1993        — Opened the PRISMA Lab (funded by CNR Progetto Finalizzato Robotica)
1995                    — Published English-language robotics textbook with McGraw-Hill (co-authored with Sciavicco); presented first copy to Lou Paul (Herrsching ISRR)
1996                    — Member of RAS AdCom
1999                    — Appointed Vice President for Publications
1999                    — Promoted to Full Professor
2000                    — Elected IEEE Fellow
2000~2003               — Full Professor of Automatic Control at University of Salerno
Around 2001             — Co-Editor of the Springer Tracts in Advanced Robotics (STAR) series (jointly with Khatib and Frans Groen)
2002                    — Commissioned by Springer to compile the Handbook of Robotics; decided to begin with Khatib at ICRA (Crystal City)
2003                    — Returned to University of Naples Federico II as Full Professor of Control & Robotics, Director of PRISMA Lab
October 2005            — Elected IEEE RAS President-Elect
2008                    — Published Handbook of Robotics (6 years of work; won the AAP PROSE Award)
2008                    — Attended the Accademia dei Lincei robotics symposium with Vatican participation
2008~2009               — Served as IEEE RAS President
2009                    — General chair of Naples Futuro Remoto event; ASIMO demonstration (in cooperation with Khatib)
2009                    — Published 3rd edition of textbook (Springer)
2010~2011               — Continued RAS service as Junior Past President
2011                    — Coordinator of EU projects including DEXMART (dual-arm/hand manipulation)
2011                    — Conducted the AIRobots (aerial manipulation) project
At interview            — Director of the Interdepartmental Center for Advanced Robotics in Surgery (ICAROS)
July 25, 2011           — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro in Naples
Later (intro note)      — Recipient of the 2015 IEEE RAS George Saridis Leadership Award; received an ERC Advanced Grant
```
