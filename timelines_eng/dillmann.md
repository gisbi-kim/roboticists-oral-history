# Rüdiger Dillmann — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/dillmann.md`)

```
1949                — Born in Mannheim, Germany
Primary/secondary   — Mannheim primary school → high school focused on technology/natural sciences; met computing pioneers including Zuse; captivated by cybernetics
University entry    — Entered Karlsruhe University (electrical engineering); audited some medical semesters in Heidelberg
Late undergrad      — Specialized in biocybernetics and control engineering; student assistant at the biomedical and control engineering institutes
Around 1969         — While still an undergraduate, founded Scientific Precision Instruments, selling high-precision micromanipulators (micron- and 200nm-precision)
Mid-1970s           — Supplied high-precision manipulators to Heidelberg University for synapse research
Around 1976         — Joined the Karlsruhe institute (Ulrich Rembold's division); installed the 7th PUMA robot in Europe (one of the earliest)
1977~80             — PhD work: pulse frequency control systems (muscle stimulation + Viking satellite limit-cycle) research
1980                — PhD from Karlsruhe University (advisors Föllinger and Paul Frank)
After PhD           — Developed the Pascal for Robots language; participated in the ESPRIT (CIM) project
Early 1980s         — Joined IARP (NATO International Advanced Robotics Programme) US and Japan missions; visited MIT, CMU, NBS, Stanford, Berkeley, JPL
Early 1980s         — First international conferences in Tokyo (ISIR/ICAR); met Japanese pioneers Kato, Hasegawa, Miura, Inoue; viewed the piano-playing humanoid at Tsukuba Expo
Around 1984~85      — Wound down heavy involvement with industrial robots; pivoted toward programming-by-demonstration / learning robots
1985                — Completed Habilitation (on machine learning + learning robots)
1986                — Passed the state exam; appointed associate professor at KIT (then Karlsruhe)
Late 1980s          — Collaborated with biologist Holk Cruse; developed the first generation of 6-legged stick-insect robots (wood → carbon → metal)
1987                — Full professor in computer science
1996                — Started a collaborative research center in the medical area (Heidelberg medical staff + Karlsruhe engineering team, computer-supported surgery)
1999                — Completed the first humanoid arm (the starting point of the ARMAR-1 series)
2001                — Began the SFB 588 "Humanoid Robots" collaborative research center (an 11-year program, about 10 institutions)
2002                — Director of the FZI (Research Center for Information Science) innovation lab
2009                — Spokesperson of the Institute of Anthropomatics at Karlsruhe Institute of Technology; founded the KIT-Focus Anthropomatics and Robotics
At time of interview — Assembling the 4th-generation ARMAR humanoid; pursuing the "Experience" project (started 2011-01); EURON co-founder
2011-08-30          — IEEE oral history interview in Flagstaff, AZ
```
