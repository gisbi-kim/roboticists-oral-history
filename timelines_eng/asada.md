# Minoru Asada — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/asada.md`)

```
October 1, 1953   — Born in Shiga Prefecture (Nagahama City), Japan
Elementary (6 yrs) — Attended school in Nagahama City
1st year of MS    — Moved to Toyama Prefecture due to his father's JNR job transfer; attended middle school there for 2 years
High school        — Lived with his older brother in Toyama and attended a top high school (3 years)
Around 1971       — Entered Osaka University, Department of Control Engineering at age 18
Undergrad years   — Married and had a child; took an extra year (5 years total)
Late undergrad    — Joined Prof. Saburo Tsuji's computer vision lab
1982              — Earned PhD in Control Engineering from Osaka University; dissertation on 3D structure recovery from image sequences
After PhD         — Continued mobile robot vision research at Osaka University as a research assistant
1986–1987         — Visited the Center for Automation Research at the University of Maryland for 1 year, 2 months, 10 days; interacted with Azriel Rosenfeld, Larry Davis, and Yiannis Aloimonos; image processing work for the DARPA ALV project
After return      — Vision research with Prof. Yoshiaki Shirai at Osaka University; promoted from lecturer to associate professor
Around 1990       — Transferred to the Department of Mechanical Engineering as an associate professor running his own lab
1992–1993         — Began applying reinforcement learning to soccer robots; started discussing RoboCup with Hiroaki Kitano
1994 ICRA         — Submitted RL soccer robot paper but was rejected → presented at an ICML workshop
1995 ICRA         — Resubmitted the same paper and was selected as a Best Paper finalist (top 10)
1996              — Hosted Pre-RoboCup as IROS chair (Manuela Veloso participated)
1997              — First RoboCup held (Nagoya); co-champion in the middle-size league (tied with USC)
1998              — RoboCup Paris; Sony AIBO demo
1999              — RoboCup Stockholm; first official AIBO league
2002–2008         — President of the international RoboCup Federation
September 2005    — Launched JST ERATO Asada Synergistic Intelligence Project (5.5 years) with Yasuo Kuniyoshi, Hiroshi Ishiguro, and Koh Hosoda; developed child-mimicking humanoids such as CB²
Around 2002–2003  — Began human-oriented research on cognition, emotion, and consciousness
Around 2010       — Proposed RoboCity CoRE concept (Osaka; fell through after a new mayor took office)
2011              — ERATO project ended
2012              — At time of interview: Full Professor at Osaka University, Department of Adaptive Machine Systems; pursuing a new project on self/other concepts and the mirror neuron system
```
