# Maja Mataric — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/mataric.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Belgrade, Yugoslavia (first 16 years in "a country that no longer exists")
~Age 16               — Immigrated to the US
Undergrad             — BS in Computer Science at University of Kansas (minors in neuroscience and commercial design)
Undergrad years       — First encountered robotics through an industrial robotics textbook
Grad school           — Entered MIT AI Lab PhD program; joined Rodney Brooks' lab in first year
MS years              — Initially attempted underwater robotics, then pivoted to cognitive maps; topology-based navigation with Toto robot (inspired by Boston streets)
PhD years             — Distributed multi-robot control research with Nerd Herd (20 robots) in Brooks' lab
1994                  — PhD in EECS from MIT (distributed robotics)
After PhD             — Brandeis University and others, then decided to join USC
Fall 1997             — Joined University of Southern California (recruited by George Bekey); appointed simultaneously with Stefan Schaal
Early at USC          — Removed sand pit for space robots (joint with JPL) and rebuilt lab
1990s~2000s           — Shared and collaborated lab with Gaurav Sukhatme
2000s                 — Began HRI interest at the suggestion of PhD advisee Monica Nicolescu
Late 2000s            — Established socially assistive robotics (SAR) prompted by NSF large center proposal; collaboration with Brian Scassellati on autism
Ongoing               — Robot research targeting children with autism, stroke patients, and Alzheimer's patients
PhD graduates         — Chad Jenkins (Brown), Monica Nicolescu (Reno), Evan Drumwright (Washington U.), Dylan Shell (UT Austin), Brian Gerkey (Willow Garage; Player→ROS), and others — 9 in total
2009                  — Presidential Award for Excellence in Science, Mathematics, and Engineering Mentoring
Platforms             — Used Bandit, NAO, Willow Garage PR2, and others; PR2 too large, raising the need for friendly small humanoids
2010                  — ETHW oral history interview
Later                 — USC Chan Soon-Shiong Distinguished Professor (Computer Science, Neuroscience, and Pediatrics); IEEE Fellow
```
