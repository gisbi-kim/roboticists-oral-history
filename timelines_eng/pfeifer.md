# Rolf Pfeifer — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/pfeifer.md`)

```
Birth               — Born in Zurich, Switzerland
Undergrad–MS        — MS in Physics and Mathematics at ETH Zurich
ETH years           — Co-ran a cybernetics seminar, invited Gordon Pask, Thomas Charles Helvey, and others
After MS            — Joined Ulrich Moser's dream simulation project in the Psychology Department at the University of Zurich (Swiss NSF support, collaboration with a Swedish computational linguistics group)
PhD years           — PhD at ETH Zurich combining Computer Science and Psychology (simulation of cognitive processes)
1982–1984           — Postdoc at Carnegie Mellon University, USA (under Herbert Simon, interacted with John Anderson, Pat Langley, etc.; computational models of emotion)
Afterwards          — Moved to Yale, AI and language research with Robert Abelson and Roger Schank
Around 1987         — Joined the Computer Science faculty at the University of Zurich (in charge of expert systems courses), became director of the AI Lab
After 1987          — Expert systems projects with Schindler, banks, and insurance companies; recognized the limits after inviting William J. Clancey
Sabbatical          — Visited Luc Steels's lab in Brussels, was introduced to artificial evolution and robotics
1991                — Attended the Corsendonk workshop in Belgium (with Brooks, Mataric, Smithers, Hallam, McFarland, Varela, Clancey, etc.)
Early 1990s         — Launched first robotics project, the European "Design Principles for Autonomous Agents" project
1990s               — With Rüdiger Wehner, built robots mimicking Saharan desert ant (cataglyphis) navigation (Dimitri Lambrinos's PhD work)
1995                — Co-organized the "Practicing Future of Autonomous Agents" conference at Monte Verità, Switzerland
1999                — Published "Understanding Intelligence" (10 years of design principles distilled)
2000s               — Soft robotics and material-based intelligence research; many projects on underwater robots, bivalves, hand prostheses, etc.
2009                — Launched the Shanghai Lectures project at Shanghai Jiao Tong University
November 2010       — Opening keynote at IROS Taipei
July 15, 2011       — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro (Zurich)
2014                — Retired from the University of Zurich; continued as professor at Osaka University and visiting professor at Shanghai Jiao Tong University
Afterwards          — Vice director of Switzerland's NCCR Robotics; participated in preparing the EU Robot Companion flagship project
```
