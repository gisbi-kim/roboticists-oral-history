# Tatsuo Arai — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/arai.md`)

```
1952           — Born in Tokyo, Japan
Childhood      — Loved building and taking things apart; strong in math, physics, and chemistry
Undergrad/MS   — Entered the University of Tokyo, Department of Applied Mathematics and Instrumentation Physics
1975           — Earned BS from the University of Tokyo
1977           — Earned MS from the University of Tokyo
1977           — Joined the Mechanical Engineering Laboratory under MITI (now METI), 70 km northeast of Tokyo
Early research — Worked on a patient-care robot project; developed an omnidirectional-wheel mobile robot and ultrasonic localization sensors
1986           — Earned PhD from the University of Tokyo (on walking robots and bilateral teleoperation)
1986–1987      — Visiting researcher at MIT for one year in Tom Sheridan's group; became fascinated by parallel mechanisms (cable-driven), collaborating with Kamal Youcef-Toumi
After 1987     — Returned to Japan and started a parallel-mechanism-based excavation machine project
Early 1990s    — Began research on micro hands (parallel-mechanism-based)
1997           — Appointed full professor at Osaka University, Department of Systems Innovation (Graduate School of Engineering Science)
Osaka era      — Developed a Limb Mechanism (hexapod) integrated mobile/manipulation robot; researched cell assembly via micromanipulation
Around 2000    — The Mechanical Engineering Laboratory was reorganized into AIST
2013           — At time of interview: Professor at Osaka University, leading a 5-year, $2M/year national project across a 9-university consortium
2016           — Elected IEEE Fellow (after the interview, noted in bio)
```
