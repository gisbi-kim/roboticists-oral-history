# Norihiro Hagita — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/hagita.md`)

```
1954                  — Born
Undergrad years       — Entered Keio University, majored in pattern recognition (especially handwritten kanji recognition); used VAX-11/780 minicomputer and Versatec printer
1976                  — Earned B.E. from Keio University
1978                  — Earned M.E. from Keio University; joined NTT (Nippon Telegraph and Telephone Public Corporation)
University years      — First-generation neural network research; submitted a paper on simulating respiration and pain mechanisms to IEEE SMC
1986                  — Earned Ph.D. from Keio University — pattern recognition
After PhD             — Visited Steve Palmer's lab in the Psychology Department at UC Berkeley (visual cognition, neural networks, cognitive psychology); 1-year stay
After return to NTT   — Managed personnel division, ran a postdoctoral researcher recruitment program (hired more than 20 from overseas)
NTT manager years     — Research on agent communication that behaves like humans — integrating speech recognition, image processing, and linguistics
1996–2001             — Executive Manager, NTT Communication Science Labs
2001                  — Moved to ATR (Advanced Telecommunications Research Institute International)
October 2001          — Founded ATR Media Information Science Lab and became its Director
October 2002          — Founded ATR Intelligent Robotics and Communication (IRC) Lab; met many of the world's leading researchers at IROS (EPFL)
2002                  — Developed a Google Glass-like system related to postal computing
ATR years             — Began collaboration with Hiroshi Ishiguro; experimented with Robovie robot for elementary school English learning; speech recognition limits → introduced RFID tags (succeeded in teaching 50 English words in 2 weeks)
2004                  — Launched a new networked robot project after a government proposal
Subsequent work       — Deployed Robovie in supermarkets and city centers, built ambient intelligence systems that track user activity with 5cm precision; collaborated with ASIMO and Paolo Dario's Dustbot
At time of interview  — Director of ATR Intelligent Robotics and Communication Lab, ATR board member and fellow, Chairman of ATR Creative; visiting/adjunct professor at NARA, Osaka, and Kobe universities
November 5, 2013      — Interviewed by Peter Asaro (Tokyo)
```
