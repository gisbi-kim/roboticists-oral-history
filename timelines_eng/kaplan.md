# Frederic Kaplan — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kaplan.md`)

```
Birth           — Born in Paris, France (lived in the Latin Quarter, 5th arrondissement)
School years    — Attended the local school; nurtured AI/robotics as a childhood dream
Undergrad       — Entered Ecole Nationale Superieure des Telecommunications, majoring in artificial intelligence
1997            — Graduated from the telecom school (engineering degree); joined Sony Computer Science Lab Paris (first French employee)
1997–2006       — Researcher at Sony CSL robotics division (10 years)
1997–2000       — PhD candidate at Université Paris 6 (Pierre et Marie Curie) under Luc Steels (multi-agent systems and language evolution)
During PhD      — Talking Heads experiment (robots around the world looking at whiteboards to form a common language)
1999            — Observed natural-language phenomena such as polysemy in the Talking Heads experiment
Late PhD period — Joined the Sony AIBO project; regularly visited the Tokyo Japan lab (Masahiro Fujita, Toshi Doi team)
2000            — PhD in Artificial Intelligence, Université Pierre et Marie Curie
Right after PhD — Brought the first AIBO prototype back to Paris and developed its language and interaction components
Early 2000s     — Collaborated with Pierre-Yves Oudeyer to develop intrinsic motivation systems
~2006           — Decided to leave Sony following the discontinuation of AIBO
2006            — Joined EPFL's CRAFT lab at the invitation of Pierre Dillenbourg
Early EPFL      — Started the Wizkid robotic computer project (collaboration with Martino d'Esposito)
2008            — Wizkid exhibited at the Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York in "Design and the Elastic Mind"
2008            — Co-founded OZWE (interfaces, electronics, digital publishing)
2009            — Released QB1 (gesture-based computer with built-in 3D camera; about 30 units sold at $10,000 each)
2010            — Co-founded Bookapp.com (with Laurent Bolli and Cristiana Freitas)
2012            — Appointed Tenured Professor at EPFL; took the Digital Humanities Chair and became Director of DHLAB
At time of interview — Directing the "Venice Time Machine" project; Editor-in-Chief of Frontiers in Digital Humanities
```
