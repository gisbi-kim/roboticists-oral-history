# Richard Klafter — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/klafter.md`)

```
1936.08.05      — Born in New York City, USA
Primary/secondary years — Attended PS 117 elementary in NYC; graduated from Forest Hills High School
Undergrad       — B.S. (Electrical Engineering), MIT
Graduate school — Took 32 credits in one year at Columbia University, earning two graduate degrees
                — Added 30 more credits beyond the Master's for the Engineering Degree (EE)
Graduate years  — Married with children; began tutoring at Columbia for income and discovered a love of teaching
                — Volunteered as an impromptu lecturer at City College (CUNY) to fill in for a professor who had a heart attack; was hired
1969            — PhD in Electrical Engineering (Automatic Control), City University of New York; advisor George Krantz
Right after PhD — Joined the faculty at Drexel University
Early 1970s     — Was introduced to robotics after hearing a talk by Mitchell Weiss, co-founder of U.S. Robots, at a Philadelphia computer user group meeting
                — Consulted for U.S. Robots (case of diagnosing gear defects)
Early 1980s     — Began writing a robotics textbook at the urging of department chair Bruce Eisenstein
Around 1984     — Moved to Temple University (recruited by his former student Brian Butz)
1986            — Founding member of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Council (then a Council, later a Society)
1989            — Published the co-authored textbook "Robotic Engineering: An Integrated Approach" (with Tom Chmielewski and others, the field's first comprehensive textbook)
Late 1980s      — U.S. robotics mini-recession (17 years at Drexel, 17.5 years at Temple)
1986            — Elected IEEE Fellow (for contributions to robotics education and ECE graduate education)
1990s           — Mobile robot projects — high-rise window-washing robot, self-reconfiguring articulated rectangular grid structure for space
                — Research on applying optimal control to cardiac pacemakers (NSF supported)
1994–1995       — President, IEEE Robotics and Automation Society
1994            — Launched the IEEE Robotics and Automation Magazine during his presidential term
1996            — IEEE Fellow → Life Fellow
After retirement — Volunteered for Recordings for the Blind and Dyslexic (now Learning Ally), reading textbooks aloud
2019.11.11      — Passed away
```
