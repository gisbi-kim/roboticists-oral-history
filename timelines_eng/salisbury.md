# Ken Salisbury — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/salisbury.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Schenectady, NY (father Kenneth Salisbury was head of GE's steam turbine division)
Childhood             — Family moved to California two years later; primary/secondary schooling in the Atherton/Menlo Park area
Around age 6          — Built his first finger model by hand
College entrance      — Entered Stanford University (initially Mechanical Engineering)
Undergrad years       — Switched to Electrical Engineering at the urging of friend Rob Young
1975                  — Earned B.S. in Electrical Engineering from Stanford
1977                  — Earned M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford
PhD years             — Advised by Bernie Roth; designed a 3-finger robotic hand in collaboration with Carl Ruoff at NASA/JPL
1982                  — Earned Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Stanford; dissertation introduced the Stanford/JPL Hand (later known as the Salisbury Hand)
1982                  — Joined MIT AI Lab as a postdoctoral researcher (a planned one-year stay that became 17 years)
1982~1999             — Principal Research Scientist in MIT Mechanical Engineering and member of the AI Lab
MIT years             — Commercialized the Salisbury Hand and sold roughly 20 units (founded a small company)
MIT years             — With first PhD student Bill Townsend, developed the WAM (Whole Arm Manipulation) arm
MIT years             — With David Brock (first PhD student), developed a 4-sensing-finger tactile sensor
MIT years             — With Brian Eberman, researched material/contact event estimation from force information
MIT years             — Shifted to haptics research under the influence of Mandayam Srinivasan
MIT years             — With undergraduate Thomas Massie, developed the PHANTOM haptic interface
MIT years             — Co-founded SensAble Technologies (led by Massie, supported by Salisbury)
MIT years             — Designed a curling hand with Helen Greiner (later co-founder of iRobot)
MIT years             — With Akhil Madhani, developed the Silver Falcon and Black Falcon surgical robot prototypes
MIT years             — Akhil Madhani led the ball-catching arm system (co-authored with Niemeyer and Hong)
1997                  — Joined Intuitive Surgical as Fellow and Scientific Advisor
Fall 1999             — Returned to Stanford as Research Professor in Computer Science and Surgery (with courtesy appointment in Mechanical Engineering)
After Stanford return — Collaborated with Dr. Krummel (Surgery) on an NIH-funded surgical simulation project of 4-5 years
Stanford period       — With Keenan Wyrobek and Eric Berger, developed the PR1 (Personal Robot 1) prototype
Stanford period       — After roughly 40 pitches, found a backer and launched Willow Garage (laying the foundation for PR2 and ROS)
Stanford period       — Collaborated with Dr. Nick Blevins (otolaryngology) on mastoidectomy simulation
Stanford period       — With Sonny Chan, researched high-resolution CT/MRI fused skull rendering
Stanford period       — Student Kevin Loewke co-founded a company based on IVF embryo maturity tracking technology
Stanford period       — David Camarillo earned his PhD on robotic catheter accuracy research
Stanford period       — With Curt Salisbury and others, developed a cranial-facial 6-DoF haptic interface
DARPA period          — DARPA low-cost hand project for IED disposal via Sandia National Labs, in collaboration with Curt Salisbury (PI)
November 18, 2010     — ETHW oral history interview conducted at Stanford
```
