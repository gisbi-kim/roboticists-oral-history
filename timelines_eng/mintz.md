# Max Mintz — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/mintz.md`)

```
1942-09-04        — Born about three months after the Battle of Midway; grew up just outside New York City
Childhood (age 10)— Bought vacuum tube parts on Cortlandt Street's Radio Row (now the World Trade Center site)
Childhood         — Earned an amateur radio license
Age 18            — Entered Cornell University's electrical engineering program (attended for 8 years)
1965              — Began BS, MS, and PhD work at Cornell (control theory of stochastic systems)
1965-11-22~23     — Was a graduate student in Ithaca during the Northeast blackout
1965–1968         — Graduate research in differential games
1968–1969         — Postdoctoral fellow at Yale University
Around 1969       — Assistant professor at Yale (control theory and electrical engineering)
1972–1974         — Taught in the CSL group at the University of Illinois
1974              — Joined the University of Pennsylvania's Electrical Systems Engineering department
1976–1986         — Worked with the U.S. Air Force on heuristic algorithms for multi-missile evasion
Around 1979       — Ruzena Bajcsy started the GRASP Lab (with a single-finger tactile sensor in the basement)
1984              — Joined the GRASP Lab at Ruzena Bajcsy's invitation
1986              — Switched affiliation to UPenn's Computer and Information Science department
After 1986        — Began advising PhD students (Greg Hager was the first CIS PhD student)
Around 1988       — Greg Hager earned his PhD (research on active information acquisition)
1990–1995         — Multi-agent research with TRC LabMate mobile robots (collaboration with Ruzena Bajcsy, Vijay Kumar, Lou Paul)
1995              — Robbie Mandelbaum earned his PhD
After the 2000s   — Shifted focus to quantum computation research; teaching undergraduates
Around 2007       — UPenn's robotics master's program established (led by George Pappas, Vijay Kumar, Kostas Daniilidis)
2011-05-24        — IEEE History Center oral history interview (Philadelphia, PA), at 27 years with the GRASP Lab
```
