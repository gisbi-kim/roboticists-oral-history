# George Pappas — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/pappas.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in New York, USA (to Greek parents)
Age 5                 — Returned to Greece with family
Childhood–high school — Completed primary, middle, and high school education in Greece (dual citizen)
Undergrad years       — Entered Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) in the US, majored in Computer & Systems Engineering (influenced by the NASA Center on Space Robotics)
Around sophomore year — Decided on a control/robotics career after a difficult control engineering exam (encouraged by Joe Chow)
1991                  — BS from RPI
1992                  — MS from RPI (advisor Kostas Kyriakopoulos, research on parallel parking algorithms)
Starting PhD          — Entered UC Berkeley (advisor Shankar Sastry)
During PhD            — Research on hybrid systems and air traffic management
During PhD            — One year of visiting research at Harvard
1996                  — Exposed to automated highway environments via PATH platoon demonstrations on Highway 5 near UC San Diego
1998                  — PhD in EECS from UC Berkeley
1998                  — Joined University of Pennsylvania as assistant professor, joined the GRASP Lab
Early 2000s           — Shifted research from single robots to multi-robot systems, began exploring biological interfaces
2005–2008             — Director of GRASP Lab, established the Master's in Robotics program
After 2008            — UPenn Electrical & Systems Engineering chair, Joseph Moore Professor, etc. (joint appointments in CIS/MEAM, member of PRECISE Center)
May 23, 2011          — ETHW oral history interview with Selma Šabanović and Peter Asaro (Philadelphia)
Afterwards            — Served as Deputy Dean for Research, School of Engineering and Applied Science
```
