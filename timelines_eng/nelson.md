# Brad Nelson — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/nelson.md`)

```
Birth             — Born in Murphysboro, Illinois, USA
Childhood         — Grew up in a small Illinois town; attended high school in Normal
1969-07           — At age 7, watched the Apollo 11 Moon landing and became fascinated with engineering
Undergrad         — BS in mechanical engineering from the University of Illinois (Urbana-Champaign)
1984              — Began MS in mechanical engineering at the University of Minnesota (advised by Max Donath)
1984~ (during MS) — Worked on Honeywell's DARPA Intelligent Task Automation project, assembling microswitches
MS thesis         — "Optimal Location of Assembly Tasks in a Manipulator Workspace"
After MS          — U.S. Peace Corps volunteer in Botswana
Later             — Software engineer at Motorola working on wireless digital communications
1990              — Entered Carnegie Mellon University's robotics PhD program (advised by Pradeep Khosla)
PhD               — Researched vision-force fusion servoing using the Troikabot (three PUMA 560s)
PhD thesis        — "Port-Based Agents for Assimilating Disparate Sensory Feedback"
1995              — PhD in robotics from CMU
1995              — Appointed assistant professor in mechanical engineering at the University of Illinois at Chicago ($30,000 startup)
1995              — At IROS Pittsburgh, encountered micromanipulation papers by Fumi Arai/Toshio Fukuda and Ron Fearing; pivoted directions
1995              — Built first microrobotics system by modifying a Wentworth probe station
1998              — Moved to the University of Minnesota as associate professor (took advantage of microfab facilities)
Around 1998       — Met Yu Sun in China and recruited him as a PhD student
2002              — Demonstrated a system for manipulating individual biological cells
Fall 2002–2003    — Moved to ETH Zurich as a full professor of robotics and intelligent systems (relocated with 17 people)
2003              — Began wireless microrobot development (with Karl Vollmers, Berk Yesin, and others)
2005              — Selected for the "Scientific American 50"
2007, 2009        — Won the RoboCup Nanogram Competition
2007–2008         — Felix Beyeler founded the FemtoTools spinoff
Fall 2010         — Co-founded the ANT Scientific (now Aeon Scientific) spinoff
2011-07-17        — IEEE History Center oral history interview (Zurich, Switzerland), 8.5 years into ETH tenure
Ongoing           — Editor of IEEE Transactions on Nanotechnology
```
