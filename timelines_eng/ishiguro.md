# Hiroshi Ishiguro — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/ishiguro.md`)

```
Childhood/teens      — Born and grew up in Shiga Prefecture
Undergrad            — Computer Science at University of Yamanashi; once aspired to be a professional oil painter → gave up
Graduate school      — Systems Engineering at Osaka University — control theory and AI; advised by Prof. Saburo Tsuji (computer vision pioneer)
1991                 — D.Eng. from Osaka University — active vision and omni-directional vision
After PhD            — Research Associate in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Yamanashi University
1992                 — Returned to Systems Engineering at Osaka University
Around 1990          — Researched early form of SLAM (self-localization and mapping using omni-directional camera)
1994                 — Associate Professor of Information Science, Kyoto University — advised by Prof. Ishida; advice to "stop writing papers and build something that will change the world"; started two projects on interactive robots and sensor networks
1998–1999            — Visiting scholar at UCSD Electrical and Computer Engineering — interactions with Mohan Trivedi, Ramesh Jain, Don Norman, Sejnowski, etc.
1999                 — Visiting researcher at ATR Media Information Science Laboratories — began Robovie development (3-person group)
2000                 — Associate Professor of Computer and Communication Sciences at Wakayama University
2001                 — Full Professor at Wakayama University
Around 2001          — Started Android project — first android modeled on his own daughter
Subsequent return to Osaka — Department of Systems Innovation, Osaka University (Graduate School of Engineering Science)
Elementary school experiments — Introduced Robovie at elementary schools to support English learning (recalled as the world's first attempt)
2004                 — Developed second android (female form) with Aichi Expo funding
Subsequently         — Developed Geminoid (his own copy), Telenoid, Elfoid, etc.; collaborated with Kokoro
2009                 — Current Professor position at Department of Systems Innovation, Osaka University
2011                 — ATR Hiroshi Ishiguro Lab Group Leader + ATR Fellow
Theatre collaboration — Android theatre with Oriza Hirata (Seinendan theatre company) — well received in France
May 17, 2012         — Interviewed by Peter Asaro and Selma Sabanovic (St. Paul, MN)
At time of interview — Recruiting cognitive and neuroscientists; trainees like Doctor Kanda grew into ATR group leaders
```
