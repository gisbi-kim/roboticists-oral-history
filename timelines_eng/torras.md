# Carme Torras — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/torras.md`)

```
1956              — Born in Barcelona, Spain, one of 5 siblings, father a chemist
Undergrad years   — Double major in mathematics and philosophy at Universitat de Barcelona, focus on applied mathematics and computer science
After undergrad   — Took a job in industry
Motivation for grad school — After reading Michael Arbib's "Brains, Machines and Mathematics" and Frank Rosenblatt's "Perceptron", decided to study in the US
1979~1981         — University of Massachusetts Amherst on a Fulbright scholarship, advised by Michael Arbib (Brain Theory program)
PhD program       — Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC), temporal learning model of crayfish neurons
PhD               — PhD in neural modeling at UPC (published by Springer-Verlag)
Right after PhD   — Returned to Barcelona; with neurophysiology research difficult, accepted a robotics position (used Puma robot)
1985              — Organized IFAC symposium (Spain) (4 years after returning, attended by Robert Kelly, Michael Arbib, Rüdiger Dillmann)
1992~1995         — Conducted 4 European projects including B-LEARN (PROMotion led by Jean-Paul Laumond, motion planning)
Later             — Continued collaboration with Rüdiger Dillmann's B-LEARN and follow-ups
Later             — CONNY project (led by Daimler-Benz Aerospace), developed self-calibration algorithm for space robots
Later             — Thomson collaboration on neural network system for aircraft wing inspection (holds patent)
2007              — Published first SF novel "The Sentimental Mutation" (won Pedrolo and Ictineu prizes; later translated to English as The Vestigial Heart)
2010~2013         — GARNICS European project (plant phenotyping robot)
2011~2014         — IntellAct European project (intelligent action, space station assembly demo)
At time of interview — Senior Editor of IEEE Transactions on Robotics, IEEE RAS VP for Publications
At time of interview — Supervised 13 PhDs, kept 5 at the same institute
2015. 1. 23.      — Interview with Selma Šabanović conducted in College Station, Texas (during ICRA all-women organizing committee)
At time of interview — Research Professor at IRI (CSIC-UPC), researching deformable object manipulation (clothing, etc.) and assistive robots
After (post-interview) — Carried out ERC Advanced Grant CLOTHILDE
2018              — "The Vestigial Heart" published in English by MIT Press
```
