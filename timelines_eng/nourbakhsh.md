# Illah Nourbakhsh — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/nourbakhsh.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Tehran, Iran (parents were UK/US-educated)
Early childhood       — Early childhood and lower-elementary education in Iran
Around 2nd–3rd grade  — Moved to Platte City, Missouri (rural town of 300), commuted to Pembroke Country Day (private) school in Kansas City through high school
Childhood             — Captivated by R2D2 after watching Star Wars, modified a Simon game to build a motor-driven robot (also used Timex Sinclair zx81 and TRS-80)
Undergrad years       — Entered Stanford University (the only school he was admitted to), passed through comparative literature and organic chemistry before majoring in Computer Science
Undergrad years       — Took Michael Genesereth's "Agents" course and switched to AI/robotics
1991                  — With Mike Genesereth, purchased the second Nomadic Technologies robot (running Common Lisp on a Mac PowerBook)
1992                  — Mapped Stanford's campus quad to 1/10-inch precision, started the Book Store Project ("Shallow Thought" planner)
1992                  — Graduated undergrad; on the verge of taking a Bell Labs job, was persuaded by Genesereth to enter the PhD program
1992                  — BS in Computer Science from Stanford (continued on to Stanford grad school)
1994                  — MS from Stanford
PhD years             — Earned pilot's license, instrument rating, and helicopter pilot's license; took up classical guitar
PhD years             — Entered AAAI contest with the robot Dervish, interacted with John McCarthy, Carlo Tomasi, and others
1996                  — PhD from Stanford (Computer Science)
Around 1996           — Co-founded Blue Pumpkin Software with Mike Genesereth (scheduling SW), targeting the call-center market
January 1997          — Joined Carnegie Mellon University as assistant professor (the only faculty offer he received)
1997–1998             — Joined Chuck Thorpe's Automated Highway System (AHS) project (developed a "universal plan")
1997–2005             — Founder and Chief Scientist of Blue Pumpkin Software
Early CMU             — Began the Carnegie Museum of Natural History Dinosaur Hall tour-guide robot project (XR4000, ran for 5 years)
CMU era               — Developed open-source educational robotics projects such as Palm Pilot Robot Kit and CMUcam
CMU era               — Developed the Insect Telepresence museum exhibit
2004                  — CMU sabbatical, served as leader of the Intelligent Robotics Group at NASA Ames Research Center (~1.5 years)
Around 2004           — First daughter born (while living in San Francisco)
2009                  — Selected as a Kavli Fellow of the US National Academy of Sciences
November 23, 2010     — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro and Selma Šabanović (Pittsburgh)
2013                  — Inducted into the Junes Harless West Virginia Hall of Fame
```
