# Daniel Lee — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/lee_daniel.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born near San Francisco, California (his father was a Stanford grad student at the time)
Childhood             — Moved to and grew up in the Upper Peninsula of Michigan
1990                  — BS in Physics from Harvard University
Grad school years     — Took LEGO robot class at MIT and competed (during MS/PhD studies)
1995                  — PhD in Condensed Matter Physics from MIT (X-ray and neutron scattering research)
1995                  — Joined AT&T Bell Labs as postdoctoral researcher; spent 6 years in Theoretical Physics and Biological Computation departments
Bell Labs years       — Built small robot dogs in-house (before Sony AIBO release), researched walking algorithms; developed machine learning algorithms including sparse coding
2001                  — Joined University of Pennsylvania (UPS Foundation Professor of Transportation, Electrical & Systems Engineering); moved to academia after divesting company stock just before the dot-com crash
Just after joining    — Joined GRASP Lab (just before Bajcsy left, with Vijay Kumar newly appointed as Director); took over RoboCup team (soccer with Sony AIBO)
2004                  — NSF Presidential Early Career Award
2006                  — Lindback Award for Distinguished Teaching
2007                  — Led Penn team to complete DARPA Urban Challenge (experienced LIDAR problems due to desert dust storms)
2008~2011             — Evan C. Thompson Term Professor for Distinguished Teaching
Later                 — Participated in DARPA Robotics Challenge; collaborated with Dennis Hong's group and Korean companies on bipedal humanoid
PhD graduates         — First was Yuanqin Lin (Director at NEC Research Labs), Jihun Hamm (Ohio State University), and others
GRASP Director        — Served as GRASP Lab Director for ~2 years (Vijay Kumar → George Pappas → Kostas Daniilidis → Daniel Lee)
Ongoing               — Co-Director of CMU-Penn University Transportation Center (autonomous driving research)
2014                  — ETHW oral history interview (Chicago, IL)
```
