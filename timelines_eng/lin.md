# Ming Lin — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/lin.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Taiwan
1980                  — Immigrated to California, USA, with family
High school           — Sunny Hill High School in California
Early undergrad       — 2 years at UCLA (close to home, took broad general education and engineering courses)
Late undergrad        — Transferred to UC Berkeley; BS in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EE/CS)
Grad school           — MS and PhD at UC Berkeley; advised by John Canny (1st-year fellowship allowed free exploration)
PhD years             — Started with robot motion planning, shifted to collision detection algorithms
~1993                 — PhD in EE/CS from UC Berkeley (known for the Lin-Canny algorithm)
Just after graduation — Faculty appointment at Naval Postgraduate School (Monterey) (1 year)
After 1 year          — After marriage, moved to NCNT (HBCU near Greensboro, NC State area) as visiting professor → tenure track (1-hour commute)
1994                  — NSF Young Faculty Career Award (one of three recipients)
~1995                 — Moved to US Army Research Office (ARO) as founding Computer Science program manager (~2.5 years)
While at ARO          — Joint appointment at UNC Chapel Hill via Staff Research Program; continued to advise students
1997                  — Formally joined UNC Chapel Hill (chosen after competing with Michigan, together with husband Dinesh Manocha)
UNC years             — Released collision detection libraries I-COLLIDE, RAPID, V-COLLIDE, PQP (used by ~50 companies)
1997                  — Honda Research Initiation Award
~2000                 — Started haptics research after contact with SensAble Technologies (MIT spinoff); collaboration with Ken Salisbury and Elaine Chen
2000s                 — Haptic rendering and multi-sensory modality research (~7–8 years)
2010                  — IEEE VGTC VR Technical Achievement Award
2011~2014             — Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics
2012                  — IEEE Fellow
2015                  — IEEE Computer Society Board of Governors and Chair of Transactions Operations Committee; ETHW oral history interview (College Station, Texas)
Later                 — UNC John R. & Louise S. Parker Distinguished Professor, then Elizabeth Stevinson Iribe Chair at University of Maryland College Park
Ongoing               — Holds honorary professorship at Tsinghua University; co-organized ICRA workshop "Boundary Between Virtual Reality and Robotics"
```
