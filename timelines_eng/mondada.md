# Francesco Mondada — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/mondada.md`)

```
1967-03-17        — Born in Locarno, Switzerland (Italian-speaking region)
Childhood         — Learned mechanical work in the workshop of his father, a mechanics teacher
Around 1981       — Won a Commodore VIC-20 in a contest; began programming
Before college    — Built a robot arm with a Commodore Plus/4
1986              — Entered EPFL, majoring in microtechnique (systems engineering)
Early undergrad   — First project in Nicoud's lab: a 5-motor 6-legged robot (failed to operate)
Senior project    — Juggling robot based on an HC11 microcontroller (inclined surface, 25 fps vision processing)
1991              — MS in micro-engineering from EPFL (Nicoud lab)
After 1991        — Started as a regular assistant in Nicoud's lab (without intending to pursue a PhD)
1992–1993         — Developed the first Khepera robot prototype (jointly with Edo Franzi and André Guignard)
During PhD        — Researched applying neural networks and bio-inspired algorithms to mobile robots
During PhD        — Collaborated with Dario Floreano, Paul Verschure, Rolf Pfeifer, and others
1995              — Co-founded the company K-Team (began selling Khepera at 3,000 Swiss francs)
1997              — Earned PhD from EPFL (Khepera robot was central)
1997–1999         — Split time 50% K-Team, 50% EPFL
1999–2000         — Full-time CEO of K-Team for one year
2000              — Realized he was not suited to be CEO and returned to EPFL research
Early 2000s       — Started the Swarm-bots / S-bot project (with André Guignard, Michael Bonani)
2000s             — Worked on the Swarmanoid project (self-assembling robots)
Late 2000s        — Developed the E-puck educational robot (open source, sold thousands)
Later             — Founded the non-profit Mobsya; developed the Thymio educational robot
Later             — Animal-robot interaction research with Jean-Louis Deneubourg (Brussels) (cockroaches, chicks)
2011-07-18        — IEEE History Center oral history interview (Lausanne, Switzerland)
Present           — Associate professor of robotics at EPFL; researches mechatronic design of mobile robots
```
