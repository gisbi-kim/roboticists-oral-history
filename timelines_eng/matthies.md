# Larry Matthies — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/matthies.md`)

```
Birth                    — Born in North Battleford, Saskatchewan, Canada; eldest of five siblings
12th grade               — Took an 11th-grade computer science course; shifted interest from chemistry to computing
Undergrad years          — Earned bachelor's degree at the University of Saskatchewan
Master's program         — MSc in computer science at the University of Waterloo
Fall 1981                — Entered Carnegie Mellon University PhD program
Fall 1982                — Began computer vision research at CMU (Hans Moravec's mobile robot lab)
During PhD               — Advisor changed from Hans Moravec to Steve Schaffer, with co-advising by Takeo Kanade
During PhD               — Researched stereo-vision-based mobile robot autonomous navigation (origin of visual odometry)
During PhD               — Co-authored an occupancy grid multi-sensor fusion paper with Hans Moravec and Alberto Elfes
1989                     — Earned PhD from CMU and joined JPL as a Senior Research Scientist
Right after 1989         — Joined the Mars Rover Sample Return (MRSR) program; developed stereo vision algorithms (cut runtime from half an hour to 6 seconds)
Around 1989–1992         — After MRSR was cancelled, shifted toward microrobotics
Mid-1990s                — Secured funding for landing hazard detection research and built up a team
1997                     — Supported the Mars Pathfinder mission (Sojourner rover); helped debug the Light Striper sensor
1990s–2000s              — Worked on near-infrared/LIDAR vegetation classification under DARPA Demo II and PerceptOR programs
Around 2003–2004         — Led an emergency development of a horizontal-velocity estimation camera system for Mars Exploration Rover (MER) landing
21 years                 — Served as head of the JPL Computer Vision Group
2 years                  — Coordinated internal technology investments at the Mars Office
2008                     — Co-recipient of the IEEE Robotics and Automation Award (for contributions to robotic space exploration)
2011                     — IEEE History Center oral history interview (Pasadena, CA)
Ongoing                  — Adjunct professor of computer science at USC; teaches computer vision at Caltech
Ongoing                  — Editorial board member of Autonomous Robots and the Journal of Field Robotics
```
