# Danica Kragic — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kragic.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Croatia, educated there through high school
High school years     — Studies focused on programming and mathematics
1995                  — BS in Mechanical Engineering at Technical University of Rijeka
1997                  — Entered PhD program at KTH Royal Institute of Technology in Sweden; founding member of Center for Autonomous Systems (the only woman among 8–10 PhDs hired simultaneously)
PhD years             — Advised by Henrik Christensen and Jan-Olof Eklundh; research on robot vision, object recognition, and grasping using PUMA and Nomadic XR-4000
Late PhD              — 6–7 month visit to Peter Allen's group at Columbia University; research on model-based real-time tracking and grasping
2001                  — PhD in Computer Science from KTH
2001~                 — Postdoc/Assistant Professor at KTH
2003                  — Joined Gregory Hager's group at Johns Hopkins University; research on human-machine collaborative systems for microsurgery
After return          — Returned to KTH as Assistant Professor
2007                  — Promoted to full Professor at KTH; received IEEE RAS Early Academic Career Award
2008                  — Started EU project GRASP (4 years, ~8 million euros, 6–7 universities + Otto-Bock and others; prosthetics and human hand modeling)
Visit                 — Visiting researcher at INRIA Rennes
Later                 — Appointed Director of Centre for Autonomous Systems (expanded to ~25 PhDs)
Projects              — TOMSY (topology-based motion planning, ended 2014); CENTAURO (centaur-type robot with exoskeleton teleoperation)
2012                  — Received ERC Starting Grant
Ongoing               — Multi-sensor integration and tactile-based object modeling research; supported student startups including Volumental (3D scanning)
2015                  — ETHW oral history interview (College Station, Texas; while raising 7-year-old autistic son and 10-month-old daughter)
2019                  — Distinguished Professor Grant from Swedish Research Council
Ongoing               — Chair of IEEE RAS Computer & Robot Vision Technical Committee, AdCom member, ICRA/IROS Editor-in-Chief, etc.
```
