# Roland Siegwart — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/siegwart.md`)

```
1959                          — Born in a mountainous rural region of Switzerland
Elementary school             — Met a creative teacher (paper bridges, matchstick towers, etc.) and decided on engineering as a career
High school transition        — Chose engineering after weighing medical school (emergency surgery) vs. engineering
University                    — Entered ETH Zurich, majoring in Mechanical Engineering
1983                          — Earned Diploma in Mechanical Engineering from ETH Zurich
PhD period                    — Advised by Gerhard Schweitzer; mechatronics research based on magnetic bearings
PhD period                    — Partly involved in a ping-pong robot in the next-door lab (one of the world's fastest robots at the time)
1989                          — Earned Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering/Mechatronics from ETH Zurich
1989~1990                     — Postdoctoral researcher at Stanford University; nano-robotics with Larry Leifer/Mark Cutkosky
1990                          — Returned to ETH; established a new hands-on robot building course
1991~1996                     — Part-time R&D Director at MECOS Traxler AG (magnetic bearing spinoff) + lecturer/deputy head at ETH Robotics Institute
Around 1991~1992              — First robotics conference attendance
Early 1990s                   — Service robotics projects with Schweitzer including a mail delivery robot (office building)
Early 1990s                   — Robotics lead of the ETH Poly-Project (nanorobotics) (in collaboration with Olaf Kübler)
1996                          — Appointed Full Professor of Autonomous Microsystems at EPFL (Lausanne)
EPFL period                   — Interaction with Jean-Daniel Nicoud, Reymond Clavel (inventor of the Delta robot), and others
EPFL period                   — Developed the Alice micro robot (won the Japan maze contest)
EPFL period                   — Cockroach group behavior manipulation experiments using small cube robots (related to Science publication)
EPFL period                   — Aerial robotics research including a Quadrotor / 2m wingspan solar aircraft (continuous 24-hour flight)
2002                          — Hosted IROS 2002 in Lausanne
2002                          — Expo.02 (Swiss national exposition): operated 11 tour-guide robots for 5 months, serving about 700,000 visitors
2002~2006                     — Director of the EPFL School of Engineering
2002~2006                     — Founding chairman of the Space Center
2004~2005                     — IEEE RAS Vice President for Technical Activities
2006~2007                     — IEEE RAS Distinguished Lecturer
July 2006                     — Returned to ETH Zurich as Full Professor of Autonomous Systems; many PhD students moved with him
After ETH return              — Researched a quadruped walking robot (elastic elements + electric motors)
After ETH return              — Formed IRIS (Institute of Robotics and Intelligent Systems) with Brad Nelson, Robert Riener, Roger Gassert, Fumiya Iida, and others
January 2010~December 2014    — Vice President of Research and Corporate Relations at ETH Zurich (Executive Board)
Around 2010                   — Spawned multiple spinoffs including Bluebotics (Lausanne) and Alstom Inspection Robotics (Zurich)
Around 2010                   — Established a specialized Master's program in Robotics, Systems and Control
Around fall 2010              — Launch of the Swiss National Centre of Competence in Robotics (NCCR), centered on EPFL+ETH
July 15, 2011                 — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro in Zurich
```
