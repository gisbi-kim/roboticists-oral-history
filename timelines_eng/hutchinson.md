# Seth Hutchinson — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/hutchinson.md`)

```
Birth               — Born in Sellersburg, Indiana
High school years   — Amplifier broke during rock band activity → decided on electrical engineering career
1983                — B.S. in Electrical Engineering, Purdue University
1984                — M.S. in Electrical Engineering, Purdue
PhD program         — Avi Kak's computer vision lab at Purdue; viewpoint planning research using a Cincinnati Milacron donated robot (used the robot as a tool for the vision project)
1988                — Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering, Purdue — planning under uncertainty
1988~1989           — Visiting professor at Purdue for 1 year
January 1990        — Joined University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) Coordinated Science Lab and Beckman Institute as Research Assistant Professor
1990                — ECE Assistant Professor; began research on visual servo control (influenced by John Feddema during Purdue years); pursued planning research in parallel
UIUC colleagues     — Mark Spong (control theory-based robotics), Narendra Ahuja (vision), Jean Ponce (vision, joined around the same time)
Around 1986         — First ICRA attendance (San Francisco) — first plane ride and trip to the West Coast
1996                — Visual Servo Control tutorial paper with Hager and Corke (IEEE Transactions on Robotics) — based on conference workshop and tutorial materials
1996~2003           — UIUC Research Associate Professor / Associate Professor
1997~1998           — Sabbatical at Ecole Nationale Supérieure des Télécommunications in Paris
Late 1990s          — 6-month sabbatical with François Chaumette in Rennes
Subsequently        — UIUC Research Professor / Professor (until present)
Mid-2000s           — Hired 3~4 junior robotics specialists at UIUC — full-scale build-out of robotics group
PhD students        — Steve LaValle (co-inventor of RRT), Nick Gans (Texas), Sourabh Bhattacharya (Iowa State), Becky Castaño (JPL), and many who went to Google
Collaborators       — Peter Corke (Australia, "shoemaker's elves" style time-difference collaboration), Mark Spong, Rafael Murrieta (Mexico)
At time of interview — On sabbatical with Alessandro De Luca's group in Rome (impedance control)
November 5, 2013    — Interviewed by Peter Asaro (Tokyo)
```
