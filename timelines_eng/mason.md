# Matt Mason — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/mason.md`)

```
1952                  — Born in Oklahoma City, USA; grew up there
1970                  — Entered MIT just before turning 18 (started in physics/mathematics → switched to computer science)
High school years (1968~69) — Tagged along with brother to Stanford summer program, audited computer classes; developed interest in computer science
1976                  — BS in Computer Science from MIT
Start of grad school  — Shared office with Marc Raibert, John Hollerbach, and Shimon Ullman; introduced to robotics
~1977                 — MS thesis on compliant motion and force control (theoretical, published in IEEE Trans. SMC)
1978                  — MS in AI from MIT
After MS              — Internship at IBM Yorktown Heights; implemented force control system on RS1 arm with Russ Taylor
PhD years             — Advised by Berthold Horn; PhD on mechanics of pushing
1982                  — PhD in AI from MIT (mechanics of pushing/manipulation)
1982                  — Joined Carnegie Mellon University (at Raibert's recommendation)
Around PhD time       — Fine motion planning project (so-called LMT) with Russ Taylor and Tomas Lozano-Perez
1983                  — Systems Development Foundation Prize
1980s                 — Joint research on tray tilting (orientation determination without sensors) with Mike Erdmann
Later                 — Research on learning-based manipulation with Alan Christiansen, influenced by Tom Mitchell's arrival
PhD graduates         — Yu Wang (Michael Wong), Randy Brost, Ken Goldberg, Alan Christiansen, Kevin Lynch, Wes Huang, Garth Zeglin, Devin Balkcom, Amir Degani, and others
1994~1995             — Two summers as visiting researcher at Sandia National Labs
1995~2004             — Chair of CMU Robotics Doctoral Program
2000                  — IEEE Fellow
2004~2014             — Director of Robotics Institute (succeeding Chuck Thorpe)
2007                  — Provided RI administrative support for DARPA Urban Challenge; Red Whittaker's team won
2009                  — IEEE Robotics and Automation Society Pioneer Award
~2010                 — Received Willow Garage PR2 (limited by simple hand)
2010                  — ETHW oral history interview (Pittsburgh, PA); "simple hands" research underway
2018                  — IEEE Robotics and Automation Award
```
