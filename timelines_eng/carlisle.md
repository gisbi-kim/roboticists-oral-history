# Brian Carlisle — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/carlisle.md`)

```
Birth/upbringing      — Born in San Francisco; grew up in San Francisco, Marin County, and the Peninsula (grandfather was a pattern maker for diesel locomotives)
Adolescence           — Immersed in model railroading from age 16 to 18
Undergrad             — ME at Stanford (advised by Bernie Roth)
1975                  — MS in EE from Stanford (focused on servomechanisms and microprocessor control); met Vic Scheinman and Bruce Shimano
1975                  — Joined Vic Scheinman and Bruce Shimano at Vicarm; on his birthday (February), delivered the Stanford Arm to GM Research Lab (Troy, MI) through a snowstorm
Late 1976             — GM issued the PUMA RFQ; Vicarm was sold to Unimation, which won the PUMA contract
Early 1978            — Delivered the first PUMA prototype to GM (later donated the 2003 prototype to the Smithsonian)
1978–early 1980s      — Ran the R&D group at Unimation, based in California
1981                  — Demoed machine vision integrated with a 6-axis robot (then a first in the industry)
Around 1980           — Licensed SRI vision technology at Unimation; recruited Caltech alum Scott Roth
1983                  — Westinghouse acquired Unimation ($107M); integration turmoil
1983                  — Co-founded Adept Technology with Bruce Shimano; started with 27 people; licensed direct-drive motor technology
1984                  — Launched the Adept One (first-year revenue $14M)
1996                  — Adept went public; acquired SILMA (co-founded by John Craig)
Late 1990s            — Adept revenue passed $100M
1999                  — Major photonics automation project with JDS Uniphase; received $25M investment
2000                  — Y2K and dot-com bust caused revenue to plunge from $120M to about $50M
2000–2003             — Adept downsized
2000                  — Co-chaired ICRA 2000 (San Francisco) with Oussama Khatib
2003                  — After raising $10M from Special Situations Fund, a new CEO was brought in; Carlisle and Shimano were fired about three weeks later
2003                  — Co-founded Precise Automation with Shimano (life sciences, semiconductor, and disk drive sectors)
November 21, 2010     — IEEE oral history interview in Auburn, CA (Precise Automation revenue around $4M, growing 100%)
RIA activity          — Served as a board member and three-term President of the Robotic Industries Association
```
