# Stefan Schaal — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/schaal.md`)

```
Birth                  — Born in Germany
University~PhD years   — Studied Mechanical Engineering and AI at the Technical University of Munich
PhD period             — Advised by Klaus Ehrlenspiel (Department for Engineering Design); CAD/AI-based design research
Late 1980s             — Began postdoctoral work amid the new wave of neural network/computational motor control at MIT
1988                   — Earned Diploma (M.S. equivalent) in Mechanical Engineering from TU Munich
1991                   — Earned Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering + AI from TU Munich
1991~1994              — Postdoctoral researcher in the MIT Brain & Cognitive Sciences department and AI Lab
MIT years              — Under Chris Atkeson, developed juggling machines including a 5-ball bounce juggler
MIT years              — Worked alongside Marc Raibert, Emilio Bizzi, and others
1992                   — Began collaboration with the ATR Japan research lab (Mitsuo Kawato's group)
1992                   — Attended the Santa Fe Complex Systems Summer School
1994~2002              — Spent at least about 4 months per year at ATR in Japan
1996                   — Kawato's group secured a 5-year ERATO grant; humanoid robot research began in earnest
1997                   — Joined University of Southern California (USC) as faculty (appointed simultaneously with Maja Mataric)
Postdoc/early USC      — Adjunct Assistant Professor at Georgia Tech
Postdoc/early USC      — Adjunct Assistant Professor in the Penn State University Kinesiology department (behavioral experiment collaborations)
1998                   — Developed one of the world's earliest humanoids through the ATR ERATO project
Mid-2000s              — With Auke Eisberg (original transliteration) of EPFL, developed motor primitives-based imitation learning approaches
Around 2005            — Co-founded the Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS) conference
Around 2009            — Concluded the DARPA Little Dog project on machine learning for quadruped locomotion on rough terrain
At interview           — Operated multiple platforms including Sarcos hydraulic humanoid, PR2 (Willow Garage), NAO, Barrett arm, and HUBO
At interview           — Collaborated with Gerald Loeb (USC BME) on human skin-like tactile sensors
At interview           — Joint grasping/manipulation projects with Gaurav Sukhatme
At interview           — Founding director of the Max-Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems (Tuebingen); Invited Researcher at the ATR Computational Neuroscience Lab
November 12, 2010      — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro in Los Angeles
```
