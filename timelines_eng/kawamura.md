# Sadao Kawamura — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kawamura.md`)

```
Birth           — Born in Osaka Prefecture, Japan; grew up in Osaka
School years    — Equally interested in robots and animals/fish/insects; practiced Aikido from high school
Undergrad years — Entered the Department of Biophysical Engineering, Osaka University (chosen because both artificial and biological systems could be studied)
Undergrad years — Built a fish robot and measured its locomotion efficiency (in collaboration with graduate students)
1981            — B.S. in Biophysical Engineering, Osaka University
Graduate school — Switched majors to mechanical engineering (to focus on building artificial systems)
Graduate years  — Joined the biped walking robot project of Associate Prof. Miyazaki under Prof. Imoto's supervision
                 — Engaged in seminars on neurophysics and mathematics with Dr. Kawato (ATR) and others
1983            — M.S. in Mechanical Engineering, Osaka University
PhD studies     — Developed iterative learning control for biped walking robots
1986            — PhD in Mechanical Engineering, Osaka University
1986–1987       — Assistant Professor, Osaka University (1 year)
1987            — Appointed Associate Professor in the Department of Mechanical Engineering, Ritsumeikan University; established his own lab
Early Ritsumeikan — Researched time-scale transformation techniques; succeeded with the nonlinear case after a year
                 — Developed a tendon-ribbon robot (43G acceleration with a 60W motor)
                 — Developed a lightweight robot for painting large ships, commissioned by Yamaha
1994            — Preparations for the Ritsumeikan Robotics and Flexible Automation Research Center, funded by Japanese companies (Daifuku, Ishida, Okura, etc.)
1995            — Robotics and Flexible Automation Research Center officially launched
1996            — Led the establishment of the Department of Robotics at Ritsumeikan University (the world's first undergraduate robotics department)
                 — Spent two years preparing for Ministry of Education approval (negotiating issues such as the use of katakana for "robot")
                 — Recruited 12–13 faculty members including Arimoto, Hanafsa, and Hirai
1996–           — Full Professor, Department of Robotics, Ritsumeikan University
1996–2001       — JSPS Human Support Robotics project (control of pneumatic wearable systems)
Around 1999–2000 — Began joint research on underwater robots with Sakagami (Tokai University), a former student (experiments at Lake Biwa)
Late 2000s      — Developed new underwater robots sponsored by Dai Nippon Screen (drum-and-bubble flotation method)
2011            — Director, Ritsumeikan University Robotics Research Center
2011–2013       — President, Robotics Society of Japan (RSJ)
At time of interview — Serving as RSJ President; focused on establishing system integration as a new scientific discipline
```
