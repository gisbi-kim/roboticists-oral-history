# Victor Scheinman — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/scheinman.md`)

```
December 28, 1942      — Born in Augusta, Georgia, USA (father was a U.S. Army medical officer)
After WWII             — Family moved to Brooklyn, New York
Around age 8-9         — First encountered robots in the film "The Day the Earth Stood Still"; built a wooden robot model at father's encouragement
5th grade              — Began dreaming of attending MIT, influenced by a friend
Middle school years    — Family moved to the Bronx; attended a private high school
Around age 13          — Earned ham radio license
1959                   — Entered MIT at age 16, majoring in Aero/Astro
Undergrad years        — Internship at Sikorsky Aircraft (helicopter division)
1963                   — Earned B.S. in Aeronautics & Astronautics from MIT; received Louie de Flores Award
1963                   — Brief stint at Boeing (Mahogany Row), followed by world travel (Tahiti, NZ, Australia, Philippines, Thailand, etc.)
Around 1964            — Received draft notice while in India; returned to the U.S. and entered Stanford graduate school (transferred from Aero/Astro to ME)
Grad summer            — Two stints on the Apollo program (heat shield, Saturn turbopump)
Stanford M.S.          — Earned M.S. in Mechanical Engineering in just one year
Post-Master's          — Began Engineer's degree, joined the AI Lab, collaborated with Bernie Roth
AI Lab period          — Maintained the Rancho Arm and worked on its computer interface
AI Lab period          — Worked with Larry Leifer on the ORM (snake-like pneumatic arm) project (Don Pieper's PhD thesis)
AI Lab period          — Developed a hydraulic arm (PDP-6/PDP-10, space-war mode)
Around 1966~1968       — Industrial site tours including Unimation and GM via a fellowship sponsored by George Devol/Joe Engelberger
Around 1969            — Earned Engineer's degree (Stanford Arm design as thesis topic)
1969~1970              — Worked at Raychem (Menlo Park) as automation engineer
Around 1970            — Returned to Stanford; built the Stanford Arm (gold/blue), working with John McCarthy/Les Earnest
Early 1970s            — Collaborated with SRI (Charlie Rosen, David Nitzen) to build a Stanford Arm for JPL
1972                   — At Marvin Minsky's invitation, spent the summer at MIT designing the small MIT Arm (later PUMA 260)
1973                   — Founded Vicarm Inc. (Mountain View)
Vicarm period          — Hired Bruce Shimano (software, VAL) and Brian Carlisle (manufacturing)
Vicarm period          — Delivered arms to GM, NBS, AT&T, Naval Research Lab, Boston Univ., and others
Early-mid 1970s        — First public Vicarm exhibition via Engelberger's booth at Robots-1 (Chicago)
1977                   — Sold Vicarm to Unimation; joined as head of Unimation's western division
Around 1977~1979       — Developed PUMA (Programmable Universal Machine for Assembly) in cooperation with GM
Unimation period       — PUMA mass production proceeded at three sites: UK, U.S., and Kawasaki (license)
Around 1980            — Co-founded Automatix with Phil Villers (venture funding, Massachusetts headquarters)
Automatix period       — Three product lines: welding, assembly, vision; licensed SRI vision algorithms
Automatix period       — Recruited Don Pieper (VP Engineering), Gordon VanderBrug, and others
Automatix period       — Developed the Robot World system; delivered to GM and HP (inkjet cartridges) among others
Automatix period       — Sold Robot World and the assembly/welding lines to Yaskawa
Yaskawa period         — Continued as consultant developing Robot World further (about 700-800 units sold)
Late 1990s             — Joined Stanford as Consulting Professor, active for about 10-15 years
Brian/Bruce later      — Brian Carlisle and Bruce Shimano spun off and founded Adept Technology
Just before interview  — Retired; mentoring high school robotics groups, sponsoring UC Davis student projects, investing in Precise Automation
November 19, 2010      — ETHW oral history interview with Peter Asaro & Selma Šabanović in Woodside, CA
```
