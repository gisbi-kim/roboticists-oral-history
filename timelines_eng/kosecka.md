# Jana Kosecka — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/kosecka.md`)

```
Birth                 — Born in Slovakia (then Czechoslovakia)
Undergrad/MS          — BS and MS in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at Slovak Technical University (Bratislava); MS thesis on AI topics including expert systems for agricultural diagnosis
After MS              — Worked as researcher at her alma mater; became interested in robotics after attending Ruzena Bajcsy's robotics talk at a conference
PhD entry             — Joined University of Pennsylvania GRASP Lab for doctoral studies under Bajcsy
PhD years             — Researched hybrid system models and verification frameworks for autonomous mobile agents (during the era when Rodney Brooks' behavior-based robotics was emerging)
1996                  — Earned Ph.D. (UPenn)
1996                  — Moved to UC Berkeley as postdoctoral researcher; joined autonomous driving project with Jitendra Malik and Shankar Sastry (lane-change and headway-keeping demonstrations on Honda vehicles)
Late postdoc          — 3D vision research with Y. Ma, S. Soatto, and S. Sastry; co-authored textbook "An Invitation to 3-D Vision: From Images to Geometric Models"
~3-year postdoc       — Approximately three years of postdoctoral work at UC Berkeley
After postdoc         — Appointed faculty in Computer Science at George Mason University; research on localization, mapping, and structure recognition in manmade environments
Early faculty years   — Received NSF CAREER Award and David Marr Prize, among others
After tenure          — Visiting professor at Google working on early Street View prototype (one year)
Later                 — At Nokia Research Lab, researched pedestrian navigation using Navteq map data
Later                 — Visiting professor at Stanford, teaching and advising postdoctoral researchers
Recent                — Semantic segmentation research with postdocs including Cesar Cadena; received funding from ARO, IARPA, NGA, and others
Ongoing               — Served as chair of IEEE RAS Computer Vision and Robot Perception Technical Committee
2015                  — ETHW oral history interview (College Station, Texas)
```
