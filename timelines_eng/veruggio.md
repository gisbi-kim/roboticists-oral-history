# Gianmarco Veruggio — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/veruggio.md`)

```
1953              — Born in Sanremo, Italy (near the French Côte d'Azur, a flower-growing region)
Childhood          — Attended the Classical High School in Sanremo
Childhood          — Influenced daily by passing in front of Alfred Nobel's villa (now a museum) on the way to school
1978~1979         — Master's thesis at the University of Genoa on a computer-generated ship-handling simulator
                    (built with a custom graphics package and Sutherland algorithms on an HP21-MX minicomputer)
Same period        — Developed video game software at night; sold roughly 1,000 units of "Fool Race"
                    (a reverse-driving Pac-Man variant) through a small Sanremo game company
1980              — Earned an Electrical Engineering degree from the University of Genoa
After graduation   — Joined the Naval Automation Institute (Genoa); spent about 10 years researching
                    naval/marine automation and radar/oceanographic data acquisition systems
1989              — Saw a small ROV (Phillip Bot) by chance and decided to enter underwater robotics
Early days         — Reused the marine section of a broken CNL-owned robot, redesigned the electronics, succeeded in first experiment
1991              — Launched a more systematic underwater robotics project (running CNR-IAN Robotlab)
1993~1994         — First Antarctic mission, deploying the ROBY 2 robot
1996              — Started the Romeo project (aimed at an open, networked architecture)
1997~1998         — First Antarctic mission with Romeo (under-ice biological sampling, seabed monitoring,
                    and trials of the underwater laser instrument ENA)
1998              — Began public outreach for robotics and science, publishing books and giving school lectures
1998~1999         — Started research on internet robotics
1999              — Successfully teleoperated Romeo over the internet; international collaboration with Lisbon (Antonio Pascoal's team)
                    (autonomously controlling a robot in an Italian pool from Portugal)
2001              — Returned to Antarctica with Romeo on the E-Robot project
                    Internet connection via satellite link allowed the general public to control the robot in Antarctica via browser
                    Successful Antarctic Benthic Shuttle (ABS) mission
                    Mounted an Australian school's homemade underwater camera on Romeo for live filming in Antarctica
2003              — Activity scaled back as business/projects were suspended
2004              — Hosted the First International Symposium on Roboethics at Villa Nobel, Sanremo
                    Coined the term "Roboethics"; with Paolo Dario, Kazuo Tanie, Ron Arkin and others,
                    launched the IEEE Robot Ethics Technical Committee
2005              — Hosted a marine robotics conference in Genoa, inviting major international keynotes
Later activities   — Founded the Scuola di Robotica (School of Robotics) association, continuing
                    education and robot-ethics outreach for schools
2009              — Awarded Italy's Order of Merit (Commander)
2010-11-09        — Interviewed by Peter Asaro in Liguria, Italy
```
