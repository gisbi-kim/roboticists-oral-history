# Yoshiaki Shirai — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/shirai.md`)

```
August 3, 1941        — Born in Toyota City, Japan (father Takeaki Shirai worked at Toyota → Denso)
Childhood             — Grew up near Nagoya
High school           — Graduated from Tokai High School in Nagoya
Around 1960           — Entered Mechanical Engineering at Nagoya University
1964                  — Earned B.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering from Nagoya University
1964                  — Entered graduate school at the University of Tokyo, joining Masahiro Mori's lab (witnessed first demo of a 3-finger pneumatic robot)
Master's period       — Visited Ichiro Kato (Waseda); after seeing his tactile sensor, attempted his own improved version (rubber/carbon powder)
Master's period       — Recognized the limits of finger sensors and shifted research direction toward vision
Around 1964           — Discovered the Perceptron book; about a year of attempted application before recognizing its limits
1966                  — Earned M.Eng. from the University of Tokyo
PhD period            — Operations Research (forestry), and X-ray weld defect analysis research
1969                  — Earned Dr.Eng. from the University of Tokyo; published in Pattern Recognition Vol.1
1969                  — Right after PhD, joined Electrotechnical Laboratory (ETL) under MITI, robot vision group
Early ETL             — Studied under Saburo Tsuji; developed blocks-world line drawing/4-direction lighting techniques
Early ETL             — Developed a rangefinder (light projection + scanning method)
1971                  — Sent two papers (lighting/range finder) to Marvin Minsky and applied to visit MIT
1971~1972             — Spent one year at MIT AI Lab; improved line-finder using image dissector camera; befriended Minsky/Winston
1972                  — Returned to ETL with the launch of Japan's Pattern Information Processing Project; in charge of 3D portion
1970s                 — Just before the Tsukuba move, conducted long-term recognition of everyday desktop objects (cups, telephones, etc.) for about 5 years
Late 1970s            — With Oshima, researched automotive parts recognition (won the Takayanagi Award)
1970s                 — Continued the X-ray automatic screening research above (in cooperation with University of Tokyo physicians); CT brain imaging collaborations
1979                  — ETL relocated from Tokyo to Tsukuba; Shirai promoted to Computer Vision Section Chief
After 1979            — Advised Katsushi Ikeuchi (later MIT/CMU), supporting his photometric stereo research
After 1979            — Advised juniors including Sugihara (line drawing from rangefinder)
Early 1980s           — Participated in the Advanced Robot for Hazardous Environments project (Hitachi walking, Mitsubishi teleoperation, Toshiba vision)
1985                  — Promoted to Director of ETL Control Division (including the manipulator section, about 40 people + outside researchers)
1985                  — Direct-drive manipulator demo accident during Mitterrand/Thatcher visit at Tsukuba Expo
Around 1985~1989      — Board member of the Japan Society for AI
1987~1989             — Board member of the Information Processing Society of Japan
1988                  — Appointed full professor at Osaka University, Mechanical Engineering (Electrical Control)
Osaka period          — Collaborated with Minoru Asada, Yoshinori Kuno, and others
Osaka period          — Began research on outdoor scene understanding, stereo vision, and human tracking
Osaka period          — Developed mobile manipulator robots (for the disabled/elderly — fetching drinks from refrigerators)
Osaka period          — Conducted soccer game video analysis and hand gesture/posture recognition research
Around 2005           — Moved to Ritsumeikan University (in his 6th year at the time of interview)
Ritsumeikan period    — Golf swing analysis (indoor 2-camera → outdoor 1-camera), 3D hand posture recognition research
August 30, 2011       — ETHW oral history interview with Selma Šabanović and Matthew Francisco in Flagstaff, AZ
```
