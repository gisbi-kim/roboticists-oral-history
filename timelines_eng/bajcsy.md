# Ruzena Bajcsy — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/bajcsy.md`)

```
1933             — Born in Bratislava, Czechoslovakia (the year Hitler came to power)
Around 1936      — Birth mother died (Bajcsy was 3)
1938             — Father remarried; family converted to Catholicism
1938–1939        — Moved to Zvolen in central Slovakia
August 1944      — Slovak National Uprising; the Nazis invaded to suppress it
November–December 1944 — Father and stepmother were murdered by the Nazis; 11-year-old Bajcsy and her sister survived by chance, being in another room
1944             — Registered as a war orphan by the Red Cross; lived in shelters
March 1945       — Liberated when Soviet troops arrived; all relatives dead except an aunt in Budapest
1946             — Moved from Zvolen to Bratislava
1947             — Entered a Catholic orphanage; at age 14 was assigned to a city-appointed guardian
1948             — Stalin's border closure permanently separated her from her sister
1952             — To avoid forced Marxism by the Czechoslovak government, entered Slovak Technical University, Department of Electrical Engineering
1956             — Branded "enemy of the state" for defending Comenius traditions at a student gathering
1957             — Earned MS in Electrical Engineering from Slovak Technical University (would become Slovakia's first female PhD candidate in EE)
1957             — Daughter born; forced placement as an engineer at an electronics factory for 5 years
Around 1962      — Slovak Technical University introduced a Soviet-made Ural II computer; she joined as a maintenance engineer (forbidden from teaching)
1964             — Son born
1967             — Earned PhD in Electrical Engineering from Slovak Technical University (learning machines, Markov processes); first female EE PhD in Slovakia
October 1967     — Traveled to the U.S. to enroll at Stanford (invited by John McCarthy); left her two children behind
1968             — Soviet invasion of Prague made return impossible; remained at Stanford
1972             — Earned a second PhD in Computer Science from Stanford (texture recognition)
1972             — Joined the University of Pennsylvania as assistant professor (the department's first woman)
1978             — Founded the GRASP Lab (General Robotics and Active Sensory Perception) shortly after earning tenure
Penn era         — Developed a digital brain anatomy atlas; MRI/PET matching software
Penn era         — Active perception paradigm research: auto-focus/zoom systems, vision-tactile integration, cooperative robots
1990             — Daughter immigrated to the U.S.
1991–1992        — Son immigrated to the U.S.
1998–2001        — Head of NSF CISE (Computer and Information Science and Engineering Directorate)
After 2001       — Returned to Penn and considered retirement
2002             — Appointed CITRIS Director at UC Berkeley (succeeding Randy Katz)
2002             — At time of interview: Professor of EECS and CITRIS Director at UC Berkeley
2009             — Awarded the Benjamin Franklin Medal in Computer and Cognitive Science for recent work (after the interview, noted in bio)
```
