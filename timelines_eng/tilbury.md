# Dawn Tilbury — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/tilbury.md`)

```
1966              — Born in Minnesota, USA; father a Honeywell manufacturing engineer (electrical engineer)
Childhood~high school — Attended public schools; loved French and physics in high school
Undergrad years   — Electrical engineering at the University of Minnesota
1989              — Entered UC Berkeley electrical engineering PhD program
1st PhD semester  — Took Shankar Sastry's robotics class, entered the controls field
After 2nd PhD semester — Researched motion planning algorithms for nonholonomic systems (wheeled mobile robots) (collaborations with Richard Murray, Greg Walsh, Linda Bushnell)
1994              — Earned PhD at UC Berkeley
1994              — Appointed Assistant Professor in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Michigan (recommendation by Harris McClamroch)
After mid-1990s   — Joined Engineering Research Center for Reconfigurable Manufacturing Systems (Yoram Koren as director, collaborations with Pramod Khargonekar et al.)
Center activities — Collaborated with industrial partners such as Ford, GM, Rockwell Automation, Siemens
Late center period — Developed anomaly detection algorithms (network traffic-based)
2006~2007         — Began collaboration with U.S. Army Tank Automotive Command (Warren, MI), returned to robotics
After             — Researched reliability/user interfaces/energy/temperature control with PackBot, SuperDroid, etc.
Around 2014       — Served as General Chair of the American Control Conference
2014              — Launched University of Michigan PhD robotics program (4 of 8 students were women)
At time of interview — Began researching human-robot affective interaction with new student (collaboration with Emily Mower Provost)
2015. 1. 22~23.   — Interview with Selma Šabanović conducted in College Station, Texas (during ICRA all-women organizing committee activity)
At time of interview — 20 years at Michigan, many PhD graduates went into industry/government
```
