# Raja Chatila — Career Timeline

> Source: ETHW Oral History Interview (`ethw_source_eng/chatila.md`)

```
January 29, 1952      — Born in Damascus, Syria
Childhood–high school — Attended a French-Arabic school in Damascus; at 15–16, was offered a French government scholarship on his math teacher's recommendation
Around 1969 (age 17)  — Left Syria to study in France (higher math at the St. Louis preparatory school)
Afterwards            — Earned an electronics engineering degree at the National School of Civil Aviation in Toulouse
Afterwards            — MS in Control Sciences from the École de l'Aéronautique; MS in Geophysics and Space Sciences from Paul Sabatier University
1977                  — Began PhD at LAAS under George Giralt (switched after his original astrophysics advisor passed away) — HILARE project
1981                  — PhD in Control Sciences from Paul Sabatier University (regarded as France's first PhD on mobile robots); thesis "Robot Navigation Space Modeling and Decisional Processes"
1982–83               — Postdoc at Stanford AI Lab under Tom Binford; set up the Brian Carlisle/Unimation omnidirectional robot
During postdoc        — Visited Boston/MIT, met Tomás Lozano-Pérez and Rodney Brooks; interacted with Nils Nilsson at SRI
1983                  — Returned to France; joined LAAS as a CNRS researcher
1984                  — Began serious work on uncertainty in mobile robots (the foundation for SLAM)
1985                  — Presented an early SLAM paper with Jean-Paul Laumond at ICRA (St. Louis); first met Hugh Durrant-Whyte
1986–~89              — EU project SKIDS (multi-sensor fusion with Mike Brady and Hugh Durrant-Whyte)
1989                  — French space agency CNES Mars exploration national project began (George Giralt as coordinator)
1992–93               — Eden project began; the Russian robot Adam, purchased by Matra, was used
Around 1993–95        — Developed natural-terrain navigation and perception algorithms; readied for Mars exploration
1994–95               — French government decided to halt Mars exploration (ESA focused on Earth observation)
Afterwards            — Used another Russian robot, the Marsokhod model (Lama, Eve), for experiments
2002                  — Research on sensorimotor representation learning with William Paquier and Nicolas Do Huu
Around 2003–05        — COMETS project (multi-aerial robot wildfire detection with Spain, Portugal, and Germany)
2004–08               — Coordinator of the Cogniron project (cognitive robot companions, with Henrik Christensen, Kerstin Dautenhahn, Rüdiger Dillmann, and others)
August 31, 2011       — IEEE oral history interview in San Francisco (then Professor at University Pierre and Marie Curie, IEEE RAS President-Elect)
```
